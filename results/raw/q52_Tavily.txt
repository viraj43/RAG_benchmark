# How Apple Makes Money: iPhones, Services, and More

iPhones are the company’s biggest source of revenue, but its services business is growing fast

By
[Matthew Johnston](https://www.investopedia.com/contributors/53788/)

Updated January 31, 2026

Reviewed by
[Charles Potters](https://www.investopedia.com/charles-potters-4942512)

### Key Takeaways

* Apple’s iPhone sales generated slightly more than half of its total revenue in FY 2025.
* The company’s services business boasts higher margins than its product sales.
* In FY 2025, Apple’s services revenue increased by 14%.
* Apple’s Mac net sales saw a 12% increase in FY 2025.
* Antitrust investigations and unionization efforts present ongoing challenges for Apple.

Apple Inc. ([AAPL](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=AAPL)) is a global technology company that designs, manufactures, and sells smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories. Its major products include the iPhone, its largest revenue generator, plus the Mac line of personal computers, the iPad, Apple Watch, and Apple TV. The company also has a fast-growing services business that includes its iCloud service, digital content streaming services such as Apple Music and Apple TV, and its [artificial intelligence (AI)](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/a/artificial-intelligence-ai.asp) functionality, Apple Intelligence.12

## An In-Depth Look at Apple’s Financial Performance

Apple posted a net income of $112.01 billion on revenue of $416.16 billion for its 2025 [fiscal year (FY)](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/f/fiscalyear.asp), ended Sept. 27, 2025.3 Apple refers to revenue as [net sales](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/n/netsales.asp) in its financial reports. Both [net income](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/n/netincome.asp) and [revenue](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/r/revenue.asp) grew compared to the prior fiscal year. Net income climbed about 16% from 2024’s $93.74 billion, as revenue rose 6% from 2024’s $391.03 billion.4

Revenue from Apple’s major product categories increased in FY 2025, with net sales for the Mac leading the way at 12%. The only decline was for wearables, home, and accessories, which fell 4%. Services were up 14%.5

Apple breaks down its business by geographical segment, those being the Americas, Europe, Greater China, Japan, and Rest of Asia Pacific. Net sales were up in every region except Greater China, where they were down slightly.6

## iPhone Sales: The Backbone of Apple’s Revenue

Apple’s iPhone, the company’s [smartphone](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/smartphone.asp), is its top-selling product category by revenue. For FY 2025, iPhone net sales were $209.59 billion, or 50.4% of total revenue.5 That represented a 4% increase from 2024.

Current [iPhone](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/investing/022316/economics-iphone-aapl.asp) models include the iPhone 17 Pro, iPhone Air, iPhone 17, iPhone 16, and iPhone 16e.7

## Understanding Mac Sales in Apple’s Portfolio

The company’s venerable Mac category includes personal computers such as the MacBook Air and MacBook Pro laptops and the iMac, Mac mini, Mac Studio, and Mac Pro desktop computers.7

Mac sales generated $33.71 billion in revenue for FY 2025, 8.1% of Apple’s total revenue for the year. That represented an increase of 12% from the year before.5

## iPad: Revenue Trends and Market Position

Apple’s iPad products include a range of multipurpose tablets: the iPad, iPad Pro, iPad Air, and iPad mini.7

In terms of revenue, they constitute Apple’s smallest major product category, accounting for $28 billion in FY 2025, a roughly 5% increase from 2024.5

## Shrinking Growth of Wearables, Home and Accessories

Apple groups several of its other products together under the Wearables, Home and Accessories category. This portion of the company’s business includes its AirPods wireless headphone line, the Apple TV media streaming and gaming device, the Apple Watch line of smartwatches, Apple Vision Pro mixed-reality headset, and HomePod and Beats products for the home, among others.7

Apple’s Wearables, Home and Accessories products accounted for $35.69 billion in revenue in FY 2025, or 8.6% of the company’s overall revenue for the year. This was a decline of 4% from 2024 and was the only product group in the negative.5

### Exploring the Growth of Apple’s Service Sector

Apple has mounted a major corporate strategy to reduce its dependence on lower-margin hardware products, which face slowing growth, while accelerating the growth of its services business, which has higher margins and a more predictable, recurring revenue stream. The[gross margin](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/g/grossmargin.asp) in Apple’s services business for FY 2025 was 75.4%, compared with 36.8% for its products business.8

While Apple combines its services businesses into a single group for financial reporting purposes, they fall into five categories: advertising, AppleCare (technical support and services for Apple products), cloud services, digital content (including Apple Arcade, Apple Fitness+, Apple Music, Apple News+, and Apple TV), and payment services (including its co-branded credit card, [Apple Card](https://www.investopedia.com/apple-s-credit-card-is-switching-banks-here-s-what-that-means-for-your-money-aapl-jpm-11881423), and its [Apple Pay](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/a/apple-pay.asp) cashless payment service).9

Collectively, the group had net sales of $109.16 billion for FY 2025, up 14% for the year.5

## Current Challenges Facing Apple: Antitrust and Labor Issues

Apple faces numerous competitors, including smartphone manufacturers Samsung Electronics Co. Ltd. ([SSNLF](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=SSNLF)) and LG Electronics Inc. ([066570](https://www.tradingview.com/chart/?symbol=066570)), computer manufacturers Lenovo Group Ltd. ([LNVGY](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=LNVGY)) and Dell Technologies Inc. ([DELL](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=DELL)), streaming-content providers Spotify Technology S.A. ([SPOT](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=SPOT)) and Netflix Inc. ([NFLX](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=NFLX)), and other technology companies like Microsoft Corp. ([MSFT](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=MSFT)), Alphabet Inc. ([GOOGL](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=GOOGL)), and Amazon.com Inc. ([AMZN](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=AMZN)).

Even so, Apple’s dominance in certain business segments has led to [antitrust concerns](https://www.investopedia.com/apple-anti-trust-cases-what-they-mean-for-you-11730298) and investigations in the United States and European Union. In March 2024, the U.S. Justice Department and 16 state and district attorneys general filed an antitrust lawsuit against Apple “for monopolization or attempted monopolization of smartphone markets in violation of Section 2 of the [Sherman Act](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/sherman-antiturst-act.asp).”10

In January 2026, Apple faced a new antitrust lawsuit from Reincubate Ltd. The litigation claims that Apple stole technology from Reincubate’s Camo app and has an illegal [monopoly](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/m/monopoly.asp) in the U.S. smartphone software market.11

In addition, Apple’s labor practices and those of its suppliers remain under fire, both in the U.S. and overseas.1213

Meanwhile, some members of Apple’s largely non-unionized workforce have been attempting to [organize](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/o/organizedlabor.asp). Apple has had recent legal success in discouraging the [unionization](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/l/labor-union.asp) efforts:

* In July 2025, the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit reversed a 2024 National Labor Relations Board decision that Apple had violated labor laws at one of its New York City stores by questioning a worker about his support for a union and removing union flyers from the store.14
* In January 2026, Apple won a ruling from the United Kingdom’s Central Arbitration Committee to stop unionization methods at an Apple store in Cambridge.15

## What Are Apple’s Biggest Sources of Revenue?

Apple’s iPhone sales deliver the largest portion of its revenue. iPhone net sales accounted for slightly more than half of the company’s total revenue for fiscal year (FY) 2025. Next after iPhone sales is Apple’s services business.5

## What Is the Fastest-Growing Part of Apple’s Business?

Based on [year-over-year (YOY)](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/y/year-over-year.asp) revenue growth for FY 2025, the fastest-growing part of Apple’s business is its services category.5

## Which Apple Product Generates the Least Revenue?

For FY 2025, Apple’s iPad sales generated about 7% of total revenue, the smallest portion of any of the company’s major product lines.5

## The Bottom Line

Apple is [one of the largest](https://www.investopedia.com/biggest-companies-in-the-world-by-market-cap-5212784) and most ubiquitous companies in the world. The company’s iPhone sales currently dominate its other products, such as the Mac, the iPad, and more, and its various services are growing revenue at a fast pace. But the [company faces challenges](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/investing/072715/apples-key-weaknesses.asp) to its dominance, including antitrust issues and unionization efforts and mounting competition from a variety of rivals.

# Apple Statistics (2026)

[David Curry](https://www.businessofapps.com/news/author/david-curry/)

Updated: February 26, 2026

Apple is one of the most influential and recognisable brands in the world, responsible for the rise of the smartphone with the iPhone. Valued at over $2 trillion in 2021, it is also the most valuable technology company in the world.

Originally known for desktop computers, Apple was one of the first companies to switch to the graphical user interface (GUI) and saw success with the first Macintosh. Steve Jobs, the founder and CEO, was one of technology’s first “rockstars”, able to sell hardware as more than a tool for work.

Jobs resigned from Apple in 1985 and the company saw a gradual decline over the 1990s, as it struggled to compete with Microsoft on software and Windows manufacturers on hardware sales.

In a last ditch effort to save the company, Apple acquired Jobs’ company NeXT and installed him as advisor and then CEO. He turned the company’s fortunes around, launching iTunes and iPod in 2001.

The iPhone saw Apple ascend to one of the most valuable companies in the world, with revenue increasing from $37.4 billion in 2008 to $65 billion in 2010.

The success of the iPhone enabled Apple to launch new product categories, such as the iPad. Apple envisioned the tablet as a bridge between the smartphone and laptop, with the capabilities to be both.

As iPhone revenues have stagnated, Apple has looked for new ways to generate revenue from the iPhone. It launched Apple Watch in 2015, as an accessory to the iPhone which tracks various health and fitness metrics. AirPods were next, which paved the way for wireless headphones to become mainstream.

It has also built out its subscription services to include music and video streaming, video games, fitness and cloud storage. This segment alone generated $53 billion revenue in 2020, making it Apple’s second largest segment.

All eyes are on Apple’s next products, which may include a virtual reality headset and self-driving car. The past few product launches have been smaller in scope, like the HomePod and AirPod, and Apple fans are clamouring for the next iPhone.

**Note: Apple’s financial year ends in September.**

## Apple Key Statistics

* Apple generated $416 billion revenue in 2025, an increase of 6.4% on the previous year
* By region, Americas generated the most revenue for Apple, followed by Europe
* iPhone sales were responsible for 50% of Apple’s total revenue in 2025
* Apple had $112 billion net income in 2025, a new record for the company
* iPhone revenue reached $209 billion in 2025, an increase of 4.1% on the year prior
* Apple sold an estimated 247 million iPhones in 2025
* There are over 1.5 billion active iPhone units worldwide
* iPad revenue was $28 billion in 2025, an increase on the previous year but still below its peak in 2013
* Apple’s Wearable, Home and Accessories segment generated $35 billion revenue in 2025
* Apple sold about 33 million Watches, 80 million AirPods, and 6.2 million HomePods in 2025
* Mac revenue was $33 billion in 2025, an increase of 12.7% on the previous year
* Apple made over $100 billion from its Services segment in 2025
* Over 1.2 billion people subscribe to apps on iOS
* Apple Pay had over $9 trillion transaction volume in 2025

# Apple’s iPhone revenue jumps to $57 billion despite chip shortages

iPhone sales keep going up, and the debut of the MacBook Neo didn’t hurt either.

iPhone sales keep going up, and the debut of the MacBook Neo didn’t hurt either.

by Emma Roth

Nilay Patel

Emma Roth
is a news writer who covers the streaming wars, consumer tech, crypto, social media, and much more. Previously, she was a writer and editor at MUO.

Apple’s iPhone revenue jumped 22 percent to $57 billion over the past few months, despite supply chain issues impacting the device’s processor. In [an interview with *Reuters*](https://www.reuters.com/business/apple-sales-beat-expectations-powered-by-mac-iphone-hits-supply-constraints-2026-04-30/), Apple CEO Tim Cook said iPhone “demand was off the charts,” but there’s “a little less flexibility at the moment for getting more parts.” Cook added during an earnings call that supply constraints could come for Mac, too, “given the continued high levels of demand that we’re seeing.”

Apple earned $111.2 billion in revenue during the second quarter of 2026, its “best March quarter ever,” according to Cook. This latest earnings report comes a little over a week after the company announced CEO [Cook’s retirement in September](/tech/915213/tim-cook-apple-ceo-stepping-down-john-ternus). John Ternus, the company’s head of hardware engineering, will take Cook’s place as he transitions into the role of executive chairman.

Even with Cook’s departure just months away, he’ll still have to lead Apple through the 2026 Worldwide Developers’ Conference (WWDC) in June, where Apple’s expected to take the wraps off a bunch of new features, [including a more personalized Siri](/tech/877494/apple-siri-ai-overhaul-ios-personalized) powered [by Google’s Gemini AI model](/news/860521/apple-siri-google-gemini-ai-personalization).

Apple had an announcement-filled March, revealing a [budget-friendly MacBook Neo](/tech/904705/apple-macbook-neo-news-reviews-mods) that [caused a stir in the laptop space](/tech/908328/macbook-neo-windows-laptop-competitors-asus-lenovo-acer-review-comparison) for its speedy performance and aluminium chassis. The device starts at $599, featuring a 13-inch display, an A18 Pro iPhone chip, and 8GB of RAM. Apple also announced [a new iPhone 17E](/tech/891164/apple-iphone-17e-review), an [M4-equipped iPad Air](/tech/886433/apple-ipad-air-2026-launch-price-specs-availability), along with [new MacBook Air and MacBook Pro models](http://announcement).
