Whether you’re in the Financial District because you’ve had to trade in your all-day PJs for something business casual five days a week or you’re simply passing through on the way to [BART](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/restaurants-near-bart-stations-sf), you’ll probably need somewhere good to eat. While the FiDi may be better known for its many office buildings than memorable meals, there are some standouts that make heading to this area worthwhile, even if it’s your day off.

[French](/san-francisco/cuisines/french)

Perfect For:[Drinking Great Wine](/san-francisco/perfect-for/drinking-great-wine)[Date Nights](/san-francisco/perfect-for/date-night)[Impressing Out Of Towners](/san-francisco/perfect-for/impressing-out-of-towners)[Drinks & A Light Bite](/san-francisco/perfect-for/drinks-a-light-bite)

You might have to sidestep through a minefield of logoed backpacks on the floor at Verjus, but once you’ve safely made it to a seat under the gleaming red ceiling with a glass of wine in hand, memories of the day’s KPI talk will be quickly forgotten. This [French](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-french-restaurants-sf) wine bar is the sexiest place to drink in the area—and even if you’re not already around, it’s worth making your way here with a date to split some pté en croûte, and admire the mound of butter chilling in the kitchen.

[READ THE REVIEW](/san-francisco/reviews/verjus)

### How to get into Verjus

Reservations open a week in advance at midnight on SevenRooms, or you can try your luck at a walk-in at 4pm when they open. Verjus also serves lunch on Saturdays with plenty of same-day tables.

[## Cotogna](/san-francisco/reviews/cotogna)

[490 Pacific Ave, San Francisco, CA 94133](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cotogna+490 Pacific Ave, San Francisco, CA 94133)

$$$$

[Italian](/san-francisco/cuisines/italian)

[Jackson Square](/san-francisco/neighborhoods/jackson-square)

Perfect For:[Date Nights](/san-francisco/perfect-for/date-night)[Dinner With The Parents](/san-francisco/perfect-for/dinner-with-the-parents)[Special Occasions](/san-francisco/perfect-for/special-occasions)

In a world where working was simply an option, we’d spend all our free time having candlelit [date nights](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-date-night-restaurants-in-san-francisco) at Cotogna. The relaxed [Italian](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-italian-restaurants-san-francisco) spot in Jackson Square (from the Quince people) should be the subject of a masterclass in thin-crust [pizza](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-pizza-san-francisco) and decadent pastas. No visit is complete without the raviolo filled with a poppy-orange egg yolk and silky ricotta.

[READ THE REVIEW](/san-francisco/reviews/cotogna)

### How to get into Cotogna

Reservations are released 30 days in advance on SevenRooms. Or you can walk up and try to go for a seat at the bar. If you want to avoid the dinner rush, come during lunch when it’s less busy—they serve mostly the same menu.

[RESERVE A TABLE](https://www.sevenrooms.com/reservations/cotogna)

[## Quince](/san-francisco/reviews/quince-sf)

[470 Pacific Ave, San Francisco, CA 94133](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Quince+470 Pacific Ave, San Francisco, CA 94133)

$$$$

[American](/san-francisco/cuisines/american)

[Jackson Square](/san-francisco/neighborhoods/jackson-square)

Perfect For:[Special Occasions](/san-francisco/perfect-for/special-occasions)[Birthdays](/san-francisco/perfect-for/birthdays)

Right next door to Cotogna is their sophisticated fine dining older sibling, Quince. It’s the kind of place that will run you $390 apiece, so it’s not exactly a casual experience. So save a meal here for blowout promotion dinners, [milestone anniversaries](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-special-occasion-restaurants-sf), or basically any time someone else is paying. The menu changes more often than the Ferry Terminal departures board, but expect tweezered dishes with football-shaped turnips, asparagus served out of a glass pail, and caviar in a billion forms.

[## Perbacco](/san-francisco/reviews/perbacco)

[230 California St, San Francisco, CA 94111](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Perbacco+230 California St, San Francisco, CA 94111)

$$$$

[Italian](/san-francisco/cuisines/italian)

[Financial District](/san-francisco/neighborhoods/financial-district)

Perfect For:[Dinner With The Parents](/san-francisco/perfect-for/dinner-with-the-parents)[Casual Dinners](/san-francisco/perfect-for/casual-dinners)[Impressing Out Of Towners](/san-francisco/perfect-for/impressing-out-of-towners)[Corporate Cards](/san-francisco/perfect-for/corporate-cards)

Perbacco is one of the rare [old-school Italian](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/old-school-italian-restaurants-sf-from-oldest-to-newest) spots that isn’t in [North Beach](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/north-beach-restaurants-san-francisco). And it has a lot of the tell-tale signs of the genre: white tablecloths, tie-clad servers, and no music—but the food is anything but red sauce spaghetti and meatballs. Cal-Ital takes on pasta are why you’re here. Get the pillowy gnocchi topped with a tangy tomato sugo and the [Dungeness](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-restaurants-for-dungeness-crab) spaghetti that’s tart, spicy, and briny all at once. It’s a good choice when you have family visiting from out of town or for a small group dinner before hopping on BART or Muni to extend the night with drinks elsewhere.

To be reminded of what SF was like before the dot-com bubble, head to Tadich Grill. Reservations go fast, but bar seating is always poachable especially right at 5pm.

[## Crustacean](/san-francisco/reviews/crustacean-pine-street)

[195 Pine St, San Francisco, CA 94109](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Crustacean+195 Pine St, San Francisco, CA 94109)

$$$$

[Seafood](/san-francisco/cuisines/seafood)

[Financial District](/san-francisco/neighborhoods/financial-district)

Perfect For:[Special Occasions](/san-francisco/perfect-for/special-occasions)[Birthdays](/san-francisco/perfect-for/birthdays)[Dinner With The Parents](/san-francisco/perfect-for/dinner-with-the-parents)

After five years of renovations, the city’s glitziest place for a special occasion is now among downtown’s many offices. The Beverly Hills–esque dining room is all velvet banquettes, soaring ceilings, and a huge bar buzzing with people celebrating something important. Go classic with garlic noodles and roasted crab—the dishes that made its sister restaurant [Thang Long](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/reviews/thanh-long) famous—or lean into the more elevated menu items like the tuna cigars served tableside in a smoke-filled box. Crustacean is also open for lunch Tuesday through Thursday if you have a client to impress.

[200 Jackson St., San Francisco, CA 94111](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kokkari%20Estiatorio+200 Jackson St., San Francisco, CA 94111)

$$$$

[Greek](/san-francisco/cuisines/greek)

[Financial District](/san-francisco/neighborhoods/financial-district)

Last time we were at Kokkari, the server proudly told us that we were sitting at Willie Brown’s regular table. That’s the type of old-school, white-tablecloth energy you’ll get at this Greek restaurant. Water is served in goblets, the armchairs are lift-with-your-knees heavy, and there’s a [fireplace](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-restaurants-and-bars-with-fireplaces-sf) constantly crackling in the back. Order a lamb chop, and swirl around some Cretan wine. This is a meal every San Franciscan should experience at least once.

[307 Kearny St, San Francisco, CA 94108](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Muracci's%20Japanese%20Curry%20%26%20Grill+307 Kearny St, San Francisco, CA 94108)

Marucci’s is the move for downtown lunch-on-the-fly. It’s a takeout-only operation specializing in solid, uncomplicated [Japanese curry](https://www.theinfatuation.com/san-francisco/guides/best-japanese-curry-sf) and rice plates. The plan for success is to order the textbook chicken katsu curry. If you skipped lunch, Marucci’s is open until 7pm—a godsend, especially after a workday full of unnecessary sync-ups.

[433 Clay St, San Francisco, California 94111](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=The%20Third%20Floor+433 Clay St, San Francisco, California 94111)

$$$$

[American](/san-francisco/cuisines/american)

If you're hitting the office early, The Third Floor serves breakfast starting at 6:30am and has a great breakfast sandwich.

## Abacá

**Open for:** Weekday breakfast, weekend brunch, and dinner

**Price range:** $$

When Francis Ang [opened the doors](/2021/8/16/22627417/abaca-restaurant-open-california-filipino-cuisine-fishermans-wharf) to his Kimpton Alton Hotel restaurant in Fisherman’s Wharf, the city already knew it was the breath of fresh seabreeze air diners needed. In short order his [Pinoy heritage cooking](/2021/8/9/22617195/california-filipino-cuisine-abaca-restaurant-san-francisco) drew eaters to the otherwise-for-tourists neighborhood: sisig fried rice with poached egg and pickled onions, adobo-glazed yuba skin barbecue skewer, American wagyu beef pares with parsnip and soy beef jus. Ang was a pop-up phenom before rolling out the chic bar and restaurant in 2021. The vibe is fancy but accessible; this is not a suit-and-tie restaurant, though that wouldn’t be overdressing, either. Breakfast here is geared to tourists — though a $6 bibingka rice cake with caramelized brie and salted egg is awesome any day — whereas brunch is a San Francisco-wide weekend affair. Reservations are bookable [here](https://www.opentable.com/r/abaca-san-francisco?ref=1068).

**Best for:** A booming dining tour through the Philippines with a big group.

## Dalida

**Price range:** $$$

Any San Francisco naysayers can take a little jaunt into the Presidio and find their jaws somewhere on the floor, back in the grass in Tunnel Tops Park. ## Golden Boy Pizza

**Open for:** Lunch, dinner

**Price range:** $

San Francisco is absolutely swarming with [great pizzerias](/maps/best-pizza-san-francisco) whether you favor a floppy Neapolitan pie from [Tony’s](https://tonyspizzanapoletana.com/) or thick Detroit-style slabs from [Square Pie Guys](https://squarepieguys.com/). But Golden Boy remains an institution for good reason: It’s a San Francisco pastime to stand on the sidewalk under that glowing neon sign waiting for your pan or slice while soaking in all that is the North Beach neighborhood. There are classic options, including cheese, pepperoni, and sausage — but the pro move is to get the clam and garlic pie. It’s a zingy, briny, bready meal built on a doughy base with a sturdy bottom. Take your pizza to nearby Washington Square Park if the weather allows.

**If you drive:** North Beach can be a difficult neighborhood to park in, especially given neighboring apartment buildings and parklets which take up some of the area’s parking spaces. Give yourself extra time to locate that perfect spot or, even better, take public transportation to this corner and save yourself some frustration.

## Lunette

**Open for:** Lunch, dinner

**Price range:** $

Chef Nite Yun is one of the San Francisco Bay Area’s most singular cooks. There’s her love of black pepper, incorporating the spice “by the handful” as Cesar Hernandez writes for the [*San Francisco Chronicle*](https://www.sfchronicle.com/food/restaurants/article/lunette-cambodian-ferry-building-19604782.php). **Best for:** Rolling through on a weekday — preferably a sunny one to take advantage of the outdoor seating at the Ferry Building — to catch up with a friend over a mind-blowing testament to Cambodian cuisine.

## Prelude

**Open for:** Dinner

**Price range:** $$$

At Prelude, chef Celtin Hendrickson-Jones [takes inspiration from his childhood](/2024/8/7/24215497/prelude-san-francisco-jay-hotel-celtin-hendrickson-jones) spent cooking with his maternal grandmother and great-grandmother in Alabama. But rather than straight renditions of those dishes, he’s putting his spin on it, informed by his time at two-Michelin-starred Commis, Morimoto, and Niku Steakhouse. The result is a playful, but not stuffy, fine-dining take on Southern dishes, such as the smoked catfish dumplings, a spinoff on crayfish etouffee, or a boneless wing stuffed with dirty rice. The tasting menu is the best way to sample what Prelude has to offer, but there’s an a la carte option as well. **Must-try dish:** If you’re familiar with ambrosia salad, Prelude has a version that will have you rethinking your feelings toward that nostalgic dish in the best of ways.

**Must-try dish:** Most go for the chicken and sausage gumbo, and while you can’t go wrong with it, just know there’s a devoted following for the restaurant’s smoked turkey version.

# 14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You’ll Love

Looking for some unique restaurants in [San Francisco](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/road-trip-from-los-angeles-to-san-francisco/)? This city was always known for sourdough bread, clam chowder and of course, Ghiradelli chocolate.  I’ve put together some of the most [popular spots in San Fran](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/road-trip-from-los-angeles-to-san-francisco/) just for you.

These days, it is one of the hottest food destinations in the world, championing new trends and trialing new concepts. So, if you spend an entire weekend in San Francisco, you’re in for a treat.

There are fun restaurants in San Francisco, as well as cool restaurants in San Francisco with fine dining restaurant [options in the Bay Area](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/best-places-to-visit-in-bay-area/) as well as food trucks. You can expect dim sum, [ice cream](https://www.newinterestingfacts.com/interesting-facts-about-ice-cream/) and so many other exciting places that make for a unique experience from the Mission District to North Beach. There are also plenty of restaurants overlooking the water where you can catch a fantastic [San Francisco sunset](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/san-francisco-sunsets/) if it is a special occasion.

![image 9](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-9.png?resize=1000%2C668&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 1")

 I have decided to take a broad definition of the word restaurant and to include a mix of kitsch, Michelin stars and cutting edge – I do love a silly eating experience as much as a fancy one. So if you’re in the mood to find out about all the most unique restaurants in San Francisco, then keep reading to find out where you should be dining for your next foodie experience.

Here is my guide to the Unique Restaurants in San Francisco you must visit – from the classics to the latest.

## 17 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco

### **1. The Meatball Bar**

**[The Meatball Bar](http://meatballbar.com/)**formalizes meatball consumption – which means table service for a unique dining experience. It’s all about handmade meatball extravaganzas from lamb to chicken to vegetarian (eggplant and zucchini) and sauces from truffle fondue to Korean. The mouth watering experience will take you on a tasting journey to some of the [best places in Korea](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/cherry-blossoms-in-korea/).

![image 6](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-6.png?resize=468%2C700&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 3")

 The Meatball Bar has a very relaxed air. I went for happy hour. During this time there are beer/wine and meatball deals on offer. I ordered the three meatballs and wine option and was able to try three different meatballs – the Italian classic, turkey and lamb – plus some of their delicious tomato sauce.

This is one of the best value quirky restaurants San Francisco has on offer. The first Meatball Bar opened in the Financial District in May 2017 and a new branch opened in Hayes Valley in February 2018. If you’re on the look out for unique San Francisco restaurants, this should be high on your list of places to check out.

![image 12](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-12.png?resize=600%2C800&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 4")

⇒ **Planning a trip to Napa?** [**Read my review of the fantastic restaurant Solbar and the Solage Spa & Hotel**](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/solage-calistoga-spa/)

### **2. The Progress**

**[The Progress](https://theprogress-sf.com/)**was one of my favourite meals in San Francisco! This lovely relaxed restaurant and dining room is located on Fillmore Street and specializes in doing innovative things with local ingredients.

The menu at The Progress changes regularly and is designed to be shared – with lovely wines. The night I went we enjoyed everything from dumplings to filet mignon. This has to be one of the best places to eat in San Francisco. The interior of the restaurant is also exceptionally beautiful and could classify this location as one of the more unique dining experiences in San Francisco.

![image 1](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-1.png?resize=479%2C359&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 5")

### **3. Mezcalito**

[Mezcalito](https://www.mezcalitosf.com/) bar and restaurant themed around mezcal with mezcal-focused cocktails plus pork belly benedict with corn hollandaise, gouda burgers, taco Tuesday and a generally Mexican-themed menu. The interior might even remind you of some of the [resort towns in Mexico](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/best-resort-towns-in-mexico/). This makes it one of the really cool places to eat in San Francisco.

![Coffee menu](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/most-unique-restaurants-in-san-francisco.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 6")

### **4. Manufactory Food Hall (San Francisco International Airport, Boarding Area A)**

 When have you read about a great restaurant recommendation that is at an airport? Exactly! Well, four of San Francisco’s favourite chefs  Pim Techamuanvivit of Kin Khao, Elisabeth Prueitt and Chad Robertson of Tartine, and Gabriela Cámara of Cala—have joined forces to change that.

 On offer will be tacos and breakfast Tortas, great Thai and morning buns and seasonal tarts from Tartine’s. All at the  **[Manufactory Food Hall – Boarding Area A at the International Airpot.](https://www.forbes.com/sites/christinaliao/2017/12/18/san-francisco-airport-new-manufactory-food-hall-local-restaurants-tartine-cala-kin-khao/#54b056a62f0c)** While this may not be where your mind goes, it certainly is one of the more unique places to eat in San Francisco.

![cowgirl Creamery store at the San Francisco Ferry Building](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/san-francisco-unique-restaurants.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 7")

### 5. **Eight Tables by George Chen**

This restuarant in Chinatown is based on Si Fang Cai or the private chateau cuisine of the Chinese elite. That means a 10-course tasting menu that starts after you cross a gold-threaded welcome mat into a private elevator and are whisked up to your own private club. [Eight Tables by George Chen](https://eighttables.com/) is one of the top restaurants in San Francisco.  
  
Much of what you see in this restaurant won’t remind you of the [famed Chinese landmarks](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/china-landmarks/), but the food sure has the signature taste of China. If you’re looking for unique SF restaurants, this fine dining option should be a strong contender.

### **6. Dumpling Time**

For one of the most fun places to eat in San Francisco head to [**Dumpling Time**.](https://www.dumplingtimesf.com/) You can watch cooks prefer the dumplings in front of you. Make sure you order the King-Dum – the largest soup dumpling you have seen in some time that comes with a straw.

![san_francisco_tram](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/san_francisco_tram.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 8")

 Noodle ingredients at Dumpling Time San Francisco included everything from roasted pork belly to peanut butter. There are also noodles on offer if you are a complete freak and don’t like dumplings  (does anyone not like dumplings??).

These dumplings are a must-eat in San Francisco. This is one of the most fun San Francisco restaurants so be sure to give it a try. You won’t regret visiting one of the most interesting restaurants San Francisco has to offer.

![Display of wine at san francisco wine bar](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/unique-restaurants-san-francisco.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 9")

### 7. Foreign Cinema

**[Foreign Cinema](http://foreigncinema.com/)** combines amazing locally sourced food with classic films. This restaurant is very much known for its food – particularly its fried chicken.

They have an art gallery and a VIP lounge. Foreign Cinema is one of the more unique dining experiences in San Francisco.

### **8. Urban Putt**

 Who hasn’t wanted a fantastic dining experience pre or post-playing mini-golf? And wouldn’t it be better if you could play mini-golf to say 11 pm or midnight indoor?

Well, **[Urban Putt](https://www.urbanputt.com/)** heard about these desires and combined food and mini-golf. Urban Putt San Francisco has two restaurants – one for bites and one for some great California eating that goes far beyond the usual putt putt kiosk.

And have a drink at the Copper Bar first featuring many exciting craft brews. All of this makes for one of the most unique restaurants in the bay area.

![Colourful buildings on the streets of San Francisco](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/unique-restaurants-in-sf.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 11")

### **9. The Tonga room**

 If you’re looking for the most fun restaurants in [San Francisco](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/road-trip-from-los-angeles-to-san-francisco/) head no further. I loved [The Tonga Room](https://www.fairmont-san-francisco.com/dine/tonga-room/). This classic San Francisco restaurant is located in the beautiful Fairmont Hotel in the heart of San Francisco.

One of the things which makes this one of the most unique restaurants San Francisco has on offer is that it has a 75-metre swimming pool in the middle of the restaurant.

![image 16](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-16.png?resize=1200%2C800&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 13")

The swimming pool at the Tonga Room San Francisco also has a little boat in it that appears to be for a DJ. Best of all it actually rains inside the restaurant over the pool. I have never seen anything like this in a restaurant before.

There is a great bar as well as a restaurant which has its tables clustered around the pool. The [theme is Asian](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/asia-landmarks/) fusion with a bit of Tahiti and Hawaii. I enjoyed some fantastic ahi poke tuna tacos.

![image 15](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-15.png?resize=1197%2C800&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 14")

 The Tonga Room is one of those restaurants that has a sense of an occasion about it. It has fantastic fun and an upbeat atmosphere that is detectable from the moment you walk in. Put the Tonga Room high on your list for fun San Francisco restaurant options. To date, this is one of the most unique places to eat San Francisco provides.

## **10. Asia SF**

 If you are looking for fun places to eat in San Francisco look no further! The **[Asia SF restaurant](https://asiasf.com/)** is fully staffed by fabulous transgender ladies. Not only do these lovely ladies serve you food and drinks they also perform hourly on the Red Dragon Runway Bar.

Dinner shows happen nightly and the food has a good reputation. When it comes to candidates for the best dining experience in San Francisco Asia SF fits the bill and provides you with the opportunity to witness [things unique to Asia](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/asia-landmarks/).

![image 13](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-13.png?resize=1050%2C698&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 15")

### **11. Burma Superstar**

 I visited Myanmar/Burma a few years ago and fell in love with the food. I became particularly obsessed with their salads. The dressings feature a unique mix of sour and sweet flavours that take a tomato and, particularly aubergine salads to a whole new level.

However, there are very few good Burmese restaurants around – they tend to be more casual. I had to include Burma Superstar in this article as I know this is difficult to find cuisine. The menu is classic Burmese and best sampled and shared. If quirky restaurants in San Francisco is what you’re after, then don’t miss out on a visit to Burma Superstar during your time in San Fran.

![image 20](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-20.png?resize=532%2C800&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 16")

### **12. Forage SF**

**[Forage SF](https://www.foragesf.com/)** is both a restaurant, Wild Kitchen, and an experience. You can choose to do one of their amazing food-foraging classes and get involved in farm to table first hand – from urban sea foraging to wild food walks.

If you are too hungry to forage yourself Wild Kitchen is a roving underground supper club that brings 100 diners together for an 8-course meal. This is one of the opportunities that won’t be found regularly elsewhere in the world, so hop on this chance to have one of the most interesting dining experiences San Francisco has to offer.

![image 17](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-17.png?resize=532%2C800&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 17")

### **13. Eno Wine Bar**

 This super cute little wine Bar was virtually next door to the Hotel G, my boutique hotel of choice on my last visit to San Francisco. **[Eno Wine Bar](https://www.facebook.com/enowinebar/)** features an extensive menu of wine flights. I love wine flights.

From Mission Impossible (featuring the mission grape) to Faux Bordeaux it is going to be a big night when you hit Eno Wine Bar San Francisco.

They also serve wine, cheese and chocolate from local providers that constantly varies based on what is in season – which just allowed me to classify this as a restaurant as you can have the equivalent of a meal here.

It’s not one of the best restaurants in San Francisco but it is a great place for drinks and snacks.

![eno wine bar white wine flight 6 glasses](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/wine-flight.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 18")

### **14. The Ferry Building**

 I have saved the best to last. I completely fell in love with [**the Ferry Building**](https://www.ferrybuildingmarketplace.com/) on my last visit to San Francisco. It is an artisanal food heaven – if that is such a thing.

Multiple food and wine providers are in one place – from stalls to full-on restaurants. There is a fantastic outdoor seating area as well. I went on a San Francisco food tour that covered the Ferry Building and then went back again the next day.

![san_francisco_ferry-building](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/san_francisco_ferry-building.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 19")

 Everywhere here is good – from the amazing Acme bread (one of the local favorites) to Blue Bottle Coffee (you’ll know when you have found it from the queue).

Frog Hollow Farm has fantastic local produce and does a great brunch. Fantastic Vietnamese is on offer at The Slanted Door – and I love the name of its takeaway area – Out the Door.

![san_francsisco_ferry-building-humphry-slocombe](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/san_francsisco_ferry-building-humphry-slocombe-ice-cream-sauce.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 20")

 The Ferry Building is home to some of the most interesting restaurants in San Francisco and quirky new food concepts. On my most recent visit, I was delighted to discover**[Humphrey Slocombe ice cream](https://www.humphryslocombe.com/)**.

This ice cream stall was named after two characters from the classic [British](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/british-airways-world-traveller-plus-review/) comedy Are You Being Served.

![san_francisco_humphry-slocombe-menu](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/san_francisco_humphry-slocombe-menu.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 21")

 Their speciality is unique flavours. I enjoyed some very delicious Vietnamese coffee ice cream at Humphrey Slocombe. This was probably the least adventurous flavour on offer that day – I did try the pumpkin but it didn’t quite work for me.

There are then a variety of toppings available. I tried the bourbon caramel which was full-on – and hardened on top of my ice cream for some textural contrast for your taste buds.

![image 8](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-8.png?resize=468%2C700&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 22")

 Humphrey Slocombe also sells its products in tubs so you can take some back to your hotel – of course, you will have to eat it all to ensure that it doesn’t melt. When it comes to where to eat in San Francisco, The Ferry Building is at the top of my list.

![image 5](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/image-5.png?resize=480%2C640&ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 23")

 The other highlight for me at The Ferry Building is the **[Cowgirl Creamery](https://cowgirlcreamery.com/)**. Firstly – great name! Secondly wow cheese heaven. Then there is the menu – cheese toasties, grilled cheese of the day, Macaroni & Cheese – really it covers every cheese base.

Cowgirl Creamery cheese is a must eat in San Francisco.

![san_francisco_blue-bottle-coffee-bean-bags](https://i0.wp.com/theboutiqueadventurer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/san_francisco_blue-bottle-coffee-bean-bags.jpg?ssl=1 "14 Unique Restaurants in San Francisco You'll Love 24")

 There are so many amazing, unique and cool restaurants in San Francisco. It has become such a foodie city.

I think it is almost impossible to nominate the best dinner in San Francisco [California](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/lakes-in-southern-california/) or even the best restaurants in the [Bay Area](https://theboutiqueadventurer.com/best-places-to-visit-in-bay-area/) – there are too many options.

I would love to hear about your San Francisco eating experiences – please do email and let me know.

# San Francisco Best Food and Beverage 2026

### My top recommendations for all things food and beverage with a cultural norms and best etiquette to give you the best San Francisco food experience!

[Violet Witchel](https://substack.com/@violetwitchel)

Aug 15, 2025

Hi guys! As many of you know I’m originally from SF. I was born here and lived here till I was 12 then moved back after college. My family has always had their house here and I’ve lived in the city for almost 16 years now. It’s where I consider home where my love of food began. I truly think it’s one of the best food cities in the US. My most asked question in the DM is by far SF food recs so I figured it was about time to compile all of my favorites.

This list does skew a bit towards restaurants that have GF options as I’m gluten-free but I’ve included input from my husband. Not a single restaurant has paid to be on this list and I have paid to eat at every single restaurant on this list (*the support of my paid subscribers makes this possible so huge thank you!).* Meaning this includes my very honest opinions. Whether you’re visiting for work, pleasure or are a new resident I’ve tried to included things for any and every occasion along with important cultural norms to note prior to your visit.

Violet’s Substack is a reader-supported publication. To receive new posts and support my work, consider becoming a free or paid subscriber.

*The list is organized chronologically by category:*  
**Coffee, Brunch, Bakeries, Lunch, Dinner, Occasions & Splurges, Bars, and Takeout**  
💜 Purple hearts highlight my personal favorites.

**Some notes on San Francisco culture, norms, and etiquette**

**DON’T CALL IT FRISCO:**

Nope. Not FRISCO, not San FRAN. The only acceptable things to call the are are: “San Francisco”, “SF”, “The City”, “The Bay”, or “The Bay Area” (when including things outside SF). People born and raised in SF proper are called “SF Natives”. Locals generally like tourists and are friendly and accepting of them. People really do not appreciate it when you talk about SF being a dump or how it’s portrayed negatively in the media so keep that to yourself when talking to locals.

**Personal Safety:**

San Francisco is largely an incredibly safe and walkable city. In the entire time I’ve lived here I’ve never been a victim of violent crime. BE SMART. Don’t leave things in your car (purses, camera, backpack) in any neighborhood. You can safely walk around 99.9% of the city but use your best personal judgement.

**SF Line Culture:**

San Franciscans *love* a line. Don’t be surprised to see people lining up at 7 AM for brunch on a Sunday without a second thought. If you're visiting, this might catch you off guard, so I’ve included notes on typical wait times where relevant in case you want to plan around them.

One important tip: **Know what you want before you reach the front of the line and order concisely and quickly.** It’s considered rude here to hold things up by reading the menu on the spot.

**SF Early Morning Culture and Last Call:**

San Francisco is very much a morning city—you’ll see people out and about as early as 5:30 AM. Last call is at 2 AM, but many restaurants close their kitchens by 8 PM (10 PM on weekends). If you’re looking for late-night food after the bar, you’ll probably come across little grill carts selling what locals affectionately call “street meat.” While delicious, it’s not always gentle on the stomach; tread lightly if you choose to indulge in a “Danger Dog”.

**Competitive reservations:**

The most popular restaurants in SF often book out weeks, even months in advance. That said, many of them hold back around 20% of their tables for walk-ins or call-ins. If reservations look fully booked, don’t be discouraged! Try calling directly or showing up in person right when they open (usually around 5 PM) to get your name on the list. Be friendly and explain how excited you are to visit. You’d be surprised how often this works especially with a two top, especially if you’re willing to wait a bit.

**Attire:**

San Francisco is an extremely casual city; almost no restaurants have a dress code, and if they do, it’s rarely enforced. SF is famous for it’s microclimates and temps vary 10+ by neighborhoods. DRESS IN LAYERS! Sadly, Patagonia vests are perfectly acceptable even at some Michelin-starred spots. Gauche designer clothing is uncommon here and will make you very clockable as a tourist.

That said, putting in a little effort *does* tend to go a long way. Heels, a dress, or a clean collared shirt can sometimes get you a little preferential treatment.

***Let’s get into the recs!!***

### **Coffee Shops/Cafes**

*Some SF coffee shops will do this horrific thing of putting iced coffee drinks in a hot coffee cup with a sippy lid which is an immediate disqualification from this list and why BlueBottle is not included.*

[Andytown Coffee Roasters](https://andytownsf.com/?srsltid=AfmBOopvtsrHM6BTr58vI2rrMw9T8NkybORBHFQg85ksvZAC5Zb3gn0f)  
📍 *Fidi and Outer Sunset*

A classic: **The Snowy Plover** is their signature drink and a local favorite.

💜 [Neighbors Corner](https://www.instagram.com/neighborscorner/?hl=en)   
📍 *Noe Valley*

My favorite coffee shop in SF: absolutely perfect matcha and espresso drinks. I always get an iced oat milk matcha with vanilla syrup, and it is *divineeee*.

[The Mill](https://www.themillsf.com/)  
📍 *NOPA*

A great spot for getting work done: tons of natural light, wifi, excellent coffee, and a decent selection of delicious pastries and their signature toasts.

[Linea Caffe](https://lineacaffe.com/)  
📍Mission Dolores and Design District

Amazing specialty drinks.

[Saint Frank’s](https://www.saintfrankcoffee.com/)  
📍Russian Hill

My favorite hot latte in the city.

### **Brunch**

💜[Heist](https://www.heistsf.com/)  
📍 *Lower Nob Hill*

A cozy, sunlit spot with typical lines of 15-20 minutes. They serve cheesy scrambles, gluten-free breakfast burritos, and GF pancakes. My go-tos are the Captain Crunch Burrito and the GF Berry Pancakes.

[Breakfast Little](https://www.instagram.com/breakfastlittle/?hl=en)  
📍 *Mission*

Best gluten-free breakfast burritos in SF—my favorite is The Zeus. Great for takeout, fine for dining in.

[Zazie](https://www.zaziesf.com/)  
📍 *Cole Valley*

A cozy little French café known for its phenomenal benedicts. I love the Crab Benedict on a gluten-free muffin or the Mexico Scramble. To skip the line, put your name in and then head next door for a Bloody Mary at Finnegan’s till you get a text.

[Plow](https://www.eatatplow.com/)  
📍 *Portola*

Expect 30–60 minute lines at Plow, they’re one of the most hyped breakfast spots in SF. They serve great egg plates with delicious sausage and bacon, plus gluten-free pancakes. My favorite is The Plow with chicken apple sausage.

[Wooden Spoon](https://www.woodenspoonsf.com/)  
📍 *Castro*

Lots of classics with healthier options like yogurt bowls and veggie hash. They’re one of the few brunch spots with a full liquor license so they get my rec for boozy brunch. I get the soft scramble and Bloody Mary.

Pork Store  
📍*Haight, 1451 Haight St (not to be confused with the one on Valencia)*

Classic old school American diner with greasy food that can cure any hangover. This is a true locals spot and has been around forever. The waitress calls you sweetie and the check is a paper slip. I get the 2 egg breakfast platter with eggs over medium.

### **Bakeries**

[Arsicault](https://arsicault-bakery.com/) *(Pronounced: Ar-si-co)*  
📍*Presidio Heights*

Widely accepted as the best croissants in the city, long lines but move quickly (10-20min). No gf options. Sometimes a crew of 20+ cyclists will roll up at once.

💜[Mariposa Bakery](https://www.mariposabaking.com/)  
📍*Ferry Building*

Phenomenal 100% gluten-free pastries, I get a toasted cream cheese bagel. 10 minute line.

[Dynamo Donut](https://www.instagram.com/dynamodonut/?hl=en)  
📍 *Lower Mission and Marina Green Hut*

Great donuts, unique flavors, and gf options. They sellout of GF quickly so go early. The lemon buttermilk and carrot cake are the best gf donuts, I dislike the chocolate gf donut.

[Tartine](https://tartinebakery.com/)  
📍 *Inner Sunset and Mission Dolores*

Tartine has delicious breakfast/brunch and amazing pastries. Very kid friendly. They have a big selection from breads to cakes. I get the chocolate buckwheat cookie (gf) or the flourless chocolate cake. The banana cream pie is life changing if you’re not gf and people rave about the quiche.

### **Lunch**

[Souvla](https://www.souvla.com/)  
📍Marina and Hayes Valley

A classic SF Greek salad and wrap spot. I get the Pork Salad with a side of feta fries and extra yogurt dipping sauce. It’s kinda basic but always hits and feels healthy. It’s a perfect working lunch and they have really good soft serve.

[Golden Gate Deli](https://www.yelp.com/biz/golden-gate-market-deli-and-liquor-san-francisco-2)  
📍Presidio

This is my husband’s favorite deli. Dutch Crunch Rolls and Sourdough are SF classics if you’ve never tried. He gets a Dutch Crunch toasted with everything on it. Unfortunately they don’t have gf options. Ike’s is a chain so I’m hesitant to recommend but they has the best GF sandwiches in SF IMO.

### **Dinner**

[Wayfare Tavern](https://www.wayfaretavern.com/)  
📍Fidi

If they were filming Mad Men in SF this is where they would drink scotch and have business meetings. It’s a finance man hotspot. Classic American food, great burgers and potatoes. I get a filthy gin martini, shrimp cocktail, Wayfare Cobb (RIP to their caesar, they need to put it back on the menu ASAP), and the Whipped Ricotta Potatoes. *Private room available.*

[Little Original Joe’s and Original Joe’s](https://www.littleoriginaljoes.com/)  
📍 *Marina, West Portal and North Beach*

Great American/Italian. Tons of GF options (GF pasta), top tier service, classic cocktails, fun vibes. Perfect for date night and birthday parties. *Private room available.*

[Hog Island](https://hogislandoysters.com/)  
📍 *Embarcadero*

Best oysters in the bay. Fresh, cold, delicious. Their oyster bar is the place to get them the freshest but they’re the main suppliers to a lot of restaurants as well. I love the Sweetwaters.

[China Live](https://chinalivesf.com/)  
📍 *Embarcadero*

Enormous restaurant, great for bigger parties, delicious Dim Sum style food, dietary accommodating options available for most diets. I love the crackling skin chicken. *Private room available.*

[Zuni Cafe](https://zunicafe.com/)  
📍 *Castro*

Sexy vibes, legendary food, an iconic SF staple. A great date night spot with a really nice wine list. I get the chicken and fries.

[Pabu](https://theminagroup.com/restaurants/pabu-izakaya/)  
📍 *Fidi*

This is like the Hillstone of sushi. Staff are very well educated on gf options. Delicious cocktails (I love a lychee martini), sexy date night vibes, easy to get a res, accommodates large bookings. I get the rainbow roll. *Private room available.*

[Daeho](https://www.daeho-kalbijjim.com/)  
📍 *Japantown*

No gf options. An honorable mention for my husband’s favorite restaurant. Apparently insane Korean food, his favorite is the flaming cheesy short ribs.

[Lovely’s/Wood’s Burger](https://www.woodsbeer.com/colevalley)  
📍 *Cole Valley*

I could go on about this burger, it’s like In’n’Out but better. GF buns too. They share a space with the bar Lovely’s. Long lines (20-40 min wait), but you can drink outside while you wait. I get The Drive Thru on a gf bun.

💜[Elena’s](https://www.elenasmexican.com/)  
📍 *West Portal*

My go to date night/ friends are in town Mexican spot. The chicken enchiladas and margs are to die for. It’s hard to go wrong with anything on the menu. 30+ minute wait. Very friendly staff.

### **Special Splurges and Celebrations**

[Kokkari](https://kokkari.com/)  
📍 *Embarcadero*

Crazy good Greek food, wine, and cocktails. They roast a whole lamb in the fireplace sometimes and it’s next level. People dress a bit nicer here as well. Lots of corporate dinners here. *Private room available.*

💜[Roka Akor](https://rokaakor.com/san-francisco/location-information/)  
📍 *Jackson Square*

High end sushi and steak pairings, with an extremely well curated sake pairing and extensive Japanese Whiskey list. 95% of the menu is gf and they use house-made tamari on everything. Luxury vibes and amazing service. I like the chef’s selection sashimi (tuna and salmon are highlights), shishito peppers, miso corn, and Waygu offerings. Lychee spritz is a delicious NA. *(A semi private room with glass walls is available as well as a full buyout of the downstairs bar).*

💜[Cotogna](https://www.cotognasf.com/)  
📍 *Fidi*

I think this is the best Italian food in SF. Intimate, authentic, with an extremely high standard of service. The restaurant has the best vibes and manages to avoid the faux pretentious techie Michelin scene . It has the coziest decor with outdoor blankets. A fantastic date night spot for something like an anniversary. The corn pasta is probably one of the most famous dishes in SF and the chicken is next level. Best for celeb spottings.

### **Bars**

**Wine Bars:**

*Liquor licenses are typically around 250k+ in SF making SF cocktails expensive and SF wine bar culture quite prevalent!*

[Bodega](https://www.instagram.com/bodegasf/?hl=en)   
📍 *North Beach (not to be confused with Bodega the Vietnamese restaurant in the Tenderloin)*

Amazing people watching, the seats face big windows facing the street. Great wine. Nicely sunlit and a good spot for cute IG photos.

[Verjus](https://www.verjuscave.com/)  
📍Fidi

Shared tables, good bites, great downtown location to meet a coworker or a date night. (Slightly overwhelming ambient noise/restaurant sound design so if you have autism or sensory issues I’d avoid). But if you enjoy a raucous happy hour this place is amazing and friendly spot to socialize.

[Arcana](https://www.arcanasf.com/bar-restaurant)  
📍 *Mission*

Lush indoor greenery decor and lots of natural wines and tasty bites. The restaurant has really fresh air bc of all the plants. Closes on the early side. *Private room available.*

💜[Celeste](https://www.instagram.com/celestewinebar/?hl=en)  
📍 *Marina*

This wine bar is for the girlies: good music, greek apps, and a short but delicious wine list. Cute outdoor seating for daytime drinking and turns into more of a scene later.

**Full Service Bars:**

*The Marina is the main barhopping neighborhood, on Friday and Saturday nights it gets packed. If you’re young and want to go out without Ubering around this is the spot. The one bar I’d avoid is Westwood, my husband was accidentally roofied there and people get their phones stolen sometimes.*

[Bar Sprezzatura](https://www.barsprezzatura.com/)  
📍 *Fidi*

A sexy vibe and interior design. Great for cocktails and bites with coworkers, lots of mocktail options. Deviled eggs are delicious, spritzes are crisp and refreshing, imo the cheese plate is overpriced but very tasty and perfect to put on a company card.

[Balboa Cafe](https://www.balboacafesf.com/)  
📍 *Marina*

This is an iconic SF bar, it’s admittedly a little fratty but who doesn’t love a fratty time on rare occasion. Espresso martinis are good, dirty martinis are great, there’s occasional celeb spottings, and men usually pay for drinks. It’s a fun outdoor space that turns into a party on a sunny day. Crowd is early 20’s.

💜[Left Door](https://www.leftdoor.com/about)  
📍 *Cow Hollow*

This is a less fratty version of Balboa with better cocktails and slightly older crowd. Great to meet people if you’re in your later 20’s-30’s There’s sometimes a line and the bouncers can be a bit picky with who they let in, dressing nicely helps. I think they have the best espresso martini in SF, it’s a little chocolatey, it’s really good. This is a great night cap to GNO.

[Golden Gate Tap Room](https://www.ggtaproom.com/m)  
📍 *Nob Hill*

My guy friends have told me this is the best sports bar in SF. Great place to watch a Warriors game and lots of beers on tap.

### **Takeout**

*These restaurants are setup for takeout. They’re my go-to’s for UberEats. The in person experience is eh, but if you’re ordering in they absolutely knock it out of the park.*

**[Le Ros Thai](https://lersros.com/)**  
Delicious Thai food, curry’s are amazing and don’t skip the mango sticky rice.

💜**[Om Indian](https://omindianonline.com/)**  
My favorite Indian in the city with amazing GF naan. I get medium spicy butter chicken with basmati rice and gf garlic naan. I UberEats this at least once a week.

**[Pakwan (Indian/Pakistani)](https://pakwanrestaurant.com/)**

Also delicious Indian with more Pakistani influence. Lamb Biryani is delicious.

If you’ve made it this far, THANK YOU. As always thank you for your support and I hope you have the best trip!!!
