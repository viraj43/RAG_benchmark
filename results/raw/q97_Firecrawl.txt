# Things to Do in San Francisco in the Winter: 24 Fun Cool Weather Activities

![Jill on a windy day at the Golden Gate Bridge](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/profile-jill-68x68.png)

**By**: [Jill Loeffler](https://www.sftourismtips.com/about-me.html)  • **Updated**: December 8, 2025

Things to do in San Francisco in the winter include both indoor and outdoor activities.

While the weather is generally cool during this time of year, you might find it pleasant one day and too cold out the next.

I love this time of year in SF, but it's good to expect the weather to change from one day to the next.

My list will give you plenty of options so you can select the right activities for your stay.

I've included top museums, cruises, and shows.

I've also included some great ideas for you if you plan to be here during the Thanksgiving, Christmas, or New Year's holidays.

## 1\. California Academy of Sciences

![Two gold and blue Macaws at the California Academy of Sciences](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/gold-blue-macaws-cal-academy.jpg)A pair of gold and blue Macaws that call the California Academy of Sciences' rainforest their home.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fgold-blue-macaws-cal-academy.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

A great way to escape the winter weather and spend the day indoors is by marveling at the wide range of cool exhibits at the California Academy of Sciences.

SF's most visited museum contains a planetarium, an aquarium, a four-story living rainforest, and a natural history museum, all in one. Plus, you get to admire a huge skeleton of a T-Rex.

The Cal Academy of Sciences is located on the eastern side of San Francisco's beautiful [Golden Gate Park](https://www.sftourismtips.com/golden-gate-park-san-francisco.html), right across from the de Young Fine Arts Museum.

You can take the N-Judah or the 5-Fulton Muni bus to get here or stop by on your [Hop-On/Hop-Off tour](https://www.getyourguide.com/san-francisco-l61/24-48-or-72-hour-san-francisco-hop-on-hop-off-tour-t61883/?partner_id=C8D2E&utm_medium=online_publisher&placement=content-middle&cmp=things-winter-hop-on).

## 2\. Theater Performances

When it's cold out, it's an especially nice treat to spend the evening at the theater. In San Francisco, you'll find several performances a month at different venues.

Broadway musicals like Hamilton, The Lion King, and Finding Neverland are very popular, but you can also watch other stage plays and alternative dramatic productions.

During the holidays, you may opt for a show like A Christmas Carol.

San Francisco's Theater District encompasses an area near downtown, spread among the Civic Center, [Union Square](https://www.sftourismtips.com/union-square-san-francisco.html), and Tenderloin neighborhoods. Here, you'll find grand, historic theaters like the Orpheum, and smaller, independent spaces like the SF Playhouse.

## 3\. Christmas Lights & Tree Lighting Ceremonies

![Macys Decorations in San Francisco](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/macys-christmas-sf.jpg)Macy's in Union Square is always lit up for the Christmas Season!

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-4&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fmacys-christmas-sf.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

One of my favorite things to do in San Francisco in the winter is attend the various tree lighting ceremonies and admire the twinkling decorations all around town.

This is the time of year when SF really comes alive with beautiful lights and holiday spirit, some public spaces bringing in [Christmas](https://www.sftourismtips.com/christmas-in-san-francisco.html) trees that are several stories tall, and others displaying life-size gingerbread houses.

Among the top spots to check out our lovely trees are in Union Square, [Pier 39](https://www.sftourismtips.com/pier-39-san-francisco.html), and [Ghirardelli Square](https://www.sftourismtips.com/ghirardelli-square-san-francisco.html).

You'll also find numerous decorations throughout the city, including lights on the City Hall, downtown buildings during Let's Glows, bridges, and in residential neighborhoods.

## 4\. City Bus Tours

If you want a convenient way to see all the major attractions and learn more about San Francisco, consider a city bus tour.

You have several options that vary in price and duration, but they all allow you to skip figuring out how to get from one place to another.

This is especially popular on cool weather days, when people prefer not to walk or wait outside for long.

Almost all the bus tour options hit the [Golden Gate Bridge](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-golden-gate-bridge.html), [Chinatown](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-chinatown.html), and [Fisherman's Wharf](https://www.sftourismtips.com/fishermans-wharf-san-francisco.html) along their route, and some include up to 19 stops.

You can choose from a variety of tours, including self-paced options that allow you to hop on and off at your preferred sights, a tour that includes a visit to Alcatraz, an evening tour, and more.

## 5\. Alcatraz

![Winter brings sweeping clouds over Alcatraz and the Golden Gate Bridge](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/alcatraz-in-winter.jpg)The sweeping clouds around San Francisco in the winter make for amazing photos. I took this in early December on my way over to Alcatraz.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-5&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Falcatraz-in-winter.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

While the ferry ride over to the island may be chilly, most of the tour of the prison is actually indoors. Plus, no matter what season you visit San Francisco, touring Alcatraz is a must.

This fascinating ex-federal prison, which once housed the worst of the worst criminals in the middle of the bay, is one of [SF's most popular attractions](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-attractions.html), so tickets go quickly.

Tours to Alcatraz run seven days a week, and in the evenings from Tuesday to Saturday. To board the ferry, head to Pier 33 on the Embarcadero near Fisherman's Wharf.

Dress in warm clothes for the 20-minute boat ride, then enjoy your audio tour and walk around the grounds on the island.

## 6\. Exploratorium

The Exploratorium is a fantastic science museum for adults and kids of all ages, as all of its exhibits are interactive.

Explore five large galleries, each based on a different topic: sound and vision, the structure of living things, weather, mechanics, and human behavior.

You'll have so much fun as you learn first-hand how things work.

The Exploratorium is located on Piers 15 and 17, about a half mile north of the Ferry Building along the Embarcadero. The museum is spacious and well-designed, and a great indoor stop from the winter weather.

>\> [Pick up your tickets before your trip](https://www.getyourguide.com/san-francisco-l61/san-francisco-exploratorium-skip-the-line-full-day-ticket-t244442/?partner_id=C8D2E&utm_medium=online_publisher&utm_source=jill_loeffler&placement=content-middle&cmp=winter-things-to-do-exploratorium)

## 7\. Chronicle Wine Tasting Event

One of the best things to do in San Francisco in the winter takes place every [February](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-events-in-february.html) and gives you the opportunity to sample over 800 award-winning wines.

It's the annual Chronicle Wine Competition, which selects the very best American wines among some 5,000 entries to share at this public tasting.

You can attend this delicious event at the Fort Mason Center on the waterfront in the [Marina District](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-marina.html), not far from Fisherman's Wharf. The large festival pavilion is indoors, and the wine will be sure to warm up your Saturday afternoon.

## 9\. Film Festivals

San Francisco is home to nearly 50 film festivals a year, each featuring a different theme, genre, or culture.

The annual winter festivals include the American Indian Film Fest, the Transgender Film Fest, Save the Waves, Noir City, the SF Indie Fest, and more.

You'll find these film festivals all over SF, with screenings at the Roxie Theater in the [Mission](https://www.sftourismtips.com/mission-district-san-francisco.html), the Castro Theater in the [Castro](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-castro.html), and other venues.

## 11\. Holiday Lights Tours

One of my favorite things to do in San Francisco in the winter is to head out on a holiday lights tour.

You will find several fun options that take you to the hot spots around town.

My favorite places to stop include City Hall, as it's decked out in red and green, and [Pier 39](https://www.sftourismtips.com/pier-39-san-francisco.html) with its large annual Christmas tree at the entrance.

My favorite is the holiday lights tour on a shiny red fire engine. It has seating for 12 and offers unobstructed views all around town.

Don't worry, they also bring along some blankets to keep you warm during the journey.

[Find tickets for this tour here](https://www.viator.com/tours/San-Francisco/San-Francisco-Fire-Engine-Holiday-Lights-Tour/d651-3952HOLIDAY?pid=P00037366&mcid=42383&medium=link&campaign=winter-things-holiday-tour).

## 12\. Concerts & Live Music

![Winter Concert at the Chase Center](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/winter-indoor-concert-chase-center.jpg)A concert at the Chase Center, one of our largest indoor concert venues.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-2&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fwinter-indoor-concert-chase-center.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

Another one of the best things to do in San Francisco in the winter is to enjoy a concert.

SF not only attracts the biggest names in all music genres, but also boasts a wealth of local talent, emerging bands, and jazz ensembles.

San Francisco boasts a vibrant live music scene, featuring large concert halls like the Warfield and the Fillmore, as well as intimate venues like Bimbo's 365 Club and Cafe du Nord.

You'll find them all over the city, in the Civic Center District, [North Beach](https://www.sftourismtips.com/north-beach-san-francisco.html), and more.

In addition, the biggest stars in pop and rock tend to hold their concerts at sports arenas in nearby cities, such as Oakland or San Jose.

## 13\. City Hall

San Francisco City Hall is not only an important government building, but an elegant work of architecture that shines as the centerpiece of the Civic Center District.

Its gold dome is the tallest in the nation and contains ornate carvings, staircases, and columns beneath it.

For the first time in 2025, City Hall is lit up with project artwork for the holidays. The display runs from December 6 through the end of the year.

You can catch shows every half hour between 5:30 pm and 10 pm.

You can easily reach City Hall by getting off at the Civic Center station on Muni or BART.

It's located just two blocks north of Market Street, where the large plaza marks the entrance.

## 14\. Whale Watching Tour

The coast of San Francisco offers fun, exhilarating whale watching experiences all year long.

During the winter months from [December](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-events-in-december.html) to [May](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-events-in-may.html), you have the chance to see gray, sperm, and killer whales migrating in these waters.

You might also spot dolphins, leatherback turtles, and sea lions.

The whale watching tours depart from Fisherman's Wharf on weekday mornings and afternoons.

On your 2.5-hour tour, you'll cruise under the Golden Gate Bridge and out to the Pacific Ocean, where you may venture up to 8 miles out looking for whales.

## 15\. Sample Local Wines

Nearby Napa Valley and Sonoma County produce some of the best wines in the world.

While you're in San Francisco this winter, take advantage of tasting these delicious local wines at a variety of wine bars, tasting rooms, weekly seminars, and festivals around town.

Or, take a day trip over to wine country to sample from the source!

A few wineries are located right in the city and offer tasting rooms at Fisherman's Wharf, in the SoMa and Dogpatch Districts, and on Treasure Island.

Great wine bars are located all over town, and the large tasting events often take place at the Fort Mason Center in the Marina.

One of the most anticipated things to do in San Francisco in the winter is spending a night out on New Year's Eve watching the annual fireworks display.

Bundle up and head toward the bay to see them light up the sky, or check out my list of best restaurants with fireworks to watch the show as you enjoy a special meal.

The annual fireworks show launches over the water near the [Ferry Building](https://www.sftourismtips.com/ferry-building-san-francisco.html) and the Bay Bridge, and it's definitely worth making your way early to the waterfront along the Embarcadero to get a seat, taking a cruise on the bay to see them up close, or watching from one of my recommended restaurants.

You can also view them from Treasure Island.

>\> [More about watching this year's NYE fireworks display](https://www.sftourismtips.com/new-years-eve-fireworks-in-san-francisco.html)

## 17\. Comedy Shows

There's nothing like a good laugh to warm you up and get you out of the winter blues.

In San Francisco, there are comedy and improv shows nearly every night of the week, and you often have the chance to see some big names in stand-up, like Russell Peters, Dave Chappelle, and Dana Carvey.

The two main comedy clubs are Cobb's in North Beach and the Punch Line in the Financial District.

San Francisco's favorite theater performance, as an ode to the city, Dear SF, also plays in North Beach.

In addition, you'll find other venues in Union Square and the Mission.

## 18\. Shopping

![Shopping Winter SF](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/shopping-winter-sf.jpg)Shoppers at Pier 39 outside the Christmas in San Francisco store, which is open year-round.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-12&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fshopping-winter-sf.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

Whether you are holiday gift hunting, picking out souvenirs, or just like to browse, San Francisco has lots of great shopping options for you.

There are a few large malls with brand-name stores, areas like Pier 39 and [Ghirardelli Square](https://www.sftourismtips.com/ghirardelli-square-san-francisco.html) with local shops and dining, and strips of upscale neighborhoods lined with boutiques.

Many people enjoy the excitement of downtown and shopping at the big stores in Union Square.

For something a little more relaxed and personalized, try Union and Chestnut Streets in the Marina, or head to the Ferry Building for gourmet treats.

And don't forget that the best bargains on trinkets of all sorts are in Chinatown!

>\> [My list of the top places to go shopping in SF](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-shopping.html)

## 19\. Walking Tours

Though you may have to bundle up a bit more, [San Francisco's winter weather](https://www.sftourismtips.com/weather-in-san-francisco-ca.html) is often still sunny and crisp, and perfect for taking a walking tour in the city.

There are wonderful free tours offered by SF City Guides every day of the year, and there are also paid tours that offer an intimate look at neighborhoods like the Castro, North Beach, and Chinatown.

Some of the most popular walking tours show you around the [Japanese Tea Garden](https://www.sftourismtips.com/japanese-tea-garden-san-francisco.html), Fisherman's Wharf, areas with Art Deco or Victorian architecture, and the [city's legendary murals](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-murals.html).

There are tour routes in practically every part of SF, and the meeting place is usually the closest landmark.

>\> [Read more about the best walking tours around SF](https://www.sftourismtips.com/san-francisco-walking-tours.html)

## 20\. Nutcracker Ballet

One of the most classic things to do in San Francisco in the winter is to attend a special seasonal performance of The Nutcracker.

The renowned SF Ballet was actually the first company in the US to produce this beloved Christmas story on stage and remains one of the most popular today.

The SF Ballet performs at the beautiful, historic War Memorial Opera House behind City Hall in the Civic Center District.

You can easily get here by taking BART or Muni to the Civic Center station and walking west on Grove Street from Market.

## 21\. Ice Skating

![Ice Skating Union Square in December](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/ice-skating-union-square-december.jpg)The Union Square Ice Skating Rink which is open from mid-November to mid-January every year.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-11&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fice-skating-union-square-december.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

Each winter, San Francisco brings a little holiday magic right to the heart of Union Square, where you can strap on your skates and enjoy a wonderful time on ice, surrounded by a towering Christmas tree and decorated buildings.

This rink is open from early November to the first week of January, making for the perfect refreshing break from a long winter day of shopping or sightseeing.

There is also a second rink in Thrive City near the Chase Center that is open for a few days every December.

## 22\. Chinese New Year

![Chinese New Year Dragons](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/chinese-new-year-dragons-sf.jpg)Dragons dancing during the Chinese New Year Celebrations in San Francisco's Chinatown.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-9&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fchinese-new-year-dragons-sf.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

A little later in the winter, in March 2026, San Francisco celebrates the Chinese New Year with almost a month of events, the biggest of which is the parade that brings colorful dragons, lion dancers, drums, and lanterns through the downtown streets.

In addition to the large parade, the Chinese community hosts a flower market fair, basketball jamboree, Miss Chinatown pageant, street fair, and a 5K-10K run to celebrate the new lunar new year.

All events are based in Chinatown, which lies north of Union Square and south of North Beach.

>\> [More about this large annual parade in downtown SF](https://www.sftourismtips.com/chinese-new-year-san-francisco.html)

## 23\. The Great Dickens Christmas Fair

![Great Dickens Fair in SF](https://www.sftourismtips.com/images/great-dickens-fair-winter-things.jpg)Carolers performing at the Great Dickens Fair in San Francisco.

[21Save](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?guid=o88iWZRScQmS-10&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fthings-to-do-in-san-francisco-in-the-winter.html&media=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.sftourismtips.com%2Fimages%2Fgreat-dickens-fair-winter-things.jpg&description=24%20Fun%20Things%20to%20Do%20in%20San%20Francisco%20in%20the%20Winter%202025%2F2026)

Every holiday season since 1970, you'll find this fascinating period Christmas party abuzz with colorful characters from literature and history, inviting you to enjoy the quaint cobblestone lanes of Victorian London.

During this fun tradition, let yourself be led by the flickering candlelight, the variety of unique handmade gifts, and the aroma of freshly roasted chestnuts and other holiday delights.

There is so much to explore from 10 am to 6 pm over the five weekends from November 22 - December 21.

It's also open on the Friday after Thanksgiving.

You'll find this annual winter event at the Cow Palace in Daly City.

>\> [Find out more here on their official site](https://dickensfair.com/)

## 24\. SF Giants Fan Fest

Winter can be a long season for baseball fans without any games.

But luckily, SF Giants fans get to start each new season early with the annual Fan Fest, an event that allows you to meet the players, get their autographs, pick up new merchandise, and play catch on the field.

The Giants Fan Fest takes place in February or March.

It is one of the signals that winter is finally coming to an end and baseball season is soon to start.

Head over to Oracle Park on the water in SoMa, just south of the Bay Bridge, to enjoy the festivities.

# San Francisco in Winter

San Francisco is known for its cool, windy summers, but its winters are surprisingly mild and temperate. In fact, most of the time you’ll find that the days are actually warmer and sunnier between September and October. The weather is perfect for getting out and about, and there are plenty of seasonal activities and events to keep you busy.

Here are the top things to do in San Francisco over winter…

## **Enjoy fog-free sightseeing**

June to August is fog season in San Francisco. The wispy white stuff rolls in during the early morning and lingers all day giving the city sights a romantic look. However, it does mean that some of the landmarks and attractions can be heavily obscured. Pick winter for your San Fran [bus tour](https://www.bigbustours.com/en/san-francisco/san-francisco-tour-tickets-and-passes/san-francisco-bus-tours/?qty[adult]=0&qty[child]=0&focus=top-filter-form/) adventure and you’ll get a much better look at the well-known sights, including the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island. Take along your best camera and get those picture-perfect snaps.

![Sightseeing](https://www.bigbustours.com/media/wysiwyg/Article-Images-2020/San-Francisco/BBT-Winter-Article-IMG-1-GoldenGate.jpg)

## **Step back in time**

Fancy getting a taste of Victorian London right in the heart of the city? Head to the Great Dickens Christmas Fair which is dedicated to all things 19th century. You’ll get the chance to experience Dickens’ London while shopping for those handmade gifts and decorations. Stroll along the winding lanes and explore lamp-lit shops, meet colourful characters in traditional costume and enjoy the smells of roasting chestnuts. There are pubs and dance halls to duck into when you need a break from all that shopping, and literary performances on stages throughout the venue to keep you entertained too.

## **Visit the zoo**

When we say Christmas attraction, you probably don’t think zoo. But, San Francisco Zoo is one of the top places to be over the festive season. It transforms into a winter wonderland with lights, decorations and special events. There are nightly performances, a Christmas market where you can pick up gifts, and games to entertain the kids. On selected dates, Santa also makes an appearance with a few of his reindeer, so you’ll be able to meet the big man himself.

![ZooLights](https://www.bigbustours.com/media/wysiwyg/Article-Images-2020/San-Francisco/BBT-Winter-Article-IMG-2-ZooLights.jpg)

## **Explore Alcatraz**

Visiting the ex-federal former prison is a San Francisco must-do at the best of times, but it’s even better in winter. The attraction is so popular that visiting often requires advanced bookings. but from January through to March there are less crowds, so you won’t have to fight for your spot. You’ll get an amazing fog-free view of the island as you approach on the ferry, plus glorious views over the bay. And, the actual tour of Alcatraz is indoors, so you won’t be freezing your socks off when learning all about the famous former inmates.

![Sightseeing](https://www.bigbustours.com/media/wysiwyg/Article-Images-2020/San-Francisco/BBT-Winter-Article-IMG-1-Sightseeing.jpg)

## **Get crabby**

If you love your sea-to-table fare, you’ll be pleased to hear that winter is Dungeness Crab season. That means crab on tap at the city’s best restaurants and eateries around Fisherman’s Wharf. There’s even all-you-can-eat events to celebrate the harvest, accompanied by live music performances and entertainment. If you’re hanging around the Fisherman’s Wharf area in January, you’ll also catch the sea lions barking and frolicking on Pier 39 during their most active season.

![Fisherman’s Wharf](https://www.bigbustours.com/media/wysiwyg/Article-Images-2020/San-Francisco/BBT-Winter-Article-IMG-3-Crabby.jpg)

Start planning your winter escape to San Francisco today. It’s arguably the best time to sightsee in the city, and you’ll get the chance to get involved in some unique festive activities too. And for more ideas on how to spend your time in Golden City, [click here](https://www.bigbustours.com/en/san-francisco/things-to-do-in-san-francisco/) for more inspiration.

Title: Is San Francisco Worth Visiting in the Winter?
[Skip to content](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/is-san-francisco-worth-visiting-in-the-winter/#content)

Some spots just aren’t as appealing to visit during the harsh winter months. The good news? San Francisco is not one of them!

In fact, San Francisco takes a flip-flop approach to seasons. Summer is known to be foggy and chilly (cue Mark Twain’s famous quote: “The coldest winter I’ve ever spent was a summer in San Francisco.”).

Winter, on the other hand, is entirely underrated. San Francisco is known to have lovely mild winters that allow you to wear your favorite sweaters and coats without chilling you to your core. Enjoy tons of outdoor San Francisco winter activities, city skyline views and creative establishments year-round!

The adventure continues even once winter settles in – here is everything you need to know about visiting San Francisco in the winter!

TL;DR: San Francisco is definitely worth a visit in the winter! With its mild climate, it makes for a great spot for tourists with little to no snow, mild temperatures, and stunning views.

![sf skyline in winter](https://baycitybike.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/sf-winter-1024x363.png)

## Benefits of Visiting San Francisco in Winter

### **Explore the City with Fewer Crowds**

Spring and Fall are widely known to be the “best” times to visit the city, thanks to the clear, warm weather. But with that warm weather comes the price of longer lines, more competitive tour reservations and more heads blocking your views. So if you want to explore the city with minimal crowds vying for the same San Francisco activities as you, winter is a great time to visit. You’ll likely find cheaper flight options too, [especially if you use a service like Kayak](https://www.kayak.com/) to compare flight deals from hundreds of sites.

### **Lower Prices**

Thanks to flex pricing, visiting San Francisco during off-season times can get you better hotels, tours and transportation deals. So if you’re looking for a cheaper trip to a city known for its steep price tag, winter is a great time to plan your trip to San Francisco.

### **Easier to Make Reservations and Bookings**

There’s nothing more frustrating than being excited about an experience only to realize it’s booked out for your entire trip. [Alcatraz](https://baycitybike.com/activity-category/alcatraz-tours/) is a classic example of a San Francisco activity that is hard to book – but much easier in the winter than in the summer. That also goes for San Francisco’s most popular restaurants and hotels! You should always book well in advance, but visiting San Francisco in winter can give you a unique edge.

### **Great Crisp and Mild Weather (but expect quick weather changes)**

Winter in San Francisco doesn’t have that biting cold of the midwest, heavy snows of the northeast or near-constant rain of the pacific northwest. So while you can expect to experience some rainy, chilly and cloudy days, it is definitely one of the more mild winters you can find around the country!

### **Less Fog**

If you haven’t met [Karl the Fog](https://www.instagram.com/karlthefog/), you will eventually! But if you visit San Francisco during the winter, you may see a bit less than if you spend time in the area during the summer. Playfully deemed “June Gloom” and “Fogust,” the summer months are more prone to having giant walls of fog covering all the city and coastal views. Winter may be more cloudy and chilly, but you can take in the sights a bit easier.

![no fog views of the golden gate bridge in sf during the winter](https://baycitybike.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/no-fog-in-sf-1024x574.png)

## Things to do in San Francisco in the Winter

With San Francisco’s winter weather, mixing outdoor and indoor activities throughout the day is the secret recipe to a perfect trip. Here are some of our favorite things to do in San Francisco during the winter!

### [**Golden Gate Bridge Bike Tour**](https://baycitybike.com/activity/golden-gate-bridge-to-sausalito-tour/)

You can’t visit San Francisco without experiencing the views from the iconic Golden Gate Bridge. Yes, you can drive, bus or walk across – but the best way to take in the stunning scenery of the bridge is by bike.

Biking the Golden Gate Bridge has the best of all worlds – It’s easier and quicker than walking, you get more views than you could get from a bus, and you don’t need to deal with the traffic that comes with driving. And once you cross this 1.7-mile bridge, you’ll land in the charming coastal town of [Sausalito](https://baycitybike.com/blog/guided-bike-tours/what-to-do-in-san-francisco-bike-the-bridge-and-sausalito-day-trip/) for some strolling, sightseeing or a lovely lunch.

If you go during the warmest part of the day and wear layers, you can take a [Golden Gate bike trip](https://baycitybike.com/activity/golden-gate-bridge-to-sausalito-tour/) during the winter! We just recommend you wear gloves to keep your hands toasty as you cycle across the most famous bridge on the West Coast.

![golden gate bridge to sausalito tour](https://baycitybike.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Golden-Gate-Bridge-to-Sausalito-Tour-4.jpg)

### **Visit the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art**

Art lovers, unite! The [San Francisco Museum of Modern Art](https://www.sfmoma.org/) will guide you through a wide range of artists that are known for contemporary and modern art. You can walk through more than 33,000 works by world-renowned artists such as Jackson Pollock, Frida Kahlo, and Andy Warhol, as well as new and innovative exhibitions that are regularly updated. Enjoy unparalleled displays of architecture, installations and events as the perfect indoor activity during particularly chilly or rainy winter days.

### **Find some theater (past the big ticket shows)**

You can see broadway and off-broadway shows in any major city in the world – so find some performances that are uniquely San Francisco! Some truly memorable shows you can’t find anywhere else are perfect for adding intrigue to a brisk San Francisco winter evening.

[Audium](https://www.audium.org/) is a one-of-a-kind sound bath experience that uses spatial sound art to create a deeply immersive experience. [The Speakeasy](https://www.thespeakeasysf.com/) is an immersive theater experience where you can interact with flappers, gangsters, and bootleggers while you enjoy a night of 1920s fun!

### **E-bike tour around the city**

If you’re visiting for the first time, navigating the city can feel overwhelming. Having a helpful (and fun) overview of hotspots around the city by bike is the perfect way to help you find the nooks and crannies of the city you want to explore more!

You can embark on a [guided city tour](https://baycitybike.com/activity/streets-of-san-francisco-electric-bike-tour/) or create a self-guided adventure around the city with [e-bike rentals](https://baycitybike.com/rentals/#electric). Either way, you will get to enjoy details of the city you wouldn’t catch by car. If you choose a self-guided adventure on a [bike rental](https://baycitybike.com/rentals/), take a loot at our favorite [scenic bike routes](https://baycitybike.com/blog/road-biking-san-francisco/scenic-bike-routes-of-san-francisco/) and our [favorite neighborhoods](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/guide-to-must-visit-san-francisco-neighborhoods/) to bike to.

![SOSF Group Tour - San Francisco - Bay City Bike](https://baycitybike.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/SOSF-Group-Tour-San-Francisco-Bay-City-Bike.jpg)

### **Cooking classes**

San Francisco is a foodie city – so dive into the cooking world and learn some local cooking secrets!

[18 Reasons](https://18reasons.org/calendar) is a nonprofit cooking school that focuses on creating community around food and offers hands-on classes that cover a wide range of food. [The Cooking School at Cavallo Point](https://www.cavallopoint.com/dine/cooking-school) is a great romantic date night activity that provides a nice balance of luxury and fun. A stunning kitchen and a focus on local food make for a beautiful cooking experience.

### **Catch a stunning sunset**

With the city skyline, coastal views or even mountain scenery, there are endless spots to watch a sunset (if you’re lucky enough to avoid fog).

One of our favorite ways to ensure catching a gorgeous sunset is our [Sunset e-bike tour](https://baycitybike.com/activity/california-sunset-tour/). This comprehensive day tour includes an afternoon ride that will crescendo at a lovely spot to watch the sun go down with golden cotton candy skies to follow.

![Sunset at the Golden Gate Bridge - San Francisco - Bay City Bike](https://baycitybike.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Sunset-at-the-Golden-Gate-Bridge-San-Francisco-Bay-City-Bike.jpg)

### **Visit Alcatraz**

If you’re in the city, you can’t pass up an opportunity to get on [Alcatraz Island](https://baycitybike.com/activity-category/alcatraz-tours/). This prison was once impossible to escape, but nowadays, it’s increasingly hard to get into! So get your tickets as early as possible to ensure your spot on the ferry.

Known for its history as a former federal prison, you’ll learn about the prison’s most famous inmates, like Al Capone and The Birdman, as you tour the prison facilities. The chilling stories you’ll learn are softened by the stunning San Francisco coastal views you can see from the island.

## San Francisco Winter Events

Check your calendars – are you visiting San Francisco during one of these popular annual events? If so, add them to your trip itinerary to make some unique memories of the city with locals and visitors alike!

- [Silicon Valley Auto Show](https://www.svautoshow.com/) is like Disneyland for car lovers. Check out row after row of stunning makes and models.
- [Chinese New Year Parade and Festival](https://chineseparade.com/) is the biggest parade celebrating the Lunar New Year outside Asia.
- [Winter Wander-Land](https://visitunionsquaresf.com/winter-wander-land) in Union Square will include ice skating, a menorah lighting, a giant Christmas tree, a winter walk, and decorative cable cars. It’s truly iconic holidays in SF vibes!
- [San Francisco International Film Festival](https://sffilm.org/) is one of the longest-running film festivals in the United States. Returning in 2025 and 2026, showcases feature films, documentaries, and shorts from around the world to promote independent and international cinema.

## What To Know About Visiting San Francisco in the Winter

### **Bring Your Layers**

San Francisco weather can be fickle, so layers are your best friend! Be prepared for the cooler weather and bring layers, as it can be warm during the day and chilly at night.

### **Be Smart About Transportation**

San Francisco is a big city, so you’ll need to account for transportation. Walking everywhere will deplete energy and time, while renting a car or Ubering will drain your budget. One smart form of transportation is [e-bike rentals](https://baycitybike.com/rentals/#electric)! An e-bike is a fun, energy-conserving way to get around the city.

Check out our list of [eco-friendly transportation options](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/ecofriendly/) for more ways to get around San Francisco!

### **Include Indoor Activities**

Don’t be afraid of outdoor activities in San Francisco during winter! Most winter days are warm enough to spend extended time outside without catching a chill. But for the best experience, work in some cozy restaurants, fascinating museums or unique indoor performances to give yourself a break from the brisk weather.

## Winter FAQs

#### [Do you need rain gear to visit SF in the winter?](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/is-san-francisco-worth-visiting-in-the-winter/\#1758568191626-67f1c5ba-8018)

Yes, it’s a good idea. December through February is San Francisco’s rainy season. While it rarely rains all day, quick showers are common. A lightweight, packable rain jacket or umbrella will keep you comfortable, especially if you’re walking or biking around the city. Waterproof shoes are helpful too if you plan on lots of outdoor exploring.

#### [How cold is San Francisco in the winter?](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/is-san-francisco-worth-visiting-in-the-winter/\#1758568180713-2b0216d0-31a6)

San Francisco winters are mild compared to most U.S. cities. Average daytime highs are usually in the mid-50s °F (12–15°C), while nights dip into the upper 40s °F (8–10°C). You won’t see snow, but the combination of cool temps, wind, and damp air can make it feel colder than the thermometer suggests—especially near the Bay or Golden Gate Bridge.

#### [What is daylight like in winter in SF?](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/is-san-francisco-worth-visiting-in-the-winter/\#1758568180727-f14932c7-6db8)

Winter days are shorter, with about 9.5–10 hours of daylight. The sun typically rises around 7:20 AM and sets close to 5:00 PM in December and January. By February, the days get a bit longer. It’s smart to plan your outdoor activities earlier in the day so you can make the most of the natural light.

#### [Is SF's public transportation reliable in winter?](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/is-san-francisco-worth-visiting-in-the-winter/\#1758568292614-d66f8024-9931)

Yes. BART trains, Muni buses, streetcars, and cable cars run year-round regardless of weather. Rain may cause minor delays, but snow and ice are never an issue. If you’re biking, ferries are also a reliable option for crossing the Bay. Having a Clipper Card (SF’s transit pass) makes it easier to switch between systems. It’s also a good idea to rent a bike if you want to make sure you have a way to get somewhere, especially if you don’t mind (or even enjoy) a little bike ride in SF’s drizzle!

#### [What should I pack to visit San Fran in the wintertime?](https://baycitybike.com/blog/travel/is-san-francisco-worth-visiting-in-the-winter/\#1758568318875-c575b08c-ef1a)

Consider packing layers. The city’s microclimates can make one neighborhood sunny and mild while another is windy and chilly. Here’s a good packing list: A medium-weight jacket or fleece, a packable rain jacket or umbrella, long-sleeve shirts and sweaters, comfortable walking shoes (water-resistant if possible), a scarf, beanie or gloves if you get cold easily, and sunglasses.
