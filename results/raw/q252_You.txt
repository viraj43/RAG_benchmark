Title: DoD strikes deals with major tech firms to deploy AI on classified ...
The Defense Department has struck agreements with some of the nation’s largest technology companies to deploy their advanced artificial intelligence capabilities on its classified networks, part of a broader push to accelerate the military’s adoption of AI. SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, NVIDIA, Microsoft, Amazon Web Services and Reflection will integrate their AI capabilities into the department’s Impact Level 6 and Impact Level 7 network environments. The announcement excludes Anthropic, which was the first AI company to deploy its models on the Pentagon’s classified systems and was later designated by Hegseth a “supply chain risk” following a dispute over how the military can use the firm’s AI models. The department said the companies will deploy their capabilities for “lawful operational use,” a standard that was at the center of the dispute between Anthropic and DoD. The company’s tools were widely viewed across the federal government as superior to those of its competitors, and military users have been slow to phase them out.
