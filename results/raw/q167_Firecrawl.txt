Use Notes:

The Tags column includes information on application state (open, closed, late, rolling, etc.), program location and industry focus (if any, those without an industry focus are industry agnostic)

If the only published info on Next Program Start date is the month, it is listed as starting the first day of that month (ex. October 1, 2025 for the YC Fall 25 batch)

Sources for published terms are linked in the terms section if they differ from the main site

Rolling applications have a deadline set for the end of 2026

Tier explanations:

A - gold standard: good investment terms, strong reputation with other investors, high touch program

B - solid option: good investment terms, unproven or ok reputation with other investors, or low touch program

C - unproven: terms aren’t as good, niche focus or middling reputation with other investors, unclear program value

D - be wary: ok or bad terms, reputation with other investors is close to negative, unclear program value

F - avoid: terrible terms, joining program will turn other investors away

##### WE ARE A SEED STAGE ACCELERATOR

## What is AngelPad?

AngelPad is a seed-stage accelerator program based in NYC and San Francisco. Since 2010 we have launched more than [150 companies](https://angelpad.com/#portfolio). Every 6 months we select about 15 teams from a huge pool of applicants (usually around 2000) to work with us.While other accelerators have decided to “scale” and take on more and more companies or launch more and more cities, we have stayed true to our original goal: **Find awesome companies with founders we like to work with and spend three very intense months with them.** This focus has paid off, we have risen to be [the #1 Accelerator in the U.S.](https://angelpad.com/#sarp) (based on a real study from MIT/Brown University) and Strictly VC called us the “Anti-Y Combinator“.

During the program, [we work on everything](https://angelpad.com/#do) from finding product market fit, defining a target market to getting first validation for the company. We are also instrumental in helping companies prepare for fundraising and of course for investor meetings.

##### IN SHORT: EVERYTHING TO BE SUCCESSFUL

## What we do at AngelPad

Let’s face it, building a company is hard, especially the first year when there are limited resources, no or little support, and frankly all you have is you (maybe a co-founder) and the belief that you can do great things. That’s when we come in – we are always the first investor in companies, but more importantly, we are there for you: in our offices, together with you, figuring stuff out.

#### 1:1 Mentorship

Mentorship is at the core of AngelPad and we don’t believe in the “one-size-fits-all” advices that founders often get. At AngelPad Carine and Thomas work alongside the startup founders. We roll up our sleeves and figure stuff out – everything – together with the founders.

#### Funding

Starting a company costs money. We invest $120,000 in each company. Startups have also access to more than $300,000 in cloud credits from AWS, Google and Digital Ocean.

#### Community

Working, not just alongside us, but with the other founders of the cohort will become the foundation of your support system. Being a founder can be tough, few people understand what you go through – no matter if you struggle alone or are responsible for hundreds of people. We are there for you during and after the program and founders often form strong fellow entrepreneur ties that subside long after they exit AngelPad.

#### Investors

Getting funded is a key component of startups’ success and a core focus at AngelPad. We know most investors, we know what they invest in and what gets them excited. Investors know us and the quality of companies emerging from AngelPad. Being an AngelPad company opens many doors.

## You'll be in good company

AngelPad startups can be found in almost any tech vertical: SaaS, Marketplaces, Core Technology, Advertising, API, Mobile, Healthcare, AI, Data, B2B, B2C – even Drones. They all have one thing in common:  they tackle big problems in big markets and aim to build very big companies. Most of them are based in San Francisco and New York, but many come from all over the US and beyond to join AngelPad.
