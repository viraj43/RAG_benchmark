(LNVGY) and Dell Technologies Inc. (DELL), streaming-content providers Spotify Technology S.A. (SPOT) and Netflix Inc. (NFLX), and other technology companies like Microsoft Corp. Even so, Apple’s dominance in certain business segments has led to antitrust concerns and investigations in the United States and European Union. ## Understanding Mac Sales in Apple’s Portfolio
The company’s venerable Mac category includes personal computers such as the MacBook Air and MacBook Pro laptops and the iMac, Mac mini, Mac Studio, and Mac Pro desktop computers.
Mac sales generated $33.71 billion in revenue for FY 2025, 8.1% of Apple’s total revenue for the year. That represented an increase of 12% from the year before.


## iPad: Revenue Trends and Market Position
Apple’s iPad products include a range of multipurpose tablets: the iPad, iPad Pro, iPad Air, and iPad mini.
In terms of revenue, they constitute Apple’s smallest major product category, accounting for $28 billion in FY 2025, a roughly 5% increase from 2024.


## Exploring the Growth of Apple’s Service Sector
Apple has mounted a major corporate strategy to reduce its dependence on lower-margin hardware products, which face slowing growth, while accelerating the growth of its services business, which has higher margins and a more predictable, recurring revenue stream. The gross margin in Apple’s services business for FY 2025 was 75.4%, compared with 36.8% for its products business.
While Apple combines its services businesses into a single group for financial reporting purposes, they fall into five categories: advertising, AppleCare (technical support and services for Apple products), cloud services, digital content (including Apple Arcade, Apple Fitness+, Apple Music, Apple News+, and Apple TV), and payment services (including its co-branded credit card, Apple Card, and its Apple Pay cashless payment service).
Collectively, the group had net sales of $109.16 billion for FY 2025, up 14% for the year.

## An In-Depth Look at Apple’s Financial Performance
Apple posted a net income of $112.01 billion on revenue of $416.16 billion for its 2025 fiscal year (FY), ended Sept. 27, 2025. Apple refers to revenue as net sales in its financial reports. Both net income and revenue grew compared to the prior fiscal year. Net income climbed about 16% from 2024’s $93.74 billion, as revenue rose 6% from 2024’s $391.03 billion.
Revenue from Apple’s major product categories increased in FY 2025, with net sales for the Mac leading the way at 12%. The only decline was for wearables, home, and accessories, which fell 4%. Services were up 14%.
Apple breaks down its business by geographical segment, those being the Americas, Europe, Greater China, Japan, and Rest of Asia Pacific. Net sales were up in every region except Greater China, where they were down slightly.
