Additionally, headphones with minimal leakage let you crank your music without bothering your nearby colleagues.

The Sony WH-1000XM6 are the best headphones for work we've tested. These premium headphones have a comfortable fit for long days at work. Like previous XM-series headphones, they have a class-leading ANC system that can easily block out chatty coworkers and whiny A/C fans. With a continuous battery life of 31 hours, they can get you through a few workdays without recharging. They also support multi-device pairing, so you can stay connected to your smartphone and PC simultaneously.



They have a warm default sound profile, but voices are present and clear, so they're suitable for phone calls and meetings. You can always switch up the sound via the graphic EQ and presets in the app. If you like streaming your music at higher bitrates, LDAC and LC3 are also supported. Their integrated mic makes your voice sound clear and natural, and does a great job of separating it from the background din. That said, if you wear glasses, it's worth noting that the seal they form around your ears can easily be disrupted by the glasses' arms, causing a drop-off in bass frequencies.



If you're looking for office daily drivers with a more comfortable fit that don't suffer from the same issues with glasses, the [Bose QuietComfort Ultra Headphones (2nd Gen)](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/bose/quietcomfort-ultra-headphones-2nd-gen) are worth considering. They also have a powerful ANC system to effectively block noise in a bustling office space, though they don't attenuate common office sounds as well as the Sony. Their microphone performs worse, too, especially when separating your voice from a noisy environment: they're not ideal if you work in a noisy environment and can't book a room for a voice call. You can also check out our [best headsets for phone calls](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/best/by-usage/bluetooth-headset-for-phone-calls) article for other ideas.

If your office isn't noisy enough to warrant the outlay on the [Sony WH-1000XM6](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/sony/wh-1000xm6)'s top-tier ANC, the Razer BlackShark V3 Pro are a strong alternative. At this price point, no cans can match the Sony's noise isolation performance, but the Razer have a few strengths of their own. Their almost 70-hour battery life is over double that of the Sony's, and they also come with a wireless dongle that you can connect to your computer for a low-latency connection, which can help reduce lip-sync issues on video calls. They support Bluetooth, and you can use both technologies simultaneously to stay connected to your smartphone and PC. That said, you can only connect to one Bluetooth device at a time.



The Razer's detachable boom microphone does an impressive job of capturing your voice accurately, so you sound lifelike when attending online meetings. The mic also does a good job separating your voice from noise around you, so the sound of chatty coworkers won't intrude on your meetings. That said, there's no integrated mic. If you don't like the idea of being stuck with a boom mic all day, consider the [Sony ULT WEAR](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/sony/ult-wear-wireless). The Sony's integrated mic is almost as good as the Razer's, and their ANC performs a little better. That said, the Razer feel better-built, and their dedicated on-ear controls allow you to quickly and easily mute/unmute your mic, which can save hassle during the workday.

If noise isolation is a top priority for you and you don't mind your voice sounding a little thin on the phone, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 Wireless are a great option. These mid-range headphones have a sturdy build and a comfortable fit for most people. Their ANC feature punches above its price point, doing a fantastic job of blocking out a wide range of ambient sound, including noise like background conversations at the office. The integrated mic doesn't make your voice sound as natural and full-bodied as the [Razer BlackShark V3 Pro](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/razer/blackshark-v3-pro), but it does a comparable job of separating it from ambient sounds around you, which is a bonus if you work in a pretty noisy office.



Their continuous battery life of around 28 hours easily lasts through a few workdays, though not a full week. You can also listen to audio passively with the included cable. Their V-shaped sound profile adds a lot of extra rumble and punch to your audio. Instruments and voices can sound harsh, but you can use the in-app graphic EQ and presets to adjust the sound. The headphones don't leak much audio, so even if you like to listen to loud music at work, it won't bother people nearby. They also support multi-device pairing, which is nice if you connect to your phone and computer simultaneously.

If you're keeping your spending to a minimum, the Anker Soundcore Life Q20 2024 are versatile all-rounders. It's no surprise that this manufacturer dominates the lower-end market, thanks to their powerful ANC systems, which are uncommon in over-ears at this price point. They may not provide the same level of noise isolation as the [Anker Soundcore Space Q45 Wireless](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/anker/soundcore-space-q45-wireless), but they can still cut down on a good amount of ambient sound. They also last over 49 hours continuously, which will easily get you through a busy week at the office.



Like their predecessor, the [Anker Soundcore Life Q20 Wireless](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/anker/soundcore-life-q20-wireless), these headphones have a bassy sound, though there's some room for customization via the EQ and presets in the app. This newer model introduces Bluetooth multi-device compatibility into the mix, which is great for staying connected to both your laptop and phone. Plus, they have a comfortable and lightweight fit, although their design feels plasticky. If you need to take calls, their integrated mic offers decent recording quality, making your voice easy to understand. ## Notable Mentions

- **Bowers & Wilkins Px7 S3:**
The Bowers & Wilkins Px7 S3 are premium over-ears. They're more comfortable than the Sony WH-1000XM6 and have a better battery life, but they don't block out as much noise across the entire frequency range, and their mic's quality isn't as good.
[See our review](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/bowers-wilkins/px7-s3)

The Noble FoKus Apollo are great if you have a larger budget and won't compromise on mic quality. They're equipped with both a built-in and a detachable boom mic, which render your voice naturally. They're comfortable too, with a 46-hour battery life. While their ANC is outstanding, it's not quite up to par with the Sony WH-1000XM6, and they come at a much heftier price.

- **Sennheiser MOMENTUM True Wireless 4:**
The Sennheiser MOMENTUM True Wireless 4 are a decent alternative to the Sony WF-1000XM6 if you're looking for slightly more affordable earbuds that will work well for the office. The Sennheiser just about match the Sony for mic performance, but their noise isolation is a step below our pick's. Still, they can often be found on deep discount.
[See our review](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/sennheiser/momentum-true-wireless-4)
- **Devialet Gemini II True Wireless:**
The Devialet Gemini II True Wireless are premium in-ears with an outstanding noise isolation performance similar to the Sony WF-1000XM6. They're comfortable and well-built, with an integrated mic that has great noise handling. That said, they last less than five hours off a single charge, which is much shorter than the Sony's eight.

## 🧠 Why Noise-Canceling Headphones Matter for Developers

As a developer, deep focus is everything. Whether you’re debugging a tricky SwiftUI layout or trying to ship your latest app MVP in 7 days, distractions can kill your flow.

That’s where **noise-canceling headphones** come in. Not only do they block out the world, but they also help you create a private workspace — even in a noisy café, coworking space, or a chaotic home office.

So, I tested and researched the best noise-canceling headphones tailored for developers. Here’s what stood out in 2025:

## 🎧 1\. Sony WH-1000XM5

- ✅ **Best Overall for Developers**
- 🔋 Battery Life: ~30 hours
- 🌐 Great for remote calls (best-in-class mic)
- 🎵 Amazing sound quality + strong ANC

**Why developers love it:** Lightweight for long sessions, perfect for calls, and pairs seamlessly across devices.

## 🎧 2\. Bose QuietComfort Ultra

- ✅ **Most Comfortable for Long Coding Sessions**
- 🔋 Battery Life: ~24 hours
- 😌 Ultra-soft ear cushions
- 🧘 Best for focused solo work (white noise, ambient modes)

**Perfect for:** Remote devs who wear headphones all day long.

## 🎧 3\. AirPods Pro (2nd Gen)

- ✅ **Best for Apple Ecosystem Users**
- 🔋 Battery Life: ~6 hours (plus charging case)
- 🍏 Seamless switching between iPhone, iPad, Mac
- 🎧 Surprisingly strong ANC for in-ear buds

**Use case:** Ideal for iOS/macOS devs deep in the Apple ecosystem.

## 🎧 4\. Anker Soundcore Space One

- ✅ **Best Budget Noise-Canceling Headphones**
- 💸 ~Half the price of Sony/Bose
- 🔋 Battery Life: ~40 hours
