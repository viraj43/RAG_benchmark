Title: What is -2^2? - Math Central
**Key Points:** With (-2)2 the parentheses tell you to consider -2 as a single quantity and square it so (-2)2 = (-2) × (-2) = 4. Please note that this is not really a question (or answer) about arithmetic; it is about the convention that the second expression shall be interpreted as -(2^2). That said, now that the convention is established, we all need to follow it; it's like driving on the right (or left) side of the road.

Hi Alex, When I look at -22 I see two operations, a negation and a power (square). The convention in mathematical expressions is that powers are performed before negations (also before additions, multiplications and divisions) so to evaluate -22 you first square the 2 to get 4 and then apply the negation to arrive at -4. With (-2)2 the parentheses tell you to consider -2 as a single quantity and square it so (-2)2 = (-2) × (-2) = 4. I hope this helps,
Penny   It's -4. Please note that this is not really a question (or answer) about arithmetic; it is about the convention that the second expression shall be interpreted as -(2^2). There is no deep mathematical reason for this; it has just been found to be more convenient. That said, now that the convention is established, we all need to follow it; it's like driving on the right (or left) side of the road. Good hunting!
