Title: Weather in San Francisco - Climate, Weather & When to Visit
The water that surrounds San Francisco regulates its climate, meaning that the temperature does not change that much between seasons. Regardless of when you visit, it is likely that the city will be covered by its characteristic fog, formed by the combination of the cold ocean air and the heat of the Californian peninsula. San Francisco has a Mediterranean climate, with wet winters and dry summers. Summer in San Francisco is not overwhelming, as although temperatures rise, it is not extremely warm and temperatures drop at night. We recommend bringing light, but warm clothing as temperatures generally range between 50 and 70º F (10 and 21ºC). Winters in San Francisco tend to be cold, particularly in January and February when temperatures drop to as low as 41ºF (5ºC), with highs of 57ºF (14ºC).

You are here: Home --> North America --> United States --> San Francisco See the climate normals Weather by month The city of San Francisco is indicated by a red star. Every so often, however, hot air from the desert manages to reach the city, so much so that the temperature can reach 35/38 °C (95/100 °F) from May to October. Anyway, these periods of hot weather are very rare (in fact, they may even not occur for a whole year), they do not last long (just one or two days), and the heat is felt only during the day, while it is cooled by the ocean breeze during the evening. The best time to visit San Francisco is the summer, from June to September, since it is the driest and warmest season of the year. However, you have to take into account cool temperatures, and sometimes even a little cold, at night and in the early morning, as well as morning fogs, so it's better to bring a jacket and a sweater. Usually, in the months of April, May and October, the weather is good as well, in fact, it's often mild and sunny, even though there's a greater chance of rain; in addition, it can be cold at night, especially in April. The climate of San Francisco can be defined as "Mediterranean with cool summers". In fact, both the winter temperatures and the rain pattern remind of the Mediterranean climate, but summer is unusually cool because of the cold current that flows along the coast of California and is able to cause fog in San Francisco Bay, and to function as a natural air conditioner for the city and the surrounding areas.
