# 10 Best Beaches in the San Francisco Bay Area

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/cuppajyo_sanfrancisco_california_style_fashionblogger_lifestyle_travelblogger_carmel_beach_swimwear_sundress_beachbrella_summer_2.jpg?resize=750%2C938&ssl=1)

It goes without saying that I am a total beach bum at heart. While it still remains a life goal to travel the world one beach at a time, I do love exploring new local beaches whenever I am back in the Bay Area. Sadly Norcal beaches always get the bad rep of being the “not so pretty/cold beaches” compared to its prettier cousin – the quintessential sunny Socal beaches. But having explored a bunch I guarantee that you will find a beach you love here in beautiful Norcal too; there’s so much beauty in a dark moody beach too dont you think?! None of these beaches are quite swimmable and are mostly considered dangerous to be in the water, so please exercise caution in and around the water. But my fave activity to do in these beaches if not swim – get my toes sandy of course! Over the past couple of years Anush and I have explored a ton of local beaches and safe to say that now we definitely have our top faves whenever we want to get a dose of Vitamin Sea. So without further adieu here is my list of the 10 Best Beaches in the San Francisco Bay Area (going from North to South) :

**1 ) [Black sand beach in Marin County](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/black-sands-beach-marin-headlands/)** : Did you know there is a black sand beach here in SF? This gorgeous beach is located in the Marin headlands, which is a stunning drive by itself. On a clear day, you will see stunning views of the Golden Gate and the bay from the top of Marin Headlands.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/cuppajyo-sanfrancisco-fashion-lifestyle-blogger-black-sand-beach-valentines-day-2.jpeg?resize=750%2C1125&ssl=1)

Black sand beach

**2 ) [Baker Beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/baker-beach-san-francisco/) and [China Beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/china-beach/)** : Listing these two SF beaches side by side as I love both of them for the spectacular view they have of Golden Gate Bridge! Great for that perfect tourist postcard photo opp!

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/cuppajyo-sanfrancisco-fashion-lifestyle-blogger-baker-beach-feelthepiece-casual-knit-5-1024x1536.jpg?resize=750%2C1125&ssl=1)

Baker Beach

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/cuppajyo_sanfrancisco_fashion_lifestyle_blogger_frankies_bikinis_coverup_swimwear_palapaloungeswimwear_swimseason_8.jpeg?resize=750%2C500&ssl=1)

China Beach

**3) [Grey Whale State Cove](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/gray-whale-cove-state-beach/)** :This beach is a little south of Pacifica, we recently discovered this hidden gem last fall and we cant wait to go back! Its usually quite empty and the views are simply breathtaking! As the name implies, this beach is a also good spot to see migrating whales in season.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/DSC3245.jpg?resize=750%2C501&ssl=1)

Grey whale state cove

**4 ) [Montara Beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/montara-state-beach/)** : Located right next door to Grey whale state cove, Montara beach has the prettiest colored water, smooth sands (IMO wayy better than Pacifica!) and spectacular views of the Pacific. Its a wide beach that is great for spending the afternoon or walking along the long sandy stretch.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/jyoshankar_styleblogger_travelblogger_moodyblooms_fallflorals_flynnskye_7.jpg?resize=750%2C938&ssl=1)

**5) [Poplar beach, Half Moon Bay](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/poplar-beach/)** : Without a doubt this is **our personal FAVE** and the beach we have probably visited the most no of times here in the Bay. Tons of reasons why we love it –

- easy/cheap parking
- not so difficult climb down to the actual beach (compared to other beaches in Half Moon Bay)
- hugee beach so even if its crowded you are sure to find a secluded spot
- the sand is so pretty and soft
- dog friendly beach (for those of you who want to bring your pooch friends)
- close proximity to the town of Half Moon Bay
- Although there are no facilities on this beach and the water is far from swimmable, there are other fun activities you can do like – enjoy the magnificent cliff walk along the bluffs or even horseback riding on this beach!

All in all this is an ideal Norcal beach for spending the day, I highly recommend it!

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/jyoshankar-styleblogger-travelblogger-bluebreezybeachy-toripraverswimwear-5.jpg?resize=750%2C938&ssl=1)

Poplar beach

**6) [Cowell State Ranch , Half Moon Bay](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/cowell-ranch-beach/)** : We love love Cowell State not only for the beautiful beach but also the picturesque half-mile walk from the entrance to the lookout point. At this point both the north and south beaches unfold for majestic views. The south beach is off limits during harbor seal calving season, and you are sure to see tons of seals here! There is public access to the north beach along a steep staircase. This beach is usually pretty secluded and is simply so so stunning. Many times Anush and I have been pretty much some of the only few people on the beach making it such a memorable experience.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/03/jyoshankar_styleblogger_bloomingdales-swimwear-resortwear-coverup-pilyq-pitusa-9.jpg?resize=750%2C938&ssl=1)

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/cuppajyo-sanfrancisco-fashion-lifestyle-travel-blogger-vitaminaswim-palapaloungebeachwear-sporty-swimwear-6.jpg?resize=750%2C1123&ssl=1)

Top 2 pics : Cowell State Ranch

**7) [Beach at Ritz Carlton Half Moon Bay](http://www.ritzcarlton.com/en/hotels/california/half-moon-bay)** : This beach at the end of the bluff walk at the stunning Ritz Carlton in Half Moon Bay is a great place to have a beach picnic or bbq or even just relax after a fab brunch at Navio at the Ritz (the [Saturday’s farmers market brunch](https://www.instagram.com/p/Bj09xtClFaU/?taken-by=jyo_shankar) is our absolute favee – highly recommend it!)

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/07/cuppajyo_styleblogger_fashionblogger_travelblogger_sanfranciscoblogger_temptationpositano_whiteset_linen_beachstyle_resortstyle_summeroutfits_7.jpg?resize=750%2C1123&ssl=1)

Ritz, Half moon Bay

**8) [Davenport Landing Beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/davenport-landing-beach/)** : Davenport is one of our favorite sleepy little Norcal beach towns. Its literally a couple of streets that make up this town along highway 1. While most people go to the main Davenport beach, our fave is this hidden gem called Davenport Landing Beach, especially in the spring – for the prettiest blooms and best weather. Its also super popular among the surfers.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/cuppajyo-sanfrancisco-lifestyle-fashion-blogger-seagypsies-ancientgreeksandals-solescapes-springstyle-travel-scottcreekbeach-5.jpg?resize=750%2C501&ssl=1)

Davenport Landing

**9) [Shark Fin Cove, Santa Cruz](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g32282-d10598228-Reviews-Shark_Fin_Cove-Davenport_California.html)** : I generally do not go to beaches in Santa Cruz that often cos they are usually super windy and cold (even when I have been to them in the peak of summer)! But the ones along highway 1 are crazy gorgeous so definitely worth a visit. My personal faves are [Bonny Doon beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/bonny-doon-beach/), [Panther beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/panther-beach/) and Shark Fin Cove. I would highly recommend driving down this stretch during early spring when the wildflowers are blooming, its a sight to behold! Shark Fin Cove is a tiny stretch of beach but makes for the best photo opps – both in day and night (if you are into night photography!).

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/DSC9033.jpeg?resize=750%2C1123&ssl=1)

Shark Fin Cove

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/cuppajyo_styleblogger_fashionblogger_travelblogger_sanfrancisco_bayarea_santacruz_highway1_springblooms_redcarterswim_giveaway_capwell_wildflowers_8.jpg?resize=750%2C501&ssl=1)

Wildflowers along highway 1 in Santa Cruz (April-May time)

**10) [Carmel City Beach](https://www.californiabeaches.com/beach/carmel-city-beach/)** – This beach handsdown wins for the prettiest beach in Norcal. On a sunny day one will certainly mistake this place for a tropical beach haha cos the sand is SO white and the water is such a pretty blue! Located in the heart of charming little Carmel this is a great stopover while on a little weekend getaway to Carmel or Big Sur.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jyoshankar.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/cuppajyo_sanfrancisco_california_style_fashionblogger_lifestyle_travelblogger_carmel_beach_swimwear_sundress_beachbrella_summer_6.jpg?resize=750%2C1123&ssl=1)

Carmel City beach

Hope this list helps you plan your next beach day here in the Bay Area! What are your personal faves? xoxo

# My 5 Favorite Beaches in San Francisco

[travel](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/travel)[san francisco](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/san+francisco)[california](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/california)

Jun 5

Written By [Sarah Murawski](https://sarowly.me/blog?author=5b68bdb9a4f00203c5e15718)

San Francisco is located on a peninsula between the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco Bay. Thanks to that fantastic location, it has so many picturesque beaches all over the city. From long and sandy beaches to narrow, rocky coves, everyonce can find something suitable. In today’s post, I put together a list of my 5 favoraite beaches in San Francisco, that I often go to on a sunny and warm day!

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/012677f6-e2a6-48cf-9323-875fc84724a1/san+francisco+top+beaches.jpg)

## Baker Beach

**Baker Beach** is the most famous beach in San Francisco, offering picturesque views **over the Golden Gate Bridge and Marin Headlands**. It’s located in between the Presidio and the Cliffside neighborhoods. The beach is very long and sandy, but unfortunately due to rip currents, the water is unsafe for swimming. On top of that, the water is very cold. However, it’s a good place for hiking, sunbathing or picknicking. In the summer, it’s also possible to see dolphines or whales from Baker Beach. I often go there to take photos or just relax and admire the sunset!

TIP: At the north side of the beach, you may see some nudists, because the beach is clothing optional, so be prepared for that. I highly recommend going to Baker Beach for sunset, the views are spectacular!

How to get there? The best and easiest way is by car (there are two free parking lots), but you can also take the Muni bus from the city center, which goes directly to the beach (line #29)

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/410bf9f9-e299-435d-a83f-e3f900d3d1cc/baker+beach+san+francisco.jpg)

## Marshall Beach

**Marshall Beach** is a hidden gem and a smaller version of Baker Beach. It’s also more narrow and it has lots of big rocks. This beach offers the best views off the Golden Gate Bridge, because it’s located right next to it, further up north from Baker Beach (this is why the view is even closer). You can take a walk during low tide from Marshall Beach towards the bridge, but it’s a pretty adventurous walk. To get to Marshall Beach, you need to start your hike at the trailhead located at **the northern end of Batteries to Bluffs Trail**(it’s located near Golden Gate Overlook, about 5 minutes walk from the parking lot, you can find the exact location below). Halfway there’s a detour branching off the main trail, that goes down to Marshall Beach (you need to take a right turn). The trail itself is well-maintened, it’s a combination of wooden stairs and dirt paths. It’s easy and it’s **0,5 mile long**.

TIP: The best time to visit beach is during low tide, because it’s pretty narrow with lots of rocks, so it’s not very walkable. I recommend watching sunset there.

How to get there? The easiest way is by the car, you can leave it at the Golden Gate Overlook parking lot. It’s only 5 minutes walk to reach the trailhead. If you decide to go by bus, it’s by line #27 or #28.

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/50566620-1f6b-42db-ab9b-9631d5b2e4ca/marshall+beach+hike.jpg)

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/de27d75e-0b59-491e-861d-0d3f0a2e0024/marshall+beach+san+francisco.jpg)

## Black Sands Beach

**Black Sands Beach** is a beautiful, black beach loceted in Marin Headlands (Marin County), about 30 minutes drive up north from San Francisco. It reminds me of Hawaii, because of its dark and black pebbels, which look like black sand. The beach features lots of rocks and it’s surrounded by big cliffs, that’s why it feels so private, but at the same time it’s pretty empty and open. Black Sands Beach is a hidden gem, only locals know about. You can find there “shaka rock”, which give me all the surfing vibes!

TIP: Visit Black Sands Beach during **your day trip to Marin Headlands,** since there is lots of beautiful spots nearby. You can also stop at Battery Spencer on your way.

How to get there? I highly recommend a car to get there, it may be hard with Uber/Lyft. The parking lot is not obvious, it’s very small and located off the Conzelman Road, which turns into a one-way road, so don’t miss the entrance! Otherwise, you would need to go around. It’s 10 minutes walk from the parking lot to the beach itself, but it’s pretty easy.

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/87b0a9c2-3208-494b-8905-e86624566a25/black+sands+beach+marin+headlands.jpg)

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/58076f9f-cab0-4c32-98a5-921e342d3d6d/black+sands+beach+sarowly.jpg)

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1938110f-61fb-45e5-86a6-a95dea4c6e0c/sarowly+black+sands+beach+sunset.jpg)

## Crissy Field Beach

**Crissy Field** is a long and sandy, dog-friendly beach located in the northern part of the city, inside the Bay. If offers beautiful views of the Golden Gate Bridge and Sausalito. The name Crissy Field comes from **the large green fields behind the beach**.  It has lots of walking trails and a long promenade, which make it easily-accesible and walking friendly. Crissy Field is a flat beach, and very windy, it’s popular among kite-surfers and wind-surfers. The beach is perfect for a walk or hike, because it’s very long. I usually start my hike at East Beach at Crissy Field and walk up towards Fort Point at Golden Gate Bridge, the views are picturesque. Crissy Field Beach is perfect for a picnic, since you may find there lots of picnic areas (near East Beach and Fort Point).

TIP: Crissy Field is a great beach for swimming on a warm day, because there are no rip currents, like on Baker or Marshall’s Beach. Check out the Warming Hut Cafe and Torpedo Wharf.

How to get there? I recommend a car, you can park at East Beach parking lot, which is free. Also, the Muni Bus #30 stops at Crissy Field.

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/5ba6c39a-ea47-4b8e-80b6-34e5379edb3e/crissy+field+beach+san+francisco.jpg)

## Mile Rock Beach

**Mile Rock Beach** is a small cove located along Lands End Trail. It has a beautiful, rugged coastline and it’s very wild, surrounded by forests. It requires a long trail walk to get the the beach. The easiest way is to start at the visitor center (Lands End Lookout Visitor Center), near Sutro Baths.

TIP: At hide tide the beach can get very narrow, but when it’s out, you can explore it! I recommend checking out this beach when hiking Lands End Trail

How to get there? Leave your car at 680 Point Lobos Avenue (Lands End Visitor Center) and hike for about 20-30 minutes.

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1658cd04-9e55-4ec4-88aa-2b5b4c3d9cc4/mile+rock+beach+lands+end.jpg)

![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/93fcb69d-cc39-426a-bcda-a27e113b5e01/mile+rock+beach+lands+end+2.jpg)

Title: A Quick Guide to San Francisco Beaches - HI USA
[Skip to main content](https://www.hiusa.org/blog/travel-guides/san-francisco-beach-guide#site-main)

_Updated August 2025_

## _In a Nutshell_

_Don’t let San Francisco’s reputation for fog fool you—it’s also a surprisingly great beach town with sandy shores along both the Pacific Ocean and the Bay. In this local guide, I’ll share my favorite spots, from bonfire-friendly **Ocean Beach** to iconic **Baker Beach** with its Golden Gate Bridge views. I’ll also show you how to find a quiet cove at **China Beach**, enjoy calm waters at **Crissy Field**, and explore the dog-friendly dunes of **Fort Funston**. For the best beach weather, be sure to visit during San Francisco’s “summer” in the fall._

\\_\\_\\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

You may have heard San Francisco called “the City by the Bay” before, but chances are you’ve never thought of it as a beach town. The city’s edges, though, run up against the San Francisco Bay and the Pacific Ocean, where sandy stretches of beach await.

Conventional wisdom is that it’s always foggy by the beach in San Francisco, but the city’s weather patterns and microclimates allow for exceptions to this rule. Particularly hot SF days often result in great beach weather, and visitors in the fall (a.k.a. “San Francisco summer,” and [the best time of the year to visit](https://www.hiusa.org/blog/travel-guides/why-visit-san-francisco-in-fall)) may be surprised at the fog-free views and sunny conditions out at the beach.

Ready for a beach day but not sure where to go? Here’s our insider guide to some of the best beaches in San Francisco.

## Ocean Beach

![The Great Highway in San Francisco, seen at sunset, is closed to vehicle traffic on weekends and is filled with pedestrians and cyclists.](https://www.hiusa.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/great-highway-ocean-beach-san-francisco-quandt-640x360-min.jpg)

Probably the best known of San Francisco’s beaches, Ocean Beach is a 3.5-mile-long stretch of sand right on the edge of the city. It’s a popular spot for picnics, leisurely walks, volleyball games, nighttime bonfires, and the occasional silent disco. Out on the often-massive waves of the Pacific you’ll spot at least a few advanced surfers and countless sea birds; part of the beach itself is a protected nesting area for the tiny and adorable snowy plover.

Separating the beach from the rest of the city is [Sunset Dunes](https://sunsetdunes.org/visit), a huge new park that was recently converted from a two miles of sea-level highway. It’s a great spot for a run, walk, or bike ride, and has an ever-growing lineup of public art installations to take in.

Ocean Beach is bordered on the east by Golden Gate Park, as well as the Outer Sunset and Outer Richmond neighborhoods. Both neighborhoods have laid-back surfer town vibes, cute shops, and some great places to eat and drink. In the Outer Sunset, grab a sandwich at Java Beach, a vegan breakfast or lunch at Judahlicious, upscale California cuisine at Outerlands, a glass of local beer or wine at Woods Outbound, or a draft beer and live music at the Riptide.

In the Outer Richmond, stop by Andytown coffee for their signature Snowy Plover. There’s a large Safeway supermarket nearby where you can grab sandwiches, picnic supplies, firewood, sunscreen, and anything else you might need for a beach day.

## Baker Beach

![Baker Beach in San Francisco has stunning views of the Golden Gate Bridge.](https://www.hiusa.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/baker-beach-san-francisco-hannes-richter-PBaJUyoihtQ-unsplash-640x360-min.jpg)

If you want your beach photos to have the Golden Gate Bridge as a backdrop, Baker Beach is a solid option. Smaller and more secluded than Ocean Beach, Baker takes some effort to get to, but its close-up views of the bridge make it well worth it. The beach connects to the Presidio via hiking trail, and a combination of bus and walking will get you there from [HI San Francisco Fisherman’s Wharf hostel](https://www.hiusa.org/find-hostels/california/san-francisco-building240-fortmason) or [HI San Francisco Downtown hostel](https://www.hiusa.org/find-hostels/california/san-francisco-312-mason-street) in under an hour, though most people arrive by car (or ride share).

Most of Baker Beach puts off a pretty family-friendly vibe: you’re likely to see plenty of sandy picnics, kids splashing in the waves, and dogs chasing their balls. The northern end of the beach is an unofficial “clothing optional” section, so choose your spot to sunbathe accordingly.

Because Baker Beach is more isolated than Ocean Beach, you’ll want to pick up food or other supplies before you head out there.

## China Beach

![A view of China Beach, a small crescent of sand with a view of the golden gate bridge](https://www.hiusa.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/china-beach-san-francisco-giorgio-trovato-ME9rQdPOW_I-unsplash-640x360-min.jpg)

Though it’s just next door to Baker Beach, you’ll have to hike up into the posh Sea Cliff neighborhood and back down in order to get to China Beach. This little crescent of shore on the bay is better sheltered from the wind than most of the city’s other beaches, and is too small for toddlers and dogs to run around, so it offers a more chill alternative to Baker Beach while still giving you a peek of the Golden Gate.

## Crissy Field

![An overhead view of Crissy Field in San Francisco shows a stretch of sandy beach along the bay, with the San Francisco city skyline in the background.](https://www.hiusa.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Crissy-field-san-francisco-quandt-640x360-min.jpg)

A beautiful 30-minute walk (or a 10-minute bike ride) along the edge of the city will get you from [HI San Francisco Fisherman’s Wharf hostel](https://www.hiusa.org/find-hostels/california/san-francisco-building240-fortmason) to the postcard-perfect beaches of Crissy Field. Because Crissy Field borders the San Francisco Bay rather than the Pacific Ocean, the water’s much calmer here, and you’ll see at least a few people (and dogs) going for an icy dip. Keep heading west and you’ll hit Golden Gate Beach, which gets you right up next to – you guessed it – the Golden Gate Bridge.

## Fort Funston

![A path leads down green cliffs to the beach at Fort Funston in San Francisco.](https://www.hiusa.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/fort-funston-san-francisco-quandt-640x360-min.jpg)

The Happy Place of every pup in San Francisco, the long off-leash beach at Fort Funston is a dog-lover’s dream. It’s also wildly scenic, backed by enormous sandstone cliffs covered in green ice plant. Making for more than just a great view, those cliffs serve as a launching and landing point for hang gliders and paragliders; when the weather’s just right you’ll see people floating above you in the sky while the dogs enjoy the beach below.
