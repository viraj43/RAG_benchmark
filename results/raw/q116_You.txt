Title: Meditation and Mindfulness: Effectiveness and Safety | NCCIH
Among those responders who practiced mindfulness meditation exclusively, 73 percent reported that they meditated for their general wellness and to prevent diseases, and most of them (approximately 92 percent) reported that they meditated to relax or reduce stress. In more than half of the responses, a desire for better sleep was a reason for practicing mindfulness meditation. Meditation and mindfulness practices may have a variety of health benefits and may help people improve the quality of their lives. Few high-quality studies have examined the effects of meditation and mindfulness on blood pressure. According to a 2017 statement from the American Heart Association, the practice of meditation may have a possible benefit, but its specific effects on blood pressure have not been determined. However, most of the participants were women with breast cancer, so the effects may not be similar for other populations or other types of cancer. Studies have suggested possible benefits of meditation and mindfulness programs for losing weight and managing eating behaviors.
