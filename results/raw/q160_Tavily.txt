## 5 Popular Colleges in the San Francisco Bay Area

In terms of enrollment, the five colleges we feature below educate the most students in the Bay Area. Together, these universities had a total student enrollment of over 125,000 students in the 2021-22 school year. Read on to learn more about these five colleges in the Bay Area.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/highereducation/images/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1689376793/BestColleges.com/campanile-tower-uc-berkeley-campus/campanile-tower-uc-berkeley-campus.jpg)

### 1. [University of California, Berkeley](/schools/university-of-california/berkeley/)

With a 150-history in the Bay Area, [Berkeley is a leading public research](https://www.berkeley.edu/) university and the birthplace of the Free Speech Movement. Researchers at Berkeley have made outstanding contributions to earthquake science, malaria treatment, and robotic legs.

The university features 14 schools and colleges with 80 interdisciplinary research units and more than 350 academic programs. Pursuing a degree at Berkeley offers the opportunity to study under world-renowned faculty, including 26 Nobel Prize laureates. Students at Berkeley paid an average annual net price of $19,257 in the 2021-22 school year.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/highereducation/images/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1695065573/BestColleges.com/stanford-university-aerial-view/stanford-university-aerial-view.jpg)

### 2. [Stanford University](/schools/stanford-university/)

Renowned for academic excellence, [Stanford University](https://www.stanford.edu/) routinely ranks as one of the nation’s best colleges. About 8,000 undergraduates choose from 67 programs at the university. Stanford’s catalog includes traditional degrees such as biology, computer science, psychology, and engineering.

Stanford is one of the best colleges near San Francisco for graduate school with about 200 fields of study. The school’s Silicon Valley location gives students access to job and internship opportunities. In the 2021-22 school year, Stanford charged an average annual price of $18,279.

### 3. [San Francisco State University](/schools/san-francisco-state-university/)

Established in 1899 as a two-year institution for teachers, SF State now offers 77 bachelor’s degrees, 63 master’s programs, and 57 doctoral programs. The most popular schools include the Lam Family College of Business, Health and Social Sciences, and Science and Engineering which awarded 6,488 degrees in 2021-2022.

You can study on the 144-acre campus at Lake Merced or choose from 23 online programs offered through the College of Professional and Global Education. [SF State](https://www.sfsu.edu/) charged an average annual price of $13,267 in 2021-22.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/highereducation/images/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1695065983/BestColleges.com/san-jose-state-university-building/san-jose-state-university-building.jpg)

### 4. [San Jose State University](/schools/san-jose-state-university/)

SJSU offers a close-knit learning community in Silicon Valley. You can pursue undergraduate and graduate degrees in more than 250 areas of study. [San Jose State University](https://www.sjsu.edu/) also offers baccalaureate-level teacher preparation in subjects such as French, environmental studies, and kinesiology.

SJSU is also one of the best colleges near San Francisco for engineering, with 40 majors, minors, and graduate degrees offered in the subject. Graduate programs include nursing, marine science, and urban planning. Students at SJSU paid an average annual net price of $14,219 in the 2021-22 school year.

### 5. [California State University, East Bay](/schools/california-state-university/east-bay/)

Cal State East Bay, one of the [best Cal State Schools,](/united-states/california/cal-state-schools/) opened in 1959 in Hayward, California. Of the 13,141 undergraduates, 62% identify as first-generation college students. Roughly 896 faculty members work at Cal State East Bay, where students study some 130 different fields.

The university’s most popular majors include business administration, psychology, health sciences, biological sciences, and computer science. The average annual net price to attend Cal State, East Bay was $11,464 in the 2021-22 school year.

## How Much Is College in San Francisco?

What it costs to study in California depends on your residency status and where you go to college. If you qualify as a California resident, you can pay [in-state tuition, and avoid higher out-of-state](/united-states/california/in-state-vs-out-of-state-tuition/) tuition rates.

For in-state students, public four-year colleges in San Francisco charged an average annual net price of $15,213 in 2022, while private colleges charged $37,919. California also participates in the Western Undergraduate Exchange program, which allows students from other member states to get tuition discounts.

## Scholarships for San Francisco Students

You can apply for various types of financial aid to help pay for college. When you don’t have the option of paying out-of-pocket, you can turn to scholarships. [California scholarships](/united-states/california/scholarships/) provide funding sources that you do not need to repay if you meet eligibility requirements.

### [UC Berkeley Achievement Award Program](https://alumni.berkeley.edu/get-involved/scholarships/the-achievement-award-program/)

The Achievement Award Program provides up to $6,000 to high-achieving and first-generation students at Berkeley who demonstrate a financial need. You can also qualify for a $1,000 stipend to purchase a laptop.

Incoming first-years and students transferring into Berkeley as juniors can apply. Awardees get invited to retreats and workshops and receive academic advising. The deadline is May 7.

### [CAC Educational Scholarship Foundation](https://www.calcollectors.net/foundation/)

Created in 2005, the California Association of Collectors Education Scholarship Foundation offers scholarships to high school juniors and seniors who plan to attend any accredited college in the next year.

The association announces three winners at its annual banquet. Recipients get either $1,500; $2,000; or $2,500. Applications open in January, and you must apply by June 30.

### [California Scholarship Federation](https://csf-cjsf.org/csf-awards/)

The California Scholarship Federation offers two scholarships to eligible high school nominees from local chapters. The Seymour Memorial Awards, with a deadline of February 18, provide 50 scholarships of $2,000 each to graduating high school seniors in California.

An additional five winners will get another $3,000. The Michelson Memorial Award offers a scholarship of $1,000 to eligible students who apply by December 22.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Colleges in San Francisco

### What colleges are in San Francisco?

San Francisco is home to a number of two- and four-year colleges and universities, including Stanford University, San Francisco State University, and UC Berkeley. At any of these colleges, you can earn a certificate or enroll in an undergraduate or graduate program.

### How many colleges are in San Francisco?

In San Francisco, as well as the surrounding suburbs, you’ll find 15 colleges and universities where you can seek degrees at any level.

However, there are just four colleges in San Francisco proper: San Francisco State University, the University of San Francisco, California College of the Arts, and Minerva University. The University of San Francisco, founded in 1855, stands as the City by the Bay’s oldest college.

### What is the biggest college in San Francisco?

In the San Francisco area, Berkeley reigns as the biggest college in terms of enrollment and campus size. The campus, with examples of neoclassical architecture, is spread across 1,232 acres and boasts a total student enrollment of 45,307.

# The Complete Guide to Touring San Francisco’s Top Universities

As a national hub of the country’s best culture, fine arts, restaurants, and iconic scenery, it’s no surprise that [San Francisco](https://gogocharters.com/san-francisco-charter-bus) and the surrounding Bay Area also boast some of the U.S.’s top colleges and universities.

Whether you’re pursuing undergraduate or graduate studies, a personal charter bus rental is the perfect way to keep your tour group organized. From small groups of friends to a class trip with your school, GOGO Charters will make sure everyone travels comfortably on your tour of the Bay Area’s best universities.

As you’re narrowing down your college choices, planning can quickly become overwhelming. We talked to a few universities to make your bus tour run smoothly. Keep reading below to find out how to make the most of your trip at each of these top schools:

## University of California, Berkeley

[![Sather Tower at the UC Berkeley campus](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/uc-berkeley.jpg)](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/uc-berkeley.jpg)

![Sather Tower at the UC Berkeley campus](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/uc-berkeley.jpg)

Known for its programs in social sciences, biological and biomedical sciences, engineering, and even English language and literature, [Berkeley](https://gogocharters.com/berkeley-charter-bus) is one of the top-preferred schools in the U.S.

Load into your [minibus](https://gogocharters.com/20-passenger-mini-bus) for a day trip over to this beautiful campus. If your party is greater than 10, you’ll need to [reserve a spot](https://campustours.berkeley.edu/visitor?cal=g) for a private tour two weeks in advance of your date. Or, if you want to take a self-guided tour, Berkeley provides [a few maps](http://visit.berkeley.edu/campus-tours/self-guided-tours/) of their must-see campus spots.

Parking can be a little tricky since buses are not allowed to park on campus during business hours (Monday-Friday, 7am-5pm). Outside of these hours, though, space is available at the Southwest Crescent, which can be entered at the intersection of Center Street and Oxford Street. Parallel parking is also available along the street by the crescent– just remember that you’ll need to pay per hour per space that your bus takes up.

During business hours, buses may load and unload at the Southwest Crescent, but they may not park. If you’re concerned about where to store your bus during the day, our reservation agents will seek out parking and staging areas after you make your reservation! Call us any time at **1-855-826-6770** for more information.

## University of California, San Francisco

Another branch of the state’s extensive University of California system, UC San Francisco is a top choice for many aspiring medical students. This university is entirely dedicated to health sciences and is home to the oldest medical school in the western United States.

UCSF includes [multiple locations](https://www.ucsf.edu/about/locations#main-locations) across the city, with their main campus located at Parnassus Heights. Since all campus locations are very urban with limited space, the school advises that private tour buses do not drive directly through campuses when they can avoid it, particularly at their Parnassus location. Private buses are not allowed to park on any campus, though they may quickly pick up or drop off passengers as long as there is enough room for cars to pass.

Tour bus parking is available through [San Francisco’s Municipal Transportation Agency](https://www.sfmta.com/getting-around/commercial-vehicles/tour-bus-information). Golden Gate Park’s areas 7, 8, are the closest bus parking areas to the main Parnassus campus, according to SFMTA’s Citywide [Tour Bus Loading and Parking Map](https://www.sfmta.com/maps/citywide-tour-bus-loading-and-parking-map).

To schedule a tour, contact your campus of choice to set up a time.

Due to limited space, we recommend keeping it small with an [18-passenger minibus](https://gogocharters.com/18-passenger-mini-bus) for your group tour of UCSF.

## University of San Francisco

[![a prominent building and statue on the University of San Francisco campus](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/university-of-san-francisco-1.jpg)](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/university-of-san-francisco-1.jpg)

![a prominent building and statue on the University of San Francisco campus](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/university-of-san-francisco-1.jpg)

Image Source: [Michael Fraley](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:University_of_San_Francisco_February_2021.jpg), [CC BY 2.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0), via Wikimedia Commons

Not to be confused with the University of California *at* San Francisco, the University of San Francisco is located in the heart of the city, with all of San Francisco’s best destinations, restaurants, landmarks, and more within about a 4-mile radius.

Group tours for 5 or more can be scheduled through the [university’s website](https://www.usfca.edu/admission/undergraduate/visit/group-visit). Private buses can drop off passengers 24 hours a day in the white zone in front of the War Memorial Gym, at 2335 Golden Gate Avenue.

Buses can park for the day at the nearby Ocean Beach lot, located at Great Highway and Balboa Street.

## Stanford University

[![a panoramic view of stanford's campus across a large lawn](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/stanford-university-1.jpg)](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/stanford-university-1.jpg)

![a panoramic view of stanford's campus across a large lawn](https://gogocharters.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/stanford-university-1.jpg)

Though it’s about a 45-minute drive from San Francisco, Stanford is still one of the top school choices for many bay-area students. With its close proximity to Silicon Valley and its reputation as a leader among science, engineering, and humanities education, Stanford is one of the most prestigious universities in the world.

Tour groups are allowed to visit Stanford as long as they follow their tourist visit policies. Groups are allowed to visit but must not disrupt daily activities. They must adhere to a few simple rules, such as refraining from roaming through private campus buildings, leaving trash on campus, or talking loudly or smoking in front of campus buildings.

You can [request access](https://transportation.stanford.edu/parking/find-tour-bus-parking/tour-buses) for your private bus prior to your visit to avoid ticketing, towing, or other fees. Bus loading zones are available on Lasuen Street. While bus access to the Oval is usually prohibited, your group can request authorization to access this historic campus location!

# Colleges & Universities on The San Francisco Peninsula

![Stand University Hoover Tower in Palo Alto](https://assets.simpleviewinc.com/simpleview/image/upload/c_fill,f_jpg,h_920,q_65,w_640/v1/clients/sanmateoca/Stanford_University_HooverTower_PaloAlto_SanMateoCounty_SiliconValley_25bf309d-9cfc-4877-8ece-a7a74504d654.jpg)

The San Francisco Bay Area is renowned for its prestigious colleges and universities. Whether you're a parent exploring options for your child, a prospective student seeking a vibrant academic environment, or a visiting faculty member looking for collaborative opportunities, this guide offers a glimpse into the unique campuses and surrounding areas.

## [Stanford University](https://www.thesanfranciscopeninsula.com/listing/stanford-university/4683/)

Situated in California's intellectually vibrant Bay Area, [Stanford University](https://www.thesanfranciscopeninsula.com/listing/stanford-university/4683/), established in 1885 by Senator Leland Stanford and his wife, Jane, is a testament to academic excellence and innovation.

Renowned for its entrepreneurial spirit, the institution is deeply influenced by its proximity to Silicon Valley and is one of the world's leading research universities. It offers a wide array of academic disciplines, excelling in areas from computer science, engineering, and human biology to advanced graduate studies in business, law, and medicine. It is also an athletics powerhouse, with 900 current student-athletes and a history of 128 national titles and 22 consecutive Learfield Sports Directors’ Cups. The campus is a blend of striking architecture and natural beauty with landmarks like the iconic Stanford Memorial Church, the Rodin Sculpture Garden, and the panoramic views of the Bay Area from Hoover Tower.

Self-guided and interactive tours are available, and guided tours for groups of prospective undergraduate students are available by reservation on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays during the academic quarter.

WHERE TO STAY–Westin Palo Alto, Sheraton Palo Alto, The Cardinal Hotel, The Stanford Park Hotel, and Crowne Plaza Palo Alto.

## About the University of San Francisco

Basic information and contact details for the University of San Francisco

![institution](/academic-ui-assets/dsc/svg/flat/institution.svg?4)

With the motto ‘Change the World from Here’, the University of San Francisco (USF) is a Catholic university based in San Francisco, North California.

Its scenic 55-acre campus is situated on a hill next to the famous Golden Gate Park, in downtown San Francisco, overlooking the Pacific Ocean.

Founded by Jesuits in the mid nineteenth century, the University of San Francisco was the city’s first university. When the inaugural college, known as St. Ignatius Academy, opened its doors in the autumn of 1855, it was to a cohort of only three students. By 1858, that number has grown to 65.

It wasn’t until the college’s Diamond Jubilee in 1930 that the institution was renamed to the University of San Francisco.

Today, the university welcomes students of every faith and no faith, and its approach to learning continues to be defined by Jesuit tradition. Its unique style of education is inspired by ‘Cura personalis’, which means ‘care of the whole person’, where intellect is only one element of a student’s development.

It currently offers more than 100 undergraduate and graduate degree programmes.

USF students are encouraged to extend their education beyond the classroom via internships and ‘service learning’ where they participate in some of the city’s social and community projects.

With its location close to Silicon Valley, USF also emphasises a global outlook and entrepreneurial ethos. The magazine *Forbes* has ranked USF within the top 25 most entrepreneurial universities in the US.

USF’s athletic teams are known as the Dons and their mascot, Don Francisco (named after the city’s first mayor) appears around campus wearing a signature mask and cape.

Its alumni include two Pulitzer prize winners, a former president of Peru and one of the founders of Starbucks coffee.
