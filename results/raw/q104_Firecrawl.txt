Title: 9 Of My Favorite Places In San Francisco
# [let's go places](https://sarahlvogt.com/)

a blog full of travel guides from my adventures near and far.

I’ve lived out in San Francisco for almost three years now. Three years in January to be exact! Which is crazy to me. I guess time flies when you’re having fun! But it’s true. I always tell people that moving out to San Francisco has been one of the best decisions I’ve ever made.

I’ve fallen in love with the city by the Bay.

_(And I finally understand what Journey was talking about!)_

If you’re planning a trip out to San Francisco soon, I have a few (less touristy) places that I think you should visit. They’re places that I keep going back to time and time again. And I feel as though they really give you a sense of this beautiful city.

## 9 Places To Check Out In San Francisco

### Palace of Fine Arts

If you want to find yourself in a fairytale, head to the Palace of Fine Arts. You’ll find a gorgeous arched monument that was built in 1915. It was constructed around a lagoon that reflects the rotunda when you catch it at the right angle.

![img_20161112_172119](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20161112_172119.jpg?resize=750%2C422&ssl=1)

There’s green space around the lagoon, so it’s a great spot to bring a picnic on a sunny afternoon. Make sure to stroll along with walkway around the lagoon as well. It will take you right into the rotunda, which is in itself a beautiful architectural sight.

![img_20161127_215935](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20161127_215935.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

### Fort Point

When I need to get out on a lazy afternoon, my favorite thing to do is walk down to Fort Point, all the way from my apartment. Which ends up being about an hour or so walk. You don’t have to do that, but you should certainly drive out to the West Bluff Picnic Area (out by Crissy Field). Then take the pathway out to Fort Point.

![img_20160208_084837](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160208_084837.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

![img_20160622_203820](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160622_203820.jpg?resize=750%2C938&ssl=1)

The views of the Golden Gate Bridge are amazing. And once you get out to the fort, you’ll be right under the bridge, which is kind of cool.

![img_20160619_214157](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160619_214157.jpg?resize=750%2C422&ssl=1)

![img_20160207_182741](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160207_182741.jpg?resize=750%2C938&ssl=1)

You can go into the fort (free of charge). So if you like history, definitely take some time to check out all the different areas of the fort. If history is not so much your thing, then head straight up to the top level. You’ll get panoramic views of the bay, and you might get lucky enough to watch a ship make its way into the bay from the sea. Watching it slowing cruise under the bridge is always kind of fun. It makes you wonder where in the world it’s coming from.

### Land’s End

If you’re looking for a hike near the city, Land’s End is your place. Start at Sutro Bath, which is quite a site in itself. You can go down the steps and enjoy the ruins of what used to be a bathhouse. Built in 1896, it had seven saltwater swimming pools. Yes, seven!

![img_20160315_203716](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160315_203716.jpg?resize=750%2C422&ssl=1)

![img_20150124_165306](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20150124_165306.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

Once you’re done exploring, take the Sutro Baths Upper Trail to the Lands End Trail. Then, ultimately, to the Coastal Trail. This easy hike will take you along the coast, with beautiful views and overlooks to stop and enjoy. Go as far as you like. Technically, you could make it all the way to the Golden Gate Bridge. If you’re up for it!

On your way back to your car, stop in at [Cliff House](http://www.cliffhouse.com/home/index.html) for a drink and great views of Ocean Beach. And of the ocean, of course.

### The Presidio

Another place is that fun to explore (or really just drive through) is the Presidio. Once a military fort, it’s now mostly used as a park.

There are all kinds of hiking trails here, covered by a canopy of pine, cypress and eucalyptus trees. However, my favorite part of the Presidio is exploring the old batteries. They’re situated throughout the park.

### Huntington Park

This park is near my apartment and I absolutely adore it. I’ll go hear on warm afternoons to read a book, write or just sit and enjoy the weather.

![img_20161126_143626](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20161126_143626.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

People living in the neighbor bring their dogs here. So that’s another perk of the park if you’re a dog lover like myself. I even got to bring Milo, my friend’s dog, here one weekend while I was dogsitting him. He loved it, of course.

![img_20161125_150848](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20161125_150848.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

The other thing I love about this park: It has two beautiful water fountains. A large fountain in the center of the park, and a smaller one off to the side. The large fountain is a copy of Rome’s Fontana della Tartarughe (Fountain of the Turtles). Which makes a lot of sense now!

### Battery Spencer

Apparently, I like batteries, because this is another one of my favorites places to visit. But not so much for exploring the battery as having an AMAZING view of the Golden Gate Bridge and San Francisco.

![IMG_20150206_090128.jpg](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20150206_090128.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

Located just on the other side of the bridge, it might be easier to drive up there (there’s a parking lot). But I hear that it’s also kind of fun to bike across the bridge. You’ll have to bike up a steep hill to get up to Battery Spencer, but the view is worth it.

### Sausalito

You’ll definitely want to go check out the charming little seaside town of Sausalito. To get there, you could drive you or bike across the Golden Gate Bridge. Which I hear is rather fun. But my favorite way of transportation is by ferry. You can great views of the bay, the city, and Alcatraz island.

![img_20160626_213659](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160626_213659.jpg?resize=750%2C638&ssl=1)

So what can you do? You can shop, grab a bite to eat (try [Sausalito Taco Shop](http://www.salsalitotacoshop.com/)) or get a drink (try [Barrel House Tavern](http://barrelhousetavern.com/)). You can grab an ice cream cone at [Lappert’s Ice Cream](http://www.lapperts.com/) on a hot day. Or just walk around. I mean, it does have a pretty nice view of San Francisco.

### Embarcadaro

Walking the Embarcadaro on a warm day is one of the best things. You can enjoy sculpture gardens and a great view of the Bay Bridge. You stop at one of its many pop-up bars for a drink, or you can enjoy yummy food in the [Ferry Building](http://www.ferrybuildingmarketplace.com/).

![img_20150321_161524](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20150321_161524.jpg?resize=750%2C750&ssl=1)

On Sunday’s there is a Farmer’s Market. So if you’re looking for fresh fruits, vegetables, or flowers, it’s the place to go.

### My Rooftop

Okay, so this is not a real option for you. I’m mentioning it, though, because I feel like I’ve been super fortunate. I have a beautiful view right from my apartment’s rooftop. Seriously, I go up there quite often. Sometimes to enjoy the sun on a warm day. But mostly to take photos of the amazing sunsets we get out here in San Francisco.

![img_20160212_205535](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160212_205535.jpg?resize=750%2C422&ssl=1)

![img_20160406_195711](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20160406_195711.jpg?resize=750%2C422&ssl=1)

![img_20161112_180357](https://i0.wp.com/sarahlvogt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/img_20161112_180357.jpg?resize=750%2C422&ssl=1)

If you’re looking for something similar, the neighborhood of Nob Hill is the place to go. You could try [Top of the Mark](http://www.topofthemark.com/). Their cocktails are expensive, but it has amazing panoramic views of the city and the bay.

* * *

And if you’re into the touristy stuff (sometimes you gotta, right?), you should:

- Eat clam chowder eat Fisherman’s Wharf
- Take the ferry to Alcatraz Island
- Hop on one of the city trolleys (or one of the city tour buses)
- Go shopping in Union Square
- Spend the afternoon in Dolores Park

Let’s go places,

Sarah

# The best viewpoints and photo spots in San Francisco

[california](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/california)[san francisco](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/san+francisco)[travel](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/travel)[usa](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/usa)[sfbayareadaytrips](https://sarowly.me/blog/category/sfbayareadaytrips)

Jul 31

Written By [Sarah Murawski](https://sarowly.me/blog?author=5b68bdb9a4f00203c5e15718)

San Francisco is such a charming city, and it also has wonderful viewpoints in different parts of it. Here is the list of my top 5 viewpoints in San Francisco. They are also my favourites locations/photo spots for sessions with my clients. Enjoy the list!

# Battery Spencer

Battery Spencer viewpoint located in Marin Headlands offers definitely the best views of the Golden Gate Bridge. The views from there are breathtaking and you can get the best light at sunrise. Highly recommend waking up early and get there just before sunrise, stunning views are guaranteed.

**How to get there?** Car/Uber or Bike is the best option. There is a free parking lot.

![goldengate-batteryspencer.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596227726367-G3N01KVJW1JZSAR9LWVK/goldengate-batteryspencer.jpg)

![batteryspencer-sunrise.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596228377872-R41CL2OR0BOH47YDNH46/batteryspencer-sunrise.jpg)

![batteryspencer-sunrise3.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596228483132-L21ML2K14M54AXG53DHC/batteryspencer-sunrise3.jpg)

# Golden Gate Overlook

Another best place to view the Golden Gate Bridge and its surroundings. It’s easy to get there from the city, you can even take a bus. Sunset is my favourite time of day to take pictures there, the light looks magical! Golden Gate Overlook is located on the northside of the bridge,vnext to the historic batteries. The viewpoint offers a dramatic sight-line through the two bridge towers. I love this spot!

**How to get there?** You can either hike here, bike or come by car. There is a free parking lot.

![goldengateoverlook4.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596229918851-20ADI0VTI9QPWP9G6CU1/goldengateoverlook4.jpg)

![goldengateoverlook-2.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596229454883-E8DF657I234RR5WC397W/goldengateoverlook-2.jpg)

![goldengateoverlook.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596229491990-ZX33JP78T3EHUDCRI06J/goldengateoverlook.jpg)

# Mori Point, Pacifica

Just a 30 minutes drive down the coast from the city of San Francisco separates us from dramatic, coastal views like these! Mori Point is my beloved ocean viewpoint in San Francisco. You can find there many hiking trails surrounded by wildflowers and green hills. This photo spot is perfect for golden hour and sunset. This place is located on the huge hill from there you can admire beautiful views of the coast, the city of Pacifica and some rocks.

**How to get there?** By car. There is a free, street parking. You can also take a bus to Pacifica and then hike from the city along the beach.

![pacifica-moripoint2.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596229619258-DH1YY3D3CZZE3TTDBOTP/pacifica-moripoint2.jpg)

![pacifica-sunset.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596230648163-K5UCU3MCTT3PEIY6SB47/pacifica-sunset.jpg)

![pacifica-moripoint.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596230525591-DNKQU188Z3Q7KTH9IHP9/pacifica-moripoint.jpg)

![pacifica-waves.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596230604250-TRO897MLRNQRHF7E0AGL/pacifica-waves.jpg)

# Lands End & Sutro Baths

Lands End is located at the northwestern corner of San Francisco. This spot and the hiking trail offer gorgeous views of the Golden Gate Bridge and the Pacific Usually it’s so windy and chilly over there, so better take a jacket with you! Next to Lands End, there are Sutro Baths and a small beach. The best option is to go on a hike or just do a part of it, so you can view the best landscapes!

**How to get there?** By car, by bus (number 38) or the Lands End hiking trail from Golden Gate Bridge.

![landsend1.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596230971926-7L2X4NOWAADCDFRR1HKY/landsend1.jpg)

![landsend4.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596231030267-XC2DIU8J58M7N9H45TF6/landsend4.jpg)

![landsend3.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596231227892-AIW057UGQ0TL1FH20FS6/landsend3.jpg)

# Bernal Heights

Bernal Heights park offers 360-degree panorama views of San Francisco. This park located on the hill but it’s easy to get there. On the top of it, you can find a swing that is really cute and photogenic! The best time to take pictures is during the golden hour and sunset. I might be windy and chilly there, better be prepared to wear some extra layers.

**How to get here?** By Uber or by bus (number 24). There is no parking spot, just a few spots on the street.

![bernal-heights.jpg](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5c7f5f60797f746a7d769cab/1596231855981-PB2714AOQHSZGAP1WWH9/bernal-heights.jpg)

# A Local's Guide to the Best Viewpoints in San Francisco

[San Francisco](https://venturebeyondthepicture.com/outdoor-adventures/category/San+Francisco)[Views](https://venturebeyondthepicture.com/outdoor-adventures/category/Views)[California](https://venturebeyondthepicture.com/outdoor-adventures/category/California)

Mar 4

Written By [Vanessa Richter](https://venturebeyondthepicture.com/outdoor-adventures?author=650e32064523a93ee1d3d715)

Thanks to San Francisco's hilly terrain, there is no shortage of **amazing viewpoints and parks with a view within (and just outside) the city**.

In this guide, I'll share **my favorite spots as a resident of SF**, all of which are entirely free to access.

The list is in no particular order; each of these parks and viewpoints possesses its **own charm** and **unique perspective** on various sights in the city:

_Would you like to see the best views of the [Golden Gate Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1063%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=nvEI4PY4PqhY42K1ad45h&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), downtown, or a little bit of everything? Would you prefer to stay for a while, maybe even have a picnic while soaking in the views, or are you more inclined to quickly snap a picture and move on?_

I will share with you what **makes each of these places special**, the **optimal time** (season/time of day) to visit, and the **best way** to get there.

## Ina Coolbrith Park

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

### Why to go there

**Ina Coolbrith Park** is a **tiny yet truly picturesque** park that offers one of the best views of downtown SF, including the [Bay Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a8921%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=X8SDQ_r-CzCN0L6mlClj5&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) (though not the [Golden Gate Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1063%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=bJ7xxPOqc9puT-Fs7Fh_A&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf))! The downtown **skyscrapers** are amazingly **close** from there. The park is laid out in terraces with magnificent plantings, creating a **small oasis** within the city.

If you're planning to visit the crooked part of **[Lombard Street](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D17256%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=kOvO3cTTxXfnS_BtjdM_Z&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, this park is just a 15-minute walk away. Additionally, it's conveniently close (a 10-minute walk) to one of my **favorite ice cream shops, Swensen’s**, and equally near [North Beach](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1072%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=8BorYUazYSp31YJlZ_QKm&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) (" **Little Italy**").

### How to get there

#### By foot

If you are coming from the **downtown area** around [Union Square](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1735%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=DWwh67CuXnrtYdPNaGOcD&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), you may consider a one-mile walk up to the park (takes about half an hour each way). You could combine it with exploring **[Chinatown](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1431%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=F7AbbnoyhwI6X1rZYZTWW&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, which adds only a little time to the trip. If you come from the **[Marina district](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1774%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=8o0MvHix47fe-7YaJrhE3&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, it is a similar distance (a bit shorter and mostly flat, though) to the park, so walking is perfectly possible if you want to.

#### By car

There are a **few street parking** spaces in the area, but in my opinion, looking for a place to park can be a hassle as it is in one of the more densely populated areas of San Francisco. It is definitely possible to park with your own car, but I would **recommend avoiding** this to save nerves.

#### By public transportation

This part of the city is **very well connected** to most neighborhoods, providing you with numerous options.

Moreover, there's a **cable car stop nearby**, offering a fantastic opportunity to incorporate a scenic ride to get there. While locals don't typically rely on cable cars for efficient transportation due to the considerable time it takes to travel with them, they are definetly **a lot of fun** to ride with and a **historic attraction** worth exploring. So, if you haven't had the opportunity to experience them, it's definitely something worth checking out!

### When to go

Beautiful in any season and the views are amazing both day and night, so whatever time fits your schedule is great!

## Twin Peaks

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

### Why to go there

If I had ranked this list, definitely, [Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=Zk7dObjynN7Hka9__QvFC&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) would be in **my top 3 favorite viewpoints**. It is just such a great place to have both **views of downtown** with the bay in the backgroundAND the **[Golden Gate Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1063%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=ghYS0SzIO_nAEkEBb18ye&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**. Even if this viewpoint does not get extremely close to these sights, it does offer a **fantastic overview**. It is also a great place to just stay for a little bit longer, sit on the wall and have something to eat/drink while soaking in the view.

### How to get there

If you don’t have a car, I personally would recommend combining it with exploring the [Castro](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1559%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=7zCKSOCA95atVD7C01oVp&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) and a little bit of the [Noe Valley](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D6096%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=HB9uYKoFglGDuCJQxHpAc&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) neighborhood.

#### By foot

If you start at the Castro Metro Station, you can make it a short & beautiful, but **rather steep hike** that takes about 45 minutes (without breaks) by walking up Castro Street until you reach 21st, 22nd or 23rd Street and then take a right turn into one of these, whatever seems most appealing to you. From there I’d recommend using a navigation app as there are some twists and turns because of the hilly nature of this area.

When walking up the hill from [Castro](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1559%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=ONkCbKPNMvm6Wwqwn4U_F&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) to [Noe Valley](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D6096%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=JGrdqOjLn1dajAuuTtfFy&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), you’ll already see a ton of amazing Victorian houses. There is such a big concentration of Victorian and Edwardian homes in this area. So, you’ll probably find yourself stopping a lot taking pictures and just admiring the beautiful architecture.

#### By car

You can take an Uber/Lyft or use your own car. There is a **parking lot** directly **at the viewpoint**, so if you have a car and limited time, this might be the best option. Beware though, that you really make sure to **lock your car** and that you **do not leave anything (visible) in it.** [Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=DEoKXhMj_ztG6V59nb099&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) is a prime target for car break-ins. Having said that, in my opinion, you don’t need to worry about safety here otherwise.

#### By public transportation

There are a couple of busses that drive up close to [Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=PT9c-XmPwUA-0Ml2GFv4d&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf). Check out [here](https://www.sfmta.com/neighborhoods/twin-peaks).

### When to go

[Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=2M8XZ9smuUOhrkEG4bRQ_&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) is **great to visit both during the day or at night**. If you worry that it may be lonely up there after the sun has set, don’t be: if the vision is good (and even if it isn’t in my experience), there will usually be a ton of people. As with many viewpoints on this list, it is prettier in **spring or even winter** when the **vegetation is lusher** than in summer or fall.

**_Twin Peaks Viewpoint at night_** _: You can clearly see the bright Market Street from a distance._

_On this day, we were fortunate that the_ **_fog_** _wasn't too thick. It_ **_hovered_** _around the city, but the wind created numerous moments when downtown was visible from the viewpoint._

_(Photo is zoomed in, so the view is closer as it appears to the naked eye.)_

**Beware of the fog** though — **especially in summer**, there may not be many days when you can actually see a lot from there. Even if the sky is clear downtown, that doesn’t necessarily mean it's the same closer to the ocean. The **fog usually gathers at [Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=ICK021U3bTDyf_OZh-c1A&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, making it cold and windy up there. Just take a look towards the tower on top of the hill. If you can see most of it, it should be fine.

## Bernal Heights Park

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

### Why to go there

The **Bernal Heights Park** is located in the namesake Bernal Heights neighborhood, just south of the Mission District. Therfore, it's a bit far from the usual tourist hotspots. However, if you're interested in exploring San Francisco's **residential side** with nevertheless amazing restaurants and cafes, a visit to Bernal Heights and its park is a great choice. You can easily **pair it** with an exploration of the nearby **[Mission District](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FSan-Francisco%2FSmall-Group-Mission-District-Food-Tour-San-Francisco%2Fd651-7812P13%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=lAPjWWzmrO8qzoeyNM0ZP&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, a Mexican neighborhood that, despite being adjacent, offers a very different experience.

But let’s talk about the park itself! Once you've made it up the hill, you will be rewarded with a breathtaking **360-degree panorama**, including views of San Francisco (the bay, the [Golden Gate Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1063%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=KkQrsqMWAKFwxJlodZ9Uy&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) & downtown), [San Bruno Mountain](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D64634%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=X9ITvYDC7UqPFUcDRJ1mO&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), and the hills of the East Bay.

When [Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=-rhTvUBB0MP-VqDdD8L1W&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) is shrouded in fog, there is usually still a great view from Bernal Heights Park.

### How to get there

#### By foot

If you're exploring the **[Mission District](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=108&marker=659389&p=3965&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.getyourguide.com%2Fsan-francisco-l61%2Fsan-francisco-mission-district-food-tour-with-6-tastings-t96519%2F%3Fcurrency%3DUSD%26psrc%3Dpartner_api&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=9KlQX6jHtcxvBigqwgvNi&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, you can walk to the Bernal Heights neighborhood and its park from most locations if you feel like it. The park is also within **walking distance from [Noe Valley](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D6096%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=1oOY6G79jzUYS9ZZR32Z8&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**. However, the length of the walk will of course depend on your exact starting point. From the city center and other parts of the city you want to explore, the distance may be too far given how much time it takes to get there.

#### By car

There is a **small parking lot** at the park with four standard spots and one ADA parking space. Otherwise, the vicinity around the park is **residential**, so be careful regarding parking regulations.

#### By public transportation

There are **several bus lines** that run close to the park. If you're coming from [downtown](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1429%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=2gyWC_nHfY1vL6UbMEd-C&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) or [the Mission](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FSan-Francisco%2FSmall-Group-Mission-District-Food-Tour-San-Francisco%2Fd651-7812P13%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=8bW2XyjGa5juMYrd39zOV&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), you can take the 14, which runs approximately every 5 minutes, to get quite near the park. If you’re further away and do not want to or cannot use your own car, I’d recommend using a ride share service to save time.

### When to go

In terms of the seasons, visiting this park is **most beautiful in winter** (provided it is not raining) **or spring** when the hills' grasses are **lush and green** and **wildflowers** are blooming (spring). In summer and especially fall, the rolling hills appear a bit ‘dead’ with their brown, dried-out appearance. Nonetheless, the views are always amazing, regardless of the season!

It is a **great park to visit during** **daytime**: I haven't been there in the dark, but I believe it might be too lonely (at least for my taste). Let me know in the comments if you've experienced it in the dark and your thoughts on it!

## Corona Heights Park

### Why to go there

The park is truly beautiful with its **rocky nature** and offers an amazing **view of downtown San Francisco and [Twin Peaks](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D900126477%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=lftW2RKueCn5aDF9eGgLI&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)**, where you can often observe the lingering fog. Additionally, the **Randall Museum**, Natural History Museum, Science Museum, and Arts Center, is situated within the park.

### How to get there

#### By foot

Corona Heights Park is accessible by foot from various **nearby neighborhoods**, such as **[Haight-Ashbury](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1434%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=oXetK8dDz0-991PCTwpSs&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), Buena Vista, or Castro**. Additionally, the **[Painted Ladies](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D17266%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=79U-l0K2nQLCaH6xwxsk9&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)** are within walking distance, albeit a bit further away (30 minutes on foot). In terms of architecture, all of these neighborhoods are amazing, but I personally find the most stunning density of beautiful houses in Buena Vista.

#### By car

There is a **parking lot at the Randall Museum** and **street parking** around. As for most places in SF, I would recommend taking public transportation though or walk if it is close enough.

#### By public transportation

Coming from downtown, you can take **muni to the Castro station and walk from there** (about 10 minutes). If you’re coming from [Pacific Heights](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1433%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=2h6Ep4CSfBQ2M227jlkLm&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), [Alamo Square](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D5927%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=eO37g5uM4aogTlYe2Vf3g&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) or [Noe Valley](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D6096%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=4-nO6Sdg1HQl5SbAzzj4X&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), you can take the bus 24, which stops at Castro Street, a close walk up th hill to the park.

### When to go

The park is **most beautiful in spring** when the grasses are green and wildflowers are blooming. Though all seasons are great! It is usually **windy** up there but not foggy.

## Golden Gate View Point & Battery Spencer

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

### Why to go there

Techinically these viewpoints are not located within San Francisco. However, they offer probably the **best view of the Golden Gate Bridge** including the iconic skyline of [downtown San Francisco](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D1429%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=0iKtNOZ8NUGMOpnnqVx5o&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf). They are situated on top of a hill above the bridge. Definetely bring a jacket as it is **usually pretty chilly and windy** up there. The [Golden Gate Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1063%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=sprR8hm2Gjx_LH-l08uw2&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) is really close, so you’ll have an amazing view of it, given that it is not too foggy.

Definetely check the weather radar before heading there — even if downtown and other parts of the city closer to the bay are sunny, the GG bridge may be completely covered in fog!

### How to get there

I would definitely **recommend using a car** to get there. The walking infrastructure is not that good, and the viewpoints are quite distant from the City. While you could consider walking across the [Golden Gate Bridge](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a1063%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=ldEcQuVAZqKN_yl_JS6Cg&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf) and then ascending the hill, the area on the other side of the bridge is not particularly pedestrian-friendly. Alternatively, you might opt to take one of the sightseeing buses (in case you’re taking a tour with them anyway) that stop at the parking lot across the bridge before heading up the hill.

### When to go

Ideal during the daytime. Particularly in summer, it's usually best to visit either not too early in the morning or later in the afternoon, as the fog tends to linger or begins to roll in. Nevertheless, there are days in **summer** when the bridge remains **covered in fog all day**. The **odds** are **significantly better in spring, fall,** and **winter**.

## Mission Dolores Park

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

![](https://maps.gstatic.com/mapfiles/transparent.png)

Honestly, I wasn’t sure if I should include the **[Mission Dolores Park](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D31908%26dest_type%3Dlandmark&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=0a5K-0lQajraTvEa2n4Ca&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf)** in this _viewpoints_ list because it is **SO MUCH more than a viewpoint**: You can easily chill the entire day here with friends, have a picknick with your significant other, or just soak in the energetic atmosphere. On the weekend, there is always a lot happening. Down the hill, on the North end of the park, there are a couple of **incredible food options**, including **ice cream** and **Mexican food**. Also in this area, there is a great small supermarket ( **Bi-Rite**), where you can get something to drink as well as prepared food. On the weekend, there are often **small, local businesses** around that offer products such as sweets or even handmade soap. For kids, there is also a **playground** embedded in the park.

### How to get there

#### By foot

The park is located in the **Dolores Heights neighborhood**, adjacent to the [Mission district](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FSan-Francisco%2FSmall-Group-Mission-District-Food-Tour-San-Francisco%2Fd651-7812P13%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=1NIIlkGQI2geH6ThcjrRT&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), [Castro](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=47&marker=659389&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Fx-attractions%2Fy%2Fd0-a3244%3Fapi_version%3D2.0&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=z71Gbe-jJA2F7HH5NlVg6&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf), and [Noe Valley](https://tpembars.com/re?campaign_id=84&marker=659389&p=2076&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.booking.com%2Fsearchresults.en-gb.html%3Fdest_id%3D6096%26dest_type%3Ddistrict&trs=444761&trace_id=Zza52854bdbf46411bb946eb4-659389&install_type=am_partner_content&product_type=emerald&journey_id=Fp3qWegh1yvTQbrdsYgtn&promo_kind=tp_other&page_url=https%3A%2F%2Fventurebeyondthepicture.com%2Foutdoor-adventures%2Fbest-views-sf). It's usually a pleasant walk from any of these neighborhoods, featuring a high density of Victorian and Edwardian houses to admire.

#### By car

There is **street parking** around the park, but finding a spot can be **challenging**. I would definitely **recommend taking public transportation or using Uber/Lyft** to get there, or walk if possible.

#### By public transportation

You can reach the park most conveniently from downtown by taking the **J train** (metro), which stops directly at the park.

### When to go

For this park, I’d say there isn't really a favorite season. This area is among those that receive the **least amount of fog and wind**, so it is rarely foggy there (if so, then in summer) and the lawn is always green and “manicured”. It can be great to watch the **sunset** from here, as the rays of sunlight touch the skyline. Nevertheless, I’d say this is definitely a park to visit **during the day** when it is **milder and busier**.

## Know Before you Go

- Check which viewpoints **combine** with other sights or neighborhoods you intend to visit.


- Check the **microclimate weather** for the specific part of town you want to go.

- **Always bring a jacket**: Viewpoints in particular can be quite chilly and windy, as most of them are on top of hills that are not protected from the elements.
