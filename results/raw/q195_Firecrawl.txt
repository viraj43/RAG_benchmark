#### About our Geopolitics news

Latest news on geopolitics, covering the Iran war, US-China rivalry, Russia-Ukraine peace talks, trade wars, tariffs, sanctions, and global diplomacy.

Geopolitics shapes the forces that define the modern world, from military conflicts and trade disputes to diplomatic summits and alliance realignments. The term encompasses the strategic interplay between nations as they compete for influence, resources, and security in an increasingly multipolar international order. With great power competition between the United States, China, Russia, and the European Union intensifying across economic, technological, and military domains, understanding geopolitical dynamics has never been more essential for anyone following international relations.

The US-Israeli war on Iran, launched on 28 February 2026 with coordinated airstrikes targeting Iranian leadership and nuclear infrastructure, has rapidly become the dominant geopolitical crisis of the moment. The killing of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei triggered Iranian retaliatory strikes against Israel and US bases across the region, while Iran's effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz has caused the largest global oil supply disruption since the 1970s energy crisis. Oil prices have surged past $100 per barrel, prompting an emergency release of strategic petroleum reserves by IEA member nations, and the conflict's humanitarian and economic consequences are reverberating across global markets, shipping routes, and energy-dependent economies.

The Russia-Ukraine war continues to shape European security, with US-brokered peace negotiations progressing through multiple rounds without a decisive breakthrough. Talks have centred on ceasefire terms, territorial disputes, and security guarantees for Ukraine, with a European-led "coalition of the willing" pledging military support. Russia's demands for territorial concessions and its opposition to Western peacekeeping forces remain major obstacles to any lasting settlement, while both sides face growing exhaustion after years of conflict.

US-China rivalry dominates the global trade landscape, with tariffs, export controls, and disputes over critical minerals and rare earths reshaping supply chains worldwide. The US Supreme Court's striking down of emergency tariffs in February 2026 forced a recalibration of American trade strategy, prompting new investigations under alternative legal authorities. Competition over artificial intelligence, semiconductors, and advanced technology has emerged as a central front in the broader contest between Washington and Beijing, with implications for industries and economies across the globe.

The current geopolitical era has deep historical roots, from the post-Cold War expansion of Western-led institutions to the rise of China as a global economic power and Russia's challenge to the European security order. The erosion of the rules-based international system, the growth of protectionism, and the return of industrial policy as a tool of statecraft mark a significant departure from the globalisation consensus of recent decades. Climate change, migration, and competition over scarce resources add further complexity, creating a world in which cooperation increasingly takes the form of flexible, bilateral, or regional arrangements rather than broad multilateral agreements.

Our NewsNow geopolitics feed brings together the latest headlines on the Iran war, Russia-Ukraine peace talks, US-China tensions, NATO and EU defence policy, Middle East developments, trade disputes, sanctions, and global diplomacy from a wide range of reliable sources. Whether you are tracking conflicts, alliance shifts, or the impact of tariffs and energy disruptions on the global economy, this feed is your comprehensive resource for staying informed about the forces shaping our world.
