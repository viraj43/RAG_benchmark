_This is a weekly feature that runs down the week’s top 10 announced funding rounds in the U.S. Check out last week’s biggest funding deal roundup [here](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-ai-autonomy-biotech-anthropic/)._

Large U.S. venture deals this week were led by a massive defense tech raise for space security startup [True Anomaly](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/true-anomaly). That theme continued with another two aerospace- and defense-related companies also getting major investor backing. We also saw sizable deals for startups applying AI to fintech, marketing, customer service, healthcare and developer tools. Let’s take a closer look.

1. **[True Anomaly](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/true-anomaly), $600M, aerospace and defense:** Centennial, Colorado-based True Anomaly raised a massive $600 million Series D led by [Eclipse](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/eclipse-ventures) and [Riot Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/riot-ventures), with participation from a long list of other backers including [Accel](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/accel), [645 Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/645-ventures), [G Squared](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/g-squared-2), [Acme Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/acme-capital), [Menlo Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/menlo-ventures) and [Paradigm](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/paradigm-b23a). True Anomaly develops space security and in-orbit defense systems, an area drawing increasing venture investor attention amid rising geopolitical tensions. The new round brings its total funding up to $1.1 billion, [per Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/true-anomaly#financials).

2. **[Rogo](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/rogo), $160M, AI and fintech:** New York-based Rogo secured $160 million in Series D funding led by [Kleiner Perkins](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/kleiner-perkins-caufield-byers) and joined by other investors including [Thrive Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/thrive-capital), [Sequoia Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/sequoia-capital), [Khosla Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/khosla-ventures), [BoxGroup](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/boxgroup), [J.P. Morgan Growth Equity Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/j-p-morgan-growth-equity-partners) and [Jack Altman](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/jack-altman). Rogo builds AI-powered tools to automate financial research and workflows. The latest financing brings its total funding raised to date to $314 million, [per Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/rogo#financials). The deal is also the latest example of investor enthusiasm for startups targeting high-value knowledge work such as [law](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/ai-legal-tech-investment-all-time-high-filevine/) and [accounting](https://news.crunchbase.com/fintech/cpa-founded-ai-tax-return-startup-juno-seed-funding/).

3. **[Hightouch](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/hightouch-0e87), $150M, AI and marketing:** San Francisco-based Hightouch raised $150 million in a Series D co-led by [Bain Capital Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/bain-capital-ventures) and [Goldman Sachs Alternatives](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/goldman-sachs-alternatives). [Iconiq Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/iconiq-capital), [Amplify Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/amplify-partners), [Sapphire Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/sapphire-ventures), [Y Combinator](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/y-combinator) and other investors joined. The company focuses on agentic AI-driven marketing and customer data activation. The round brings Hightouch’s total funding to date to [more than $332 million](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/hightouch-0e87#financials) and comes amid rising demand for AI tools embedded directly into enterprise marketing stacks.

4. **[Avoca](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/avoca-ai), $125M, AI and customer service:** New York-based Avoca brought in $125 million in a Series B led by [General Catalyst](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/general-catalyst-partners) and[Meritech Capital Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/meritech-capital-partners), with participation from other investors including [Kleiner Perkins](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/kleiner-perkins-caufield-byers), [BoxGroup](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/boxgroup), [Alt Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/alt-capital-6a95) and [Amplify Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/amplify-partners). Avoca develops AI agents for customer communication workflows. The new raise brings its total funding to $125.5 million, [per Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/avoca-ai#financials).

5. **[Netomi](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/netomi), $110M, AI and customer service:** San Mateo, California-based Netomi raised $110 million in a Series C led by [Accenture Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/accenture-ventures), with participation from [Adobe Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/adobe-ventures) and others, including individual investors [Justin Wexler](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/justin-wexler), [Henry Kravis](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/henry-r-kravis), [Greg Brockman,](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/greg-brockman)[Nikesh Arora](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/nikesh-arora) and [Mustafa Suleyman](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/mustafa-suleyman). The company offers AI-powered customer experience automation across channels. The new funding brings its total raised to date to $217 million, [per Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/accenture-ventures).

6. (tied) **[Parallel](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/parallel-463d), $100M, developer tools:** Palo Alto, California-based Parallel secured $100 million in a Series B led by [Sequoia Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/sequoia-capital), with additional backing from other big-name investors [Index Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/index-ventures), [Khosla Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/khosla-ventures) and [Kleiner Perkins](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/kleiner-perkins-caufield-byers). The startup is building a suite of AI agents and developer tools to automate workflows. It has raised $260 million to date, [per Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/parallel-463d#financials).

6. (tied) **[Scout AI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/scout-ai-fcb2), $100M, aerospace and defense:** Sunnyvale, California-based Scout AI raised a sizable $100 million Series A led by [Align Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/alignventures) and [Draper Associates](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/draper-associates-2). A long list of other investors joined, including [Booz Allen Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/booz-allen-ventures), [Vaughn Capital Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/vaughn-capital-partners) and [FJ Labs](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/fj-labs). The startup develops AI systems for aerospace and defense applications. Its large early-stage round underscores continued investor appetite for dual-use and defense-focused startups, which globally raised [a record $7.7 billion](https://news.crunchbase.com/defense-tech/funding-hits-record-high-2025-snapshot/) in 2025, per Crunchbase data.

8. **[Firestorm](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/firestorm-548d#overview), $82M, aerospace and defense:** San Diego-based Firestorm closed an $82 million Series B led by [Washington Harbour Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/washington-harbour-partners). [Booz Allen Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/booz-allen-ventures) also participated in this round, as did [IQT](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/in-q-tel), [Lockheed Martin](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/lockheed-martin), [New Enterprise Associates](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/new-enterprise-associates), [Motley Fool Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/motley-fool-ventures) and others. Firestone builds modular, mission-adaptable drone systems. It has raised nearly $150 million total, [per Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/firestorm-548d#financials).

9. **[Iterative Health](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/iterative-health), $77M, health diagnostics:** Cambridge, Massachusetts-based Iterative Health raised $77 million in a Series C led by [Google Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/google-ventures) and[Intrepid Growth Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/intrepid-growth-partners), with additional backing from [Obvious Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/obvious-ventures), [Insight Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/insight-partners) and [EDBI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/edbi). The company develops AI-powered diagnostic and clinical workflow tools, particularly in gastroenterology. It has raised more than $268 million since inception, according to [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/iterative-health#financials).

10. **[Standard Intelligence](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/standard-intelligence), $75M, foundational AI:** Investors continue to back next-generation foundation model startups. One of the latest is San Francisco-based AI research startup Standard Intelligence, which raised a $75 million Series A led by [Spark Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/spark-capital) and [Sequoia Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/sequoia-capital). The raise comes at a $425 million pre-money valuation. Other investors in the deal include [Stanley Druckenmiller](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/stanley-f-druckenmiller), [Milan Kovac](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/milan-kovac-65b8) and AI researcher [Andrej Karpathy](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/andrej-karpathy-7d6e). Standard AI is developing “computer-use” models designed to interact directly with software. Its approach — training on large-scale video data rather than manually annotated screenshots — aims to significantly reduce costs and improve performance.

## Large non-US deals

We also saw several sizable deals for startups based outside the U.S.:

[**Ineffable Intelligence**](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/ineffable-intelligence)**, $1.1B, foundational AI:** London-based frontier lab Ineffable Intelligence raised a $1.1 billion seed round, the largest for a European startup on record. (The previous record was set just a couple of months ago, when Paris-based frontier lab [Advanced Machine Intelligence](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/advanced-machine-intelligence) raised [a $1.03 billion seed round](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/world-model-ai-lab-ami-raises-europes-largest-seed-round/).) [Lightspeed Venture Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/lightspeed-venture-partners) and[Sequoia Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/sequoia-capital) led Ineffable’s seed funding.

[**Volant Aerotech**](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/volant-aerotech#overview)**, $300M, aerospace:** China-based Volant Aerotech raised a $300 million Series C led by [Stone Venture](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/stone-venture). The company is developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft, or eVTOLs, designed to be used as taxis.

[**Robot Era**](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/robot-era)**, $200M, robotics:** China-based humanoid robot developer Robot Era raised a $200 million round led by [S.F. Express](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/s-f-express), with participation from a long list of investors including [Zhaoxi Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/zhaoxi-capital) and [Tsinghua Holdings](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/tsinghua-holdings). The company is developing robots designed for industrial and service work, and follows [a string of other large fundings](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/unicorn-count-4-year-high-robotics-ai-march-2026/) for China-based robotics startups.

_This is a weekly feature that runs down the week’s top 10 announced funding rounds in the U.S. Check out last week’s biggest funding deal roundup [here](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-space-tech-sierra-ai-ayar/)._

Busy week, big checks, lots of AI and robotics. That, in ultra-brief synopsis form, characterized the general startup fundraising environment this week. Notably, the two largest global rounds were U.K.-based [Nscale](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/nscale)and Paris-based [Advanced Machine Intelligence](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/advanced-machine-intelligence?_gl=1*1gsqo6a*_gcl_au*MzUwMzU4MTA4LjE3NjcyMDk5NDIuMTUzNTY2NzA1Ny4xNzczNDA1NDU0LjE3NzM0MDU0NTM.*_ga*MjAzODAwMzgwMi4xNzQzNDI5MzYx*_ga_97DKWJJNRK*czE3NzM0MTI5NjYkbzEyNTQkZzEkdDE3NzM0MTk3MjEkajYwJGwwJGgw), which raised $2 billion and $1.03 billion, respectively.

In the U.S., meanwhile, e-commerce platform [Quince](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/Quince), AI networking developer [Nexthop AI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/nexthop-ai) and industrial automation startup [Mind Robotics](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/mind-robotics-1202) each picked up $500 million.

1. (tied) **[Quince](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/Quince), $500M, e-commerce:** Quince, an online fashion and home goods retailer with an affordable luxury theme, said it secured $500 million in Series E financing led by [Iconiq Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/iconiq-capital). The round sets a $10.1 billion post-money valuation for the 8-year-old, San Francisco-based company.

1. (tied) **[Nexthop AI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/nexthop-ai), $500M, AI infrastructure:** AI networking startup Nexthop AI raised $500 million in Series B funding led by [Lightspeed Venture Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/lightspeed-venture-partners), with [Andreessen Horowitz](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/andreessen-horowitz) joining as a major investor alongside other backers. The Santa Clara, California-based company develops switching technology built on open-source operating systems for AI and cloud networking.

1. (tied) **[Mind Robotics](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/mind-robotics-1202), $500M, robotics:**[Rivian](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/rivian-automotive) spin-out Mind Robotics closed on a $500 million Series A round, co-led by [Accel](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/accel) and Andreessen Horowitz. The Palo Alto, California-based company is developing an AI-enabled industrial robotics platform, with a focus on automating industrial and manufacturing tasks at scale.

4. **[Rhoda AI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/rhoda-ai), $450M, robotics:** Palo Alto, California-based robotics startup Rhoda AI emerged from stealth with $450 million in Series A funding reportedly led by [Premji Invest](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/premjiinvest). The startup trains robots using hundreds of millions of videos to develop intelligent models for operating in complex and changing environments.

5. **[Replit](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/replit), $400M, AI software creation:** Replit, an agentic AI software creation platform, picked up $400 million in Series D funding at a $9 billion valuation, up from $3 billion just six months ago. [Georgian](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/georgian-partners) led the financing for the Foster City, California-based company, joined by a long list of venture and celebrity investors.

6. (tied) **[Eridu](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/eridu-ai), $200M, AI networking:** AI startup Eridu emerged from stealth with over $200 million in a newly announced Series A round led by [Socratic Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/socratic-partners), [John Doerr](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/john-doerr-f685), [Hudson River Trading](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/hudson-river-trading), [Capricorn Investment Group](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/capricorn-investment-group) and [Matter Venture Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/matter-venture-partners). Saratoga, California-based Eridu develops a high-performance network switch for AI data centers.

6. (tied) **[Axiom Math AI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/axiom-math-ai), $200M, artificial intelligence:** Palo Alto, California-based Axiom Math AI, a developer of AI systems that can perform automated verification of computer code, [raised](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/axiommath_axiom-came-out-of-stealth-last-october-sharing-activity-7437871128997601280-Atar/) $200 million in Series A funding at a $1.6 billion valuation. [Menlo Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/menlo-ventures) led the round, joined by [Madrona](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/madrona-venture-group), [Greycroft](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/greycroft), [B Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/b-capital-group) and [Toyota Ventures](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/toyota-ai-ventures).

8. **[Sunday](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/sunday-aa0e), $165M, robotics:** Sunday, a startup planning a beta launch for a household robot called Memo later this year, raised $165 million in Series B funding. [Coatue](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/coatue) led the financing, which set a $1.15 billion valuation for the Mountain View, California-based company.

9. **[Kai](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/kai-9f1f), $125M, cybersecurity:** San Jose, California-based Kai, developer of an agentic AI cybersecurity platform, announced that it secured $125 million in funding led by [Evolution Equity Partners](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/evolution-equity-partners).

10. **[Oro Labs](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/oro-labs), $100M, procurement:** Oro Labs, developer of a procurement platform for enterprise customers, raised $100 million in Series C funding. [Brighton Park Capital](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/brighton-park-capital) and [Goldman Sachs Growth Equity](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/gs-growth) led the financing, which the company said follows a year of 300% revenue growth.

## Global financings

The week’s largest rounds went to European startups.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is AI?

Artificial intelligence, or AI, refers to the simulation of human intelligence by computers and other machines. Increasingly, there are AI applications that can problem-solve, understand and mimic human language, identify patterns, learn and reason.

Advancements in AI technologies in recent years have seen artificial intelligence adopted in industries ranging from health care to investing to manufacturing to national security and defense, and frequently put AI in the news.

But while AI systems have proven themselves adept at specific tasks, these systems have not (yet) become what researchers refer to as artificial general intelligence, or AGI —a hypothetical type of AI as smart and capable as a human that learns from experience, incorporates context into its understanding of the world, and adapts to new situations with acquired knowledge.

### What is the latest AI technology, the most common types of AI services, and most important concepts within artificial intelligence?

There are many concepts and categories within the broad realm of AI, including:

*   **Machine learning (ML):**A subset of AI that involves the use of algorithms and statistical models to enable machines to learn from data and improve their performance on a task over time. It includes techniques like supervised learning (learning from labeled data), unsupervised learning (finding patterns in unlabeled data), and reinforcement learning (learning through trial and error).
*   **Neural networks:**A class of algorithms inspired by the structure of the human brain. Deep learning is a subset of neural networks that involves complex architectures with many layers, enabling them to automatically learn hierarchies of features from data.
*   **Natural language processing (NLP):**A branch of AI that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret and generate human language. NLP technologies power chatbots, language translation, sentiment analysis and more.
*   **Large language models (LLMs):**Advanced artificial intelligence models that can understand and generate human-like text by processing vast amounts of training data. They have applications in natural language understanding, translation, content generation and more, revolutionizing how computers interact with and generate human language.
*   **Generative AI:**Closely related to NLP and LLMs, generative AI refers to a class of AI models and techniques that have the ability to generate new content, such as text, images, or even music, that resembles human-created data.
*   **Machine learning operations (MLOps):**A set of practices and tools that bridge the gap between machine-learning development and production deployment. It involves streamlining the entire machine-learning lifecycle, from data preparation and model training to deployment, monitoring and continuous improvement, to make machine-learning projects more efficient and reliable.
*   **Computer vision:**A field of AI that enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world. It’s used in applications like image and video analysis, object detection, facial recognition and autonomous vehicles.
*   **Expert systems:**These are AI systems designed to mimic the decision-making abilities of a human expert in a particular domain. These systems use rules and knowledge bases to make informed decisions.
*   **Robotics**: Integrating AI with robotics to create intelligent machines that can perform physical tasks in the real world. This includes things like assembly line work, surgical procedures and exploration in hazardous environments.
*   **Artificial general intelligence (AGI):**This is a still-hypothetical future AI system that possesses general human-like intelligence, including the ability to understand, learn and apply knowledge in a broad range of tasks, similar to the versatility of human intelligence — and considered by many to be the holy grail of AI.

### What are large language models (LLMs) and how do they work?

Large language models, or LLMs, are advanced computer programs designed to understand and generate human-like text based on the input they receive. They work using a combination of techniques from machine learning and natural language processing (NLP).

You can think of them as incredibly smart text generators that use vast amounts of data and complex math to understand and produce humanized text. They’re capable of a wide range of language-related tasks, from answering questions to composing short stories — even poems —and more.

The major LLMs on the market today include[OpenAI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/openai)’s [ChatGPT](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/chatgpt); [Google](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/google)’s PaLM, used in its Bard assistant; Claude, developed by startup [Anthropic](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/anthropic); and LLaMa, developed by Facebook parent [Meta](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/facebook).

Here’s a simplified explanation of how these LLMs generally work:

*   **Training data:** LLMs are trained on massive amounts of text data, including books, articles, websites and more. This training helps them learn the patterns, grammar and vocabulary of human language.
*   **Neural networks:**LLMs use artificial neural networks, which are computational models inspired by the human brain. These networks consist of layers of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process and transform input data.
*   **Input processing:**When you give an LLM some text as input, it processes the text through its neural network. The neural network breaks down the input into smaller parts and learns the relationships among words, phrases and sentences.
*   **Context understanding:**LLMs are designed to understand context. They analyze the words and phrases you provide and consider the surrounding context to generate coherent and contextually relevant responses.
*   **Probability and prediction:**LLMs assign probabilities to different words and sequences of words based on their training. They generate responses by predicting the most likely next word or phrase given the input and the context.
*   **Generative output:**After calculating these probabilities, the LLM generates a response. It doesn’t just regurgitate pre-written text but rather creates new text based on its understanding of the language.
*   **Iterative process:** LLMs often work iteratively, generating one word at a time and using the previously generated words to inform the next word choice. This process continues until a complete response is formed.
*   **Fine-tuning:**LLMs can also be fine-tuned for specific tasks or applications after their initial training. This helps them specialize in particular domains, like answering questions, translating languages, or generating code.
*   **Feedback loop:** LLMs can improve over time through a feedback loop. User interactions and corrections are used to refine their responses and enhance their language capabilities.

### What are the advantages of AI?

AI’s proponents say it has the potential to vastly augment and scale human capabilities, giving almost everyone access to unparalleled knowledge, personalized technologies and enhanced decision-making powers.

The venture capitalist [Marc Andreessen](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/marc-andreessen)[has argued](https://a16z.com/2023/06/06/ai-will-save-the-world/) that “what AI offers us is the opportunity to profoundly augment human intelligence to make all of these outcomes of intelligence – and many others, from the creation of new medicines to ways to solve climate change to technologies to reach the stars – much, much better from here.”

In medicine, AI could potentially analyze medical images like X-rays, MRIs and CT scans to assist doctors in identifying potential abnormalities, improving the accuracy and speed of diagnosis, or greatly accelerate the discovery of new life-saving drugs. In agriculture, AI-driven systems may be able to analyze satellite imagery and sensor data to monitor crop health, predict crop disease outbreaks, and optimize irrigation, leading to better crop yields, reducing malnutrition and starvation rates around the world. In education, children could have personalized AI tutors that evolve and adapt to their learning needs and challenges throughout their childhood and beyond.

### What are concerns around AI?

Concerns about the development of AI range from worries about ethics and bias associated with artificial intelligence development, to fears about intelligent systems displacing humans in their jobs. AI critics worry that the technology will cause mass unemployment and greater economic disparity, while AI proponents argue the technology will spur the creation of whole new industries, creating more jobs than it destroys.

Many experts have also raised concerns about AI’s potential to rapidly generate and spread misinformation or be used by bad actors to gain political and social power. Still others are worried about national security risks and the potential for AI to cause more deadly wars.

Some have also expressed concern that we could lose control of advanced AIs and that these systems could turn hostile toward humanity.

In [an open letter](https://futureoflife.org/open-letter/pause-giant-ai-experiments/) in March 2023, the [Future Of Life Institute](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/future-of-life-institute), a nonprofit, called for a pause in new AI development: “Contemporary AI systems are now becoming human-competitive at general tasks, and we must ask ourselves: Should we let machines flood our information channels with propaganda and untruth? Should we automate away all the jobs, including the fulfilling ones? Should we develop nonhuman minds that might eventually outnumber, outsmart, obsolete and replace us? Should we risk loss of control of our civilization?”

Signatories of the letter included [Tesla](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/tesla-motors) and [SpaceX](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/space-exploration-technologies) CEO [Elon Musk](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/elon-musk), [Apple](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/apple) co-founder [Steve Wozniak](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/steve-wozniak) and entrepreneur and former presidential candidate [Andrew Yang](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/andrew-yang).

### What is generative AI?

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that have the ability to generate new content, such as text, images or other data, based on patterns and knowledge learned from existing data. These systems use algorithms and neural networks to create original output that can be indistinguishable from human-generated content.

[ChatGPT](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/chatgpt), GPT-4 and [DALL-E](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/dall-e-2) — all three owned by [OpenAI](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/openai) — are examples of generative AI platforms. Other examples of generative AI platforms include [Midjourney](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/midjourney), [Microsoft](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/microsoft)-owned Bing Chat, [Stable Diffusion](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/stable-diffusion), [Meta](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/facebook)-owned LLaMA and [Google](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/google)-owned Bard.

### What is artificial general intelligence?

Artificial general intelligence (AGI) refers to a still-hypothetical form of artificial intelligence with human-like cognitive abilities that allow it to understand, learn and perform a wide range of tasks.

An AGI could theoretically adapt to various domains without specific programming. Unlike narrow or specialized artificial intelligence, AGI would have the potential to exhibit general intelligence and problem-solving capabilities comparable to human intelligence.

As of mid-2023, no AI system is advanced enough to be considered AGI.

### What are other names for AI?

*   **Machine learning**: A branch of AI that learns through the use of algorithms and data.
*   **Natural language processing**: Technology that uses AI and machine learning to “understand” and respond to human language patterns, and is used by generative AI to produce human-like text.
*   **Predictive analytics**: Technology that collects and uses data to predict and gather insights.
