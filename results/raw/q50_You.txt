Title: What is the current inflation rate in the US? | USAFacts
The inflation rate was 3.3%, as of March 2026. Inflation refers to the rise in prices of goods and services over time, which reduces the purchasing power of the dollar. The inflation rate is the percentage that describes how quickly these prices are rising. While several government datasets track price changes, the Consumer Price Index (CPI) represents about 90% of the US population. When inflation rates are positive, it means that the price of goods is increasing. When inflation decreases—for example, from 9.1% in June 2022 to 3.0% in June 2023—prices are still rising, just at a slower pace than before. If the inflation rate is above zero, prices are rising. The weighted system means that even if the prices of some items spike the headline inflation rate reflects the overall change in costs across the entire basket of goods. While the current headline inflation rate is 3.3%, the cost of fuel oil and other fuels has increased by 22.9% compared to the previous year. In March 2026, the headline inflation rate was 3.3% and core inflation was 2.6%.

Title: Historical U.S. Inflation Rate by Year: 1929 to 2025
The U.S. inflation rate measures the year-over-year (YOY) rise in prices for goods and services. The inflation rate typically reacts to phases of the business cycle, which is the natural cycle of expansion and contraction that the economy goes through over time. Consumer Price Index (CPI): Calculated by the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), it measures the change in prices U.S. consumers pay over time. It's the most popular way to gauge inflation and is published each month. Inflation is impacted by many factors, including consumer spending habits, government policies, and geopolitics. The U.S. inflation rate shows the change in prices year-over-year.
