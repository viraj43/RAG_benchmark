An irrational number is a number that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers. Its decimal expansion goes on forever without repeating. The square root of 2 and the constant e are examples. Pi is irrational because no fraction can capture its exact value. The digits of pi continue endlessly without settling into a repeating pattern, which is why such approximations as 3.14 or 22/7 are used in everyday work.
