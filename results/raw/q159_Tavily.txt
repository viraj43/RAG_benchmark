The cost of living in San Francisco, CA is [145.5% higher than the national average](https://www.bestplaces.net/cost_of_living/city/california/san_francisco), making it one of the most expensive cities in the country. There's no shortage of reasons to move to [San Francisco](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco). The city is a winning combination of urban energy, natural beauty, and a thriving economy. A prosperous housing market, economic growth, and even a bevy of sports teams paint a picture of a booming city. Additionally, San Francisco boasts a walkable design that beckons with outdoor adventures and the warmth of west coast living. However, this allure comes with a hefty price tag – San Francisco is notorious for its high [cost of living](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/what-is-cost-of-living).

If you’re thinking of becoming San Francisco’s newest resident, this comprehensive guide — with all data updated as of July 2025 — can help you learn everything you need to know [before you make your move](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/moving-to-san-francisco). We’ll dive into the various factors that contribute to [San Francisco's cost of living](https://www.apartmentlist.com/cost-of-living), providing valuable insights for prospective renters and residents alike, and explore everything from astronomical housing prices to everyday expenses, giving you a clear picture of what it truly costs to call the Golden Gate City home.

## Cost of Living in San Francisco, CA

## What Is the Cost of Living in San Francisco, CA?

The cost of living in San Francisco, California, remains one of the highest in the nation. According to our database, [San Francisco’s cost of living](https://www.apartmentlist.com/cost-of-living/ca/san-francisco) is 145% higher than the average U.S. city, with residents spending on average $9,067 a month to live in the city.

### Where Is the Cost of Living Highest in San Francisco, CA?

The average cost of living in San Francisco varies significantly by [neighborhood](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/best-san-francisco-neighborhoods), with some areas commanding particularly high prices. Neighborhoods like [Pacific Heights](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco/neighborhoods/pacific-heights) and [Presidio Heights](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco/neighborhoods/presidio-heights) are among some of the most expensive, characterized by luxurious homes, stunning views, and a wealth of amenities. In these areas, the rent for a one-bedroom apartment can exceed $4,000 per month, and home prices often soar well above $2 million.

### Where Is the Cost of Living Lowest in San Francisco, CA?

While San Francisco is known for its high cost of living, some neighborhoods offer relatively more affordable options. Areas like [Outer Sunset](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco/neighborhoods/outer-sunset) and [Excelsior](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco/neighborhoods/excelsior) tend to have lower housing costs compared to the city's most expensive neighborhoods. In these districts, the rent for a one-bedroom apartment can range from $1,500 to $3,000 per month, offering some respite for budget-conscious residents.

## H2: What Is the Average Rent in San Francisco, CA?

According to our [July 2025 Rent Report](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco#rent-report), the median rent in San Francisco is holding steady at $2,991—but that’s nearly 11% higher than this time last year. For context, that’s *seven times* the statewide growth rate (1.5%) and a big leap in a market where national rents are actually dipping (-0.7%).

With one-bedrooms averaging $3,019 and two-bedrooms at $3,579, San Francisco ranks as the second most expensive big city for renters in the U.S. And compared to the rest of the metro area—where the median sits at $2,661—rent in the city proper is 12.4% higher. While metro-wide rent rose 4.9% year-over-year, the city’s surge more than doubled that pace.

### How Much Is a 1-Bedroom Apartment in San Francisco, CA?

As of July 2025, the [average rent](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco#rent-report) in San Francisco for a one-bedroom apartment is $3,019 per month.

### How Much Is a 2-Bedroom Apartment in San Francisco, CA?

As of July 2025, the San Francisco [average rent](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco#rent-report) for a two-bedroom apartment is $3,579 per month.

### How Much is Housing in San Francisco, CA?

Housing prices in San Francisco are among the highest in the nation, reflecting its status as a global tech and cultural hub. As of 2025, the [median home price in the city exceeds $1.2 million](https://www.bestplaces.net/cost_of_living/city/california/san_francisco), making homeownership a significant financial commitment. Luxury neighborhoods like Pacific Heights often see prices far above this median, while areas like [Bayview](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco/neighborhoods/bayview) or the Outer Sunset may offer relatively lower price points, though still well above the national average.

The high cost of real estate is driven by limited housing supply and persistent demand from professionals and investors. For those considering purchasing a home, additional expenses such as property taxes and maintenance should also be factored into the budget. Despite these challenges, San Francisco’s unique blend of urban amenities and natural beauty continues to attract buyers willing to invest in its competitive housing market.

## What Salary Do You Need to Live in San Francisco, CA?

Let’s just say: it’s not cheap. With the city’s high cost of living, you’ll need a solid salary to stay ahead of the rent. Financial pros usually suggest keeping housing costs under [30% of your income](https://www.apartmentlist.com/rent-calculator). That means you’d need to earn about $120,760 a year to comfortably afford the average $3,019 rent for a one-bedroom apartment. Looking for more space? A two-bedroom or family-sized place pushes that number closer to $143,160 annually.

Of course, rent’s only part of the picture. Groceries, transportation, healthcare, and your lifestyle all play a role. The [median household income in SF](https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/fact/table/sanfranciscocitycalifornia/INC110222) is around $141,446, but depending on your goals (and spending habits), you may want to aim higher to truly live—not just get by.

## How Much is Transportation in San Francisco, CA?

San Francisco's transportation scene caters to a variety of preferences, offering a clear choice: embrace the extensive public transit system, hop on a bike, lace up your walking shoes, or face the high costs of car ownership.

For those who value convenience and affordability, the city boasts a [world-class public transit network](https://www.sfmta.com/). A monthly pass runs between $86 and $104, a significant savings compared to the $10,323 annual expense the [average San Franciscan spends on transportation](https://livingwage.mit.edu/metros/41860) (and a whopping $17,315 for a family of four!). Plus, with a stop never more than a quarter-mile away, accessibility is a major perk – no wonder over 230 million passengers utilize the system annually.

Walkers and cyclists also rejoice! San Francisco boasts near-perfect [walkability and bikeability](https://www.walkscore.com/CA/San_Francisco). This means daily errands and leisure activities can be easily accomplished without a car, promoting a healthy lifestyle and reducing your environmental impact.

Car owners, on the other hand, face a different reality. Parking is a notorious challenge, with monthly fees ranging from $200 to $400 depending on location. The city's steep hills and limited spaces add to the frustration, not to mention gas prices and tolls. While manageable with careful planning, car-related costs remain a significant factor in San Francisco's overall living expenses.

## How Much is Food in San Francisco, CA?

San Francisco's high cost of living significantly impacts food expenses. A single person can expect to spend around [$5,311 annually on groceries and dining](https://livingwage.mit.edu/metros/41860), while a family of four may spend upwards of $15,568.

Dining out can be a significant expense. The city boasts a renowned culinary scene, from the Mission District's vibrant Mexican food scene to Chinatown's authentic Asian flavors. However, expect to pay a premium, with [average meals for two at mid-tier restaurants often exceeding $137.50](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Francisco).

Groceries also tend to be pricier than the national average, with San Franciscans spending [roughly 19% more](https://www.apartmentlist.com/cost-of-living/ca/san-francisco). A single person can anticipate spending $400-$500 per month, while families may budget $800-$1,000.

Fortunately, there are ways to mitigate these costs. Shopping at farmers' markets, discount grocery stores, and bulk food stores can help. Additionally, meal planning and home cooking are effective strategies for budget-conscious residents.

## How Much is Healthcare in San Francisco, CA?

Healthcare costs in San Francisco are significantly higher than the national average. This is due to a combination of factors, including high demand for medical services, a concentration of specialized care facilities, and the overall high cost of living in the city. A single adult, for example, can expect to pay [$2,987 per year in medical expenses](https://livingwage.mit.edu/metros/41860). A family of four will be significantly more, approximately $11,498 per year.

Expect to pay more for health insurance premiums, deductibles, and out-of-pocket expenses compared to many other parts of the country. However, San Francisco boasts a world-class healthcare system with access to top-tier hospitals and specialists.

## How Much Are Utilities in San Francisco, CA?

The average San Francisco resident pays $226.86 per month in utilities, which is slightly lower than California and national averages for [basic utilities](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/average-utility-bill) like gas, water, and electricity. This is likely due to the city's temperate weather, with average highs in the fifties during winter, minimizing heating needs.

While not included in traditional utility calculations, budgeting for internet and cell phone service is essential. According to [Numbeo](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Francisco), the average cost of cell phone service in San Francisco sits around $65.30 per month, while average internet costs per month average $67.48.

Overall, San Francisco's utility costs fall within the national average. This figure can fluctuate based on factors like residence size, usage habits, and season. While not the most expensive aspect of living in San Francisco, [utilities are a necessary consideration](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/estimating-apartment-utilities-cost) when planning your monthly budget.

## How Much Is Child Care in San Francisco, CA?

Childcare in San Francisco is a budget buster, often exceeding the cost of rent. For working families, this essential expense [averages $45,645 per year](https://livingwage.mit.edu/metros/41860) for a family with two children. Even preschoolers are costly, with monthly rates typically [averaging around $3,250 per month](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Francisco).

San Francisco's high cost of living and competitive childcare market are key drivers of these prices. The need for qualified staff, comprehensive programs, and the city's overall expenses all contribute. While some families explore home-based or cooperative childcare options for potential savings, even these alternatives come at a premium compared to national averages.

## San Francisco Fitness & Entertainment

San Francisco caters to both the fitness buff and the entertainment enthusiast, but it's important to factor these costs into your budget. San Francisco's mild climate and extensive park system make outdoor activities year-round a reality. Hike trails, yoga in [Golden Gate Park](https://sfrecpark.org/770/Golden-Gate-Park), or beach volleyball at [Ocean Beach](https://www.parksconservancy.org/parks/ocean-beach) are just a few possibilities. Indoors, a diverse array of gyms, studios, and fitness centers cater to all interests and budgets. Theaters, music venues, museums, and art galleries complete the picture, offering endless options to explore the city's rich cultural scene.

### Fitness in San Francisco, CA

San Francisco caters to all fitness preferences. [Gym memberships average $102.91 monthly](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Francisco), but deals and budget-friendly options like outdoor group classes abound. Embrace the city's reputation as an outdoor paradise! Take advantage of the extensive park system within a 10-minute walk of any resident, with options for walking, biking, skating, or even free group fitness classes in [Mission Dolores Park](https://sfrecpark.org/Facilities/Facility/Details/Mission-Dolores-Park-188). Water enthusiasts can enjoy kayaking in McCovey Cove or swimming at [Aquatic Park](https://www.nps.gov/safr/planyourvisit/aquaticparkcove.htm) for a unique workout with a view.

### Entertainment in San Francisco, CA

San Francisco's entertainment scene caters to diverse interests. Catch a live game – the thrill is worth it, though ticket prices vary by sport. According to Seakgeek, you can snag a ticket for a [San Francisco Giants](https://www.mlb.com/giants) game for as low as $5, a [San Francisco 49ers](https://www.49ers.com/) game for $35, and a [Golden State Warriors](https://www.nba.com/warriors) game for $96 before taxes and fees.

For a more cultural experience, explore the city's wealth of affordable museums such as the [San Francisco Museum of Modern Art](https://www.sfmoma.org/), the [Asian Art Museum](https://asianart.org/), or the [Walt Disney Family Museum](https://www.waltdisney.org/).

Don't miss out on free options either – stroll through [Chinatown](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-francisco/neighborhoods/chinatown), relax in the [Golden Gate Park Japanese Tea Garden](https://www.japaneseteagardensf.com/), or catch a free outdoor concert at [Stern Grove](https://sfrecpark.org/facilities/facility/details/Sigmund-Stern-Grove-375).

## Other Expenses to Consider in San Francisco

Beyond core expenses, San Francisco living presents other significant costs. Pet ownership can be expensive, with vet bills, food, and grooming adding up quickly in a city with a high cost of living. Personal care expenses, such as haircuts, grooming, and clothing, also tend to be higher in San Francisco.

San Francisco boasts one of the highest tax burdens in the country. This includes a sales tax of 8.625%, a state income tax (with rates increasing as income grows), and city income tax. Property taxes can also contribute to the overall tax burden.

## San Francisco’s Job Market

San Francisco's job market is robust and diverse, with a strong focus on technology, finance, and healthcare. The city's tech industry, home to numerous giants like Google, Apple, and Facebook, creates a high demand for professionals in fields like software engineering, data science, and product management. As a major financial center, San Francisco also offers opportunities in investment banking, asset management, and fintech. Additionally, the city's world-class medical institutions provide a wide range of career paths in healthcare, including positions for physicians, nurses, and researchers.

Beyond these major sectors, San Francisco offers diverse job opportunities in fields like education, arts and culture, tourism, hospitality, and green technology. However, competition for desirable positions can be fierce, and the cost of living can significantly impact earning potential. Thoroughly research industry-specific salaries and consider the overall cost of living when evaluating job opportunities in San Francisco.

## Cost of Living in San Francisco, CA FAQs

### Is it expensive to live in San Francisco?

San Francisco is notoriously expensive. It consistently ranks among the most expensive cities in the United States, and is 145.5% more expensive than the average U.S. city. Housing costs are the primary driver, with exorbitant rent and home prices. Groceries, dining out, transportation, healthcare, and even everyday essentials like pet care and personal grooming are significantly pricier than the national average.

### What is the cost of living in San Francisco for one month?

Average monthly spending in San Francisco is $8,105.

### Is San Francisco More Expensive Than Los Angeles?

San Francisco boasts a steeper price tag than [Los Angeles](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/los-angeles). Average rent for a one-bedroom apartment in San Francisco costs $3,019 compared to [$1,868 in LA](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/los-angeles#rent-report). Additional [living costs](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/cost-of-living-in-los-angeles) like transportation and groceries are also typically lower in LA.

### What is the cost of living in San Diego vs San Francisco?

The [cost of living in San Diego](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/cost-of-living-san-diego) vs San Francisco presents a clear contrast, driven by each city's unique character. While both Californian gems offer sunshine and coastal charm, San Francisco takes the crown for overall expenses, especially housing. Renting or buying a home there will put a bigger dent in your wallet compared to [San Diego](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-diego), where average rent for a [one-bedroom apartment is only $1,975](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/san-diego#rent-report). San Diego might offer some relief with potentially lower transportation and grocery costs.

### What is the population of San Francisco?

According to the [U.S. Census Bureau](https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/fact/table/sanfranciscocitycalifornia/PST045224), there are an estimated 873,965 people living in San Francisco

### What is the cost of living in Sacramento vs San Francisco?

San Francisco is significantly [more expensive than Sacramento](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-life/cost-of-living-in-sacramento), especially when it comes to housing. As of July 2025, the median rent in San Francisco is $3,019 for a one-bedroom and $3,579 for a two-bedroom. In contrast, [Sacramento’s median rent is $1,384 for a one-bedroom](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/sacramento#rent-report) and $1,749 for a two-bedroom. That means renters in San Francisco are paying more than double what [Sacramento](https://www.apartmentlist.com/ca/sacramento) residents pay for similar apartments. Beyond rent, other living expenses like groceries, transportation, and services also tend to be higher in San Francisco due to its denser urban setting and overall costlier economy.

# Cost of Living in San Francisco for Students

![](https://www.universityliving.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/1.png.webp) 

[University Living](https://www.universityliving.com/blog/author/universityliving/) • Last updated: March 11, 2026

Table of Contents

San Francisco is one of the best cities to live and study. The city offers great weather, good night scenes, adventures, etc.

It is home to some of the best universities in the world, like UC Berkeley, [**Stanford University**](https://www.universityliving.com/united-states/stanford-california/u/stanford-university), and the [**University of San Francisco**](https://www.universityliving.com/united-states/san-francisco/u/university-of-san-francisco).

## **Is San Francisco safe and affordable?**

San Francisco is a safe city. Being a city of love, the city spreads and showers anyone living in the city with a welcoming and hospitable nature.

The cost of living in San Francisco is $2,499 per month excluding tuition fees. San Francisco offers various types of student accommodation that are close to universities.

Before we list the details of the cost of living in San Francisco, let’s summarize its cost.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Expenses | Cost (Average) |
| Average Tuition Fees | $20,000 – $45,000 / Year |
| Accommodation | $1,500 – $3,500 / Month |
| Local transport | $121 / Month |

## **Costs of Tuition Fees**

Since San Francisco is home to many top universities worldwide, the tuition fees will be expensive if you get enlisted in them. For those who have a scholarship, it might help cover the cost. In any case, the tuition fee will depend on the course type and university you choose. We only list the average tuition fee below, but it varies depending on the university.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Undergraduate Tuition Fee** | **Post-Graduate Tuition Fee** |
| $20,000 – $40,000 / Year | $25,000 – $45,000 / Year |

## **Student Accommodation Costs**

The cost of **[student housing in San Francisco](https://www.universityliving.com/united-states/city/san-francisco)** will depend upon many factors. They will be location, accommodation type, the facilities provided, etc.

### **Student Housing**

Student housing is one of the most popular types of accommodation. Students love this accommodation because it’s close to the university and offers privacy and amenities.

Most of the accommodations are at a distance of under 3 miles. While the ones outside of the city center are upto 20 miles.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Student Housing** | **Price** |
| Ensuite/ Studio | $1,491 – $2,001 / Month |

### **Private Apartment**

The private apartment is also another popular type of **[student accommodation](https://www.universityliving.com/)**. Students who like privacy and want to enjoy student life with their friends choose to stay here. Students can take the whole place or share the apartment with friends.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Apartment** | **Price** |
| Private | $2,583 – $3,335 / Month |

### **University Halls**

University halls are built inside the campus or close to one. Usually, freshers choose this type of accommodation as they are new to the country. The university hall provides rooms for students who study at the university.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **University Hall** | **Price** |
| Ensuite/ Studio | $1,500 – $3,500 / Month |

### **Food**

The cost of food in San Francisco is slightly above the national average. If you can cook meals at home, you should adopt this method of eating. It will save you a lot of money and keep you healthy too. But, on a serious note, eating out is expensive. For instance, a meal at a mid-range restaurant will cost $25 while a burger will cost $12.

### **Utility Bills**

Your utility bills will be the cost of gas, electricity, cooling, heating, WiFi, etc. Student housing usually covers utility bills.

### **Transportation**

San Francisco has the Municipal Transportation System. It is called Muni and operates buses, cable cars, and street cars. Many universities are within walking distance. You can always go by foot. Even the attractions are within walking distance from the city center.

## **About San Francisco**

San Francisco is located on Northern [**California**](https://amberstudent.com/places/search/california-1811050968030?utm_medium=blog&utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=search_lead)'s sunny Pacific coast, at the tip of a peninsula that separates the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco Bay. The city is well-known for its **beautiful scenery**, **warm climate**, and **uphill streets**. It is also one of the most culturally diverse areas to reside in the United States, and it is home to some of the world's most brilliant brains due to its proximity to **Silicon Valley**. To gain a sense of San Francisco's history, visit one of its fascinating museums or galleries. Music fans will feel at home in this city, which takes pride in its music scene. San Francisco has something for everyone, whether you appreciate **classical concerts**, **up-and-coming local musicians**, or **world-famous pop artists**.

## **Cost of Living in San Francisco for International Students**

**‍**The average living expenses in San Francisco are very high for international students. Monthly expenses in San Francisco range from **$2,600 to $2,700**, covering [**student housing in San Francisco**](https://amberstudent.com/places/search/san-francisco-1811261246298?utm_medium=blog&utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=search_lead), food, transportation, and utilities. While rent is the largest cost, other important expenses add to the range.  Let's dive into the expenses.

## **1. Rent**

Rental expenses contribute to the high cost of living in San Francisco CA. The expenses have been on the rise in 2025, due to the growth of the **AI industry** and the **return-to-office rules**. In **August 2025**, the average rent for a one-bedroom apartment in the city was **over $3,400 per month**. While San Francisco has **rent control laws** that limit how much landlords can increase rent annually, the market shows that overall rental costs are increasing.

**How to save on rent costs?**  
Sharing a place with roommates helps cut housing costs in San Francisco, letting you live in better areas. Use sites like Craigslist, Roomster, or SpareRoom to find options, and try offering a longer lease term.

## **2. Housing and Apartments**

Your entire cost of living in San Francisco will be greatly influenced by the type of student housing you choose.

**Off-campus housing**- Shared apartments cost around **$800–$1,200/month**, studios, and one-bedrooms range **$1,500 to $2,400**.  
**On-campus housing**-  Local university dorms, including meal plans, cost an average **$1,200-$1,500 monthly**.  
**Other options**-  PBSA (Purpose Built Student Accommodation) and Build-to-Rent cost **$1200-$2,000** with facilities.

**How can you save on student housing costs?**

Finding a double occupancy room and staying with friends can help you lower the cost of living in San Francisco. You can find more affordable housing a little beyond the city centre. You might look for dorm-style student housing in San Francisco and commute to your university from there. This way, you can save a lot on the price to live in San Francisco.

## 3. Travel Expenses

Travel costs are one important factor in adding to the overall cost of living in San Francisco as a student. Before moving in, you need to explore the public transportation and its expenses properly.  
  
**Bus/Muni:** San Francisco’s Muni buses are affordable, with a single adult ride of **$2.85**, a one-day pass available for **$5.70**, and monthly passes cost **$86 or $104.** Underage youths can ride free, and seniors and low-income people have a fare of **$1.40**.  
**Cable Cars:** Riding San Francisco cable cars gives a unique city experience, costing **$9** only for a single ride as of 2025. Monthly expenses of a personal car can exceed **$500**, considering gas, parking, insurance, and maintenance.  
**Taxis:** Taxi fares start at a rate of **$3.50**, with additional charges, but provide flexible travel for short or urgent trips.

**How to save on travel expenses?**  
  
Students can use unlimited ride passes like the **SF State Gator Pass** or **Muni passes**. The best thing about the Muni passes is that it is free for those under 18 years of age, and low-income residents have fare reductions using programs like **Clipper START**. Clipper card also provides **transfer discounts**. By using certain hacks, you can save on the cost of living in San Francisco.

## **4. Food and Grocery Expense**

We cannot overlook the price of groceries and food while talking about the San Francisco monthly expenses because they vary from person to person. These costs are more difficult to calculate. Monthly groceries for a single person in San Francisco range from **$400 to $500**. Dining out expenses for a casual meal are **$20 for one**, and a meal at a mid-range restaurant is **$60 or more.**

**How can you save on your food expenses?**‍

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is San Francisco expensive to live in?

Yes, San Francisco is one of the most expensive cities to live in the U.S.—65% higher than the national average—mainly due to high housing costs, utilities, and transportation. With that being said, living in SF on a budget is possible, especially if you settle down in an [affordable San Francisco neighborhood](https://www.extraspace.com/blog/city-guides/safe-affordable-neighborhoods-in-san-francisco/), like Bernal Heights, Excelsior, and Outer Sunset. These areas offer a more budget-friendly lifestyle, further from big-city costs and amenities.

### What salary is needed to live comfortably in San Francisco?

While the cost to live comfortably in San Francisco varies based on needs, a single adult typically needs to earn roughly [$122,000 annually](https://smartasset.com/data-studies/salary-needed-live-comfortably-2025). A family of four would likely require around $367,000 per year. These costs are contributed to by high San Francisco housing costs, as well as monthly utilities and transit. However, it's worth acknowledging that some [affordable neighborhoods in San Francisco](https://www.extraspace.com/blog/city-guides/safe-affordable-neighborhoods-in-san-francisco/), like Bernal Heights, Excelsior, and Outer Sunset, offer a more budget-friendly lifestyle, further from big-city costs and amenities.

### What are the average monthly expenses in San Francisco?

For a single adult, average monthly expenses in San Francisco can range from $1500-$1700, excluding rent prices. Depending on what neighborhood you live in, your lifestyle, and other factors, you may have lower or higher monthly expenses.
