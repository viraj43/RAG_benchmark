# What is the largest ocean basin on Earth?

## The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest of the world ocean basins.

![the Pacific Ocean](https://oceanservice.noaa.gov/facts/pacific.jpg)

The 'Ring of Fire' is defined by a nearly continuous series of oceanic trenches, island arcs, and volcanic mountain ranges and/or boundaries of several plates. It encircles the periphery of the Pacific Ocean Basin. (image courtesy of COMET® program)

Covering approximately 63 million square miles and containing more than half of the free water on Earth, the Pacific is by far the largest of the world’s ocean basins. All of the world’s continents could fit into the Pacific basin.

The Pacific is the oldest of the existing ocean basins. Its oldest rocks have been dated at about 200 million years. The Pacific basin is referred to as the “Ring of Fire” due to intense earthquake and volcanic activity occurring near areas of tectonic plate subduction (where one tectonic plate is forced under another).

The Atlantic basin is the second largest basin, followed by the Indian Ocean basin, the Southern Ocean, and finally the Arctic Ocean basin.

The **Pacific Ocean** is the largest and deepest of [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth")'s five [oceanic divisions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borders_of_the_oceans "Borders of the oceans"). It stretches from the [Arctic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic_Ocean "Arctic Ocean") in the north to the [Southern Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Ocean "Southern Ocean"), or, depending on the definition, to [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica") in the south, and is bounded by the continents of [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia") and [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia_(continent) "Australia (continent)") in the west and the [Americas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas "Americas") in the east.

At 165,250,000 square kilometers (63,800,000 square miles) in area (as defined with a southern Antarctic border), the Pacific Ocean is the largest division of the [World Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Ocean "World Ocean") and the [hydrosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrosphere "Hydrosphere") and covers approximately 46% of Earth's water surface and about 32% of the planet's total surface area, larger than its entire land area (148,000,000 square kilometers or 57,000,000 square miles).[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-ebc-1) The centers of both the [water hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_and_water_hemispheres "Land and water hemispheres") and the [Western Hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Hemisphere "Western Hemisphere"), as well as the [oceanic pole of inaccessibility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pole_of_inaccessibility#Oceanic_pole_of_inaccessibility "Pole of inaccessibility"), are in the Pacific Ocean. Ocean circulation (caused by the [Coriolis effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coriolis_effect "Coriolis effect")) subdivides it[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-2) into two largely independent volumes of water that meet at the [equator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator "Equator"), the **North Pacific Ocean** and the **South Pacific Ocean** (or more loosely the [South Seas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Seas "South Seas")). The Pacific Ocean can also be informally divided by the [International Date Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Date_Line "International Date Line") into the **East Pacific** and the **West Pacific**, which allows it to be further divided into four quadrants, namely the **Northeast Pacific** off the coasts of [North America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America "North America"), the **Southeast Pacific** off [South America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_America "South America"), the **Northwest Pacific** off [Far Eastern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Far_East "Far East")/ [Pacific Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Asia "Pacific Asia"), and the **Southwest Pacific** around [Oceania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceania "Oceania").

The Pacific Ocean's mean depth is 4,000 meters (13,000 feet; 2,200 fathoms).[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-3) The [Challenger Deep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Challenger_Deep "Challenger Deep") in the [Mariana Trench](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariana_Trench "Mariana Trench"), located in the northwestern Pacific, is the deepest known point in the world, reaching a depth of 10,928 m (35,853 ft; 5,976 fathoms).[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-4) The Pacific also contains the deepest point in the [Southern Hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere "Southern Hemisphere"), the [Horizon Deep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horizon_Deep "Horizon Deep") in the [Tonga Trench](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonga_Trench "Tonga Trench"), at 10,823 m (35,509 ft; 5,918 fathoms).[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-5) The third deepest point on Earth, the [Sirena Deep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sirena_Deep "Sirena Deep"), is also located in the Mariana Trench.

The Pacific has many major [marginal seas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marginal_sea "Marginal sea"), including (listed clockwise from the west) the [Philippine Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippine_Sea "Philippine Sea"), [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea"), [East China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_China_Sea "East China Sea"), [Sea of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Japan "Sea of Japan"), [Sea of Okhotsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk "Sea of Okhotsk"), [Bering Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bering_Sea "Bering Sea"), [Gulf of Alaska](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Alaska "Gulf of Alaska"), [Gulf of California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_California "Gulf of California"), [Tasman Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tasman_Sea "Tasman Sea"), and [Coral Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral_Sea "Coral Sea").

## Etymology

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Etymology")\]

In 1513, during the [Age of Discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery "Age of Discovery"), [Spanish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Spain#early_modern_Spain "History of Spain") explorer [Vasco Núñez de Balboa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa "Vasco Núñez de Balboa") crossed the [Isthmus of Panama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama "Isthmus of Panama") and sighted the great "Southern Sea", which he named _Mar del Sur_ (in Spanish). Afterwards, the ocean's current name was coined by Portuguese explorer [Ferdinand Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan "Ferdinand Magellan") during the Spanish [circumnavigation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumnavigation "Circumnavigation") of the world in 1520, as he encountered favorable winds upon reaching the ocean. He called it _Mar Pacífico_, which in Portuguese and Spanish means 'peaceful sea'.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-6)[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-7)

## History

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: History")\]

### Prehistory

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Prehistory")\]

Across the continents of [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia"), [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia_(continent) "Australia (continent)") and the [Americas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas "Americas"), more than 25,000 islands, large and small, rise above the surface of the Pacific Ocean. Multiple islands are the shells of former active [volcanoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") that have lain dormant for thousands of years (see also [Ring of Fire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ring_of_Fire "Ring of Fire")). Close to the [equator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator "Equator"), without vast areas of blue ocean, are a dot of [atolls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atoll "Atoll") that have over intervals of time been formed by [seamounts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seamounts "Seamounts") as a result of tiny [coral islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral_island "Coral island") strung in a ring within surroundings of a central [lagoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagoon "Lagoon").

### Early migrations

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Early migrations")\]

Main articles: [Peopling of Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peopling_of_Southeast_Asia "Peopling of Southeast Asia") and [Austronesian peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples "Austronesian peoples")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Fijian_double_canoe%2C_model%2C_Otago_Museum%2C_2016-01-29.jpg/250px-Fijian_double_canoe%2C_model%2C_Otago_Museum%2C_2016-01-29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Fijian_double_canoe,_model,_Otago_Museum,_2016-01-29.jpg) Model of a [Fijian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiji "Fiji") [drua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drua "Drua"), an example of an [Austronesian vessel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples "Austronesian peoples") with a double-canoe ( [catamaran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catamaran "Catamaran")) hull and a [crab claw sail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crab_claw_sail "Crab claw sail")

Important human migrations occurred in the Pacific in prehistoric times. Modern humans first reached the western Pacific in the [Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic "Paleolithic"), at around 60,000 to 70,000 years ago. Originating from a southern coastal human migration out of Africa, they reached [East Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asia "East Asia"), [Mainland Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia"), the Philippines, [New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Guinea "New Guinea"), and then Australia by making the sea crossing of at least 80 kilometres (50 mi) between [Sundaland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundaland "Sundaland") and [Sahul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia_(continent) "Australia (continent)"). It is not known with any certainty what level of maritime technology was used by these groups – the presumption is that they used large bamboo rafts which may have been equipped with some sort of sail. The reduction in favourable winds for a crossing to Sahul after 80,000 B.P. fits with the dating of the settlement of Australia, with no later migrations in the prehistoric period. The seafaring abilities of pre-Austronesian residents of Island South-east Asia are confirmed by the settlement of [Buka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buka,_Papua_New_Guinea "Buka, Papua New Guinea") by 32,000 B.P. and [Manus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manus_Island "Manus Island") by 25,000 B.P. Journeys of 180 kilometres (110 mi) and 230 kilometres (140 mi) are involved, respectively.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-8)

The descendants of these migrations today are the [Negritos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Negritos "Negritos"), [Melanesians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melanesians "Melanesians"), and [Indigenous Australians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigenous_Australians "Indigenous Australians"). Their populations in [maritime Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_Southeast_Asia "Maritime Southeast Asia"), coastal [New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Guinea "New Guinea"), and [Island Melanesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Island_Melanesia "Island Melanesia") later intermarried with the incoming [Austronesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples "Austronesian peoples") settlers from [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") and the northern [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"), but also earlier groups associated with [Austroasiatic-speakers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austroasiatic_languages "Austroasiatic languages"), resulting in the modern peoples of Island Southeast Asia and Oceania.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Jett2017-9)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Waruno2017-10)

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Chronological_dispersal_of_Austronesian_people_across_the_Pacific.svg/500px-Chronological_dispersal_of_Austronesian_people_across_the_Pacific.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Chronological_dispersal_of_Austronesian_people_across_the_Pacific.svg) Map showing the [migration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples#Migration_from_Taiwan "Austronesian peoples") of the [Austronesian peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples "Austronesian peoples")

A later seaborne migration is the [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") [Austronesian expansion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_expansion "Austronesian expansion") of the [Austronesian peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples "Austronesian peoples"). Austronesians originated from the island of [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwanese_aborigines "Taiwanese aborigines") c. 3000–1500 BCE. They are associated with distinctive maritime sailing technologies (notably [outrigger boats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outrigger_boat "Outrigger boat"), [catamarans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catamaran "Catamaran"), [lashed-lug](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lashed-lug "Lashed-lug") boats, and the [crab claw sail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crab_claw_sail "Crab claw sail")) – it is likely that the progressive development of these technologies were related to the later steps of settlement into Near and Remote Oceania. Starting at around 2200 BCE, Austronesians sailed southwards to settle the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"). From, probably, the [Bismarck Archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bismarck_Archipelago "Bismarck Archipelago") they crossed the western Pacific to reach the [Marianas Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marianas_Islands "Marianas Islands") by 1500 BCE,[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-11) as well as [Palau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palau "Palau") and [Yap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yap "Yap") by 1000 BCE. They were the first humans to reach [Remote Oceania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remote_Oceania "Remote Oceania"), and the first to cross vast distances of open water. They also continued spreading southwards and settling the rest of [Maritime Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_Southeast_Asia "Maritime Southeast Asia"), reaching [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") and [Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia "Malaysia") by 1500 BCE, and further west to [Madagascar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar "Madagascar") and the [Comoros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros "Comoros") in the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") by around 500 CE.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-12)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Gray-et-al2009-13)[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Pawley2002-14) More recently, it is suggested that Austronesians expanded already earlier, arriving in the Philippines already in 7000 BCE. Additional earlier migrations into Insular Southeast Asia, associated with Austroasiatic-speakers from Mainland Southeast Asia, are estimated to have taken place already in 15000 BCE.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-15)

At around 1300 to 1200 BCE, a branch of the Austronesian migrations known as the [Lapita culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lapita_culture "Lapita culture") reached the [Bismarck Archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bismarck_Archipelago "Bismarck Archipelago"), the [Solomon Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon_Islands "Solomon Islands"), [Vanuatu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanuatu "Vanuatu"), [Fiji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiji "Fiji"), and [New Caledonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Caledonia "New Caledonia"). From there, they settled [Tonga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonga "Tonga") and [Samoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samoa "Samoa") by 900 to 800 BCE. Some also back-migrated northwards in 200 BCE to settle the islands of eastern [Micronesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micronesia "Micronesia") (including the [Carolines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caroline_Islands "Caroline Islands"), the [Marshall Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Islands "Marshall Islands"), and [Kiribati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiribati "Kiribati")), mixing with earlier Austronesian migrations in the region. This remained the furthest extent of the Austronesian expansion into [Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia") until around 700 CE when there was another surge of island exploration. They reached the [Cook Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cook_Islands "Cook Islands"), [Tahiti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tahiti "Tahiti"), and the [Marquesas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marquesas "Marquesas") by 700 CE; [Hawaiʻi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii "Hawaii") by 900 CE; [Rapa Nui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rapa_Nui "Rapa Nui") by 1000 CE; and finally New Zealand by 1200 CE.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Gray-et-al2009-13)[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Stanley2004-16)[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-gibbons-17) Austronesians may have also reached as far as the [Americas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas "Americas"), although evidence for this remains inconclusive.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-18)[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-19)

### European exploration

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: European exploration")\]

Main article: [Exploration of the Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exploration_of_the_Pacific "Exploration of the Pacific")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Waldseemuller_map_2.jpg/330px-Waldseemuller_map_2.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Waldseemuller_map_2.jpg) _Universalis Cosmographia_, also known as the [Waldseemüller map](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waldseem%C3%BCller_map "Waldseemüller map"), dated 1507, was the first map to show the [Americas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas "Americas") separating two distinct oceans. South America was generally considered the [New World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_World "New World") and shows the name "America" for the first time, after [Amerigo Vespucci](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amerigo_Vespucci "Amerigo Vespucci")

The first contact of European navigators with the western edge of the Pacific Ocean was made by the Portuguese expeditions of [António de Abreu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Abreu "António de Abreu") and [Francisco Serrão](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_Serr%C3%A3o "Francisco Serrão"), via the [Lesser Sunda Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesser_Sunda_Islands "Lesser Sunda Islands"), to the [Maluku Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maluku_Islands "Maluku Islands"), in 1512,[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-20)[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-21) and with [Jorge Álvares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jorge_%C3%81lvares "Jorge Álvares")'s expedition to southern China in 1513,[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Porter,_Jonathan_1996-22) both ordered by [Afonso de Albuquerque](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afonso_de_Albuquerque "Afonso de Albuquerque") from [Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca "Portuguese Malacca").

The eastern side of the ocean was encountered by Spanish explorer [Vasco Núñez de Balboa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa "Vasco Núñez de Balboa") in 1513 after his expedition crossed the [Isthmus of Panama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama "Isthmus of Panama") and reached a new ocean.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Ober-23) He named it _Mar del Sur_ ("Sea of the South" or ["South Sea"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Seas "South Seas")) because the ocean was to the south of the coast of the isthmus where he first observed the Pacific.

In [1520](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1520 "1520"), navigator [Ferdinand Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan "Ferdinand Magellan") and his crew were the first to cross the Pacific in recorded history. They were part of a [Spanish expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation "Magellan–Elcano circumnavigation") to the [Spice Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spice_Islands "Spice Islands") that would eventually result in the first world [circumnavigation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumnavigation "Circumnavigation"). Magellan called the ocean _Pacífico_ (or "Pacific" meaning, "peaceful") because, after sailing through the stormy seas off [Cape Horn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Horn "Cape Horn"), the expedition found calm waters. The ocean was often called the **Sea of Magellan** in his honor until the eighteenth century.[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-24) Magellan stopped at one uninhabited Pacific island before stopping at [Guam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guam "Guam") in March 1521.[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-25)
Magellan himself died in the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines") in 1521. Spanish navigator [Juan Sebastián Elcano](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano "Juan Sebastián Elcano") led the remains of the expedition back to Spain across the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") and round the [Cape of Good Hope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope "Cape of Good Hope"), completing the first world [circumnavigation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumnavigation "Circumnavigation") in 1522.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-oceanario-26) Sailing around and east of the Moluccas, between 1525 and 1527, Portuguese expeditions encountered the [Caroline Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caroline_Islands "Caroline Islands"),[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-27) the [Aru Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aru_Islands_Regency "Aru Islands Regency"),[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-28)
and [Papua New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea "Papua New Guinea").[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Whiteway-29) In 1542–43 the Portuguese also reached Japan.[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-30)

In 1564, five Spanish ships carrying 379 soldiers crossed the Pacific from [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico") led by [Miguel López de Legazpi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_L%C3%B3pez_de_Legazpi "Miguel López de Legazpi"), and colonized the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines") and [Mariana Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariana_Islands "Mariana Islands").[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-HendersonDelpar2000-31)
For the remainder of the 16th century, Spain maintained military and mercantile control, with ships sailing from Mexico and [Peru](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peru "Peru") across the Pacific Ocean to the Philippines via [Guam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guam "Guam"), and establishing the [Spanish East Indies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_East_Indies "Spanish East Indies"). The [Manila galleons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manila_galleon "Manila galleon") operated for two and a half centuries, linking [Manila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manila "Manila") and [Acapulco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acapulco "Acapulco"), in one of the longest [trade routes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waterway "Waterway") in history. Spanish expeditions also arrived at [Tuvalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuvalu "Tuvalu"), the [Marquesas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marquesas_Islands "Marquesas Islands"), the [Cook Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cook_Islands "Cook Islands"), the [Solomon Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon_Islands "Solomon Islands"), [Vanuatu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanuatu "Vanuatu"), the [Marshalls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Islands "Marshall Islands") and the [Admiralty Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Admiralty_Islands "Admiralty Islands") in the South Pacific.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Fernandez-Armesto_2006_305%E2%80%93307-32)

Later, in the quest for [Terra Australis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terra_Australis "Terra Australis") ("the \[great\] Southern Land"), Spanish explorations in the 17th century, such as the expedition led by the Portuguese navigator [Pedro Fernandes de Queirós](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_Fernandes_de_Queir%C3%B3s "Pedro Fernandes de Queirós"), arrived at the [Pitcairn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pitcairn_Islands "Pitcairn Islands") and [Vanuatu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanuatu "Vanuatu") archipelagos, and sailed the [Torres Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torres_Strait "Torres Strait") between Australia and New Guinea, named after navigator [Luís Vaz de Torres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Vaz_de_Torres "Luís Vaz de Torres"). Dutch explorers, sailing around southern Africa, also engaged in exploration and trade; [Willem Janszoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Willem_Janszoon "Willem Janszoon"), made the first completely documented European landing in Australia (1606), in [Cape York Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_York_Peninsula "Cape York Peninsula"),[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-33) and [Abel Janszoon Tasman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abel_Tasman "Abel Tasman") circumnavigated and landed on parts of the Australian continental coast and arrived at [Tasmania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania") and New Zealand in 1642.[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-34)

In the 16th and 17th centuries, Spain considered the Pacific Ocean a _[mare clausum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mare_clausum "Mare clausum")_ – a sea closed to other naval powers. As the only known entrance from the Atlantic, the [Strait of Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan "Strait of Magellan") was at times patrolled by fleets sent to prevent the entrance of non-Spanish ships. On the western side of the Pacific Ocean the Dutch threatened the [Spanish Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines").[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-lytle-35)

The 18th century marked the beginning of major exploration by Russians in [Alaska](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alaska "Alaska") and the [Aleutian Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleutian_Islands "Aleutian Islands"), such as the [First Kamchatka expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Kamchatka_expedition "First Kamchatka expedition") and the [Great Northern Expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Northern_Expedition "Great Northern Expedition"), led by the Danish-born Russian navy officer [Vitus Bering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitus_Bering "Vitus Bering"). Spain also sent [expeditions to the Pacific Northwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_expeditions_to_the_Pacific_Northwest "Spanish expeditions to the Pacific Northwest"), reaching [Vancouver Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vancouver_Island "Vancouver Island") in southern Canada, and Alaska.
The French explored and colonized [Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia"), and the British made three voyages with [James Cook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Cook "James Cook") to the South Pacific and Australia, [Hawaii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii "Hawaii"), and the North American [Pacific Northwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Northwest "Pacific Northwest"). In 1768, [Pierre-Antoine Véron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Antoine_V%C3%A9ron "Pierre-Antoine Véron"), a young [astronomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomer "Astronomer") accompanying [Louis Antoine de Bougainville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Antoine_de_Bougainville "Louis Antoine de Bougainville") on his voyage of exploration, established the width of the Pacific with precision for the first time in history.[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Williams2004-36)

One of the earliest voyages of scientific exploration was organized by Spain in the [Malaspina Expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaspina_Expedition "Malaspina Expedition") of 1789–1794. It sailed vast areas of the Pacific, from Cape Horn to Alaska, Guam and the Philippines, [New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand "New Zealand"), Australia, and the South Pacific.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Fernandez-Armesto_2006_305%E2%80%93307-32)

### New Imperialism

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: New Imperialism")\]

See also: [New Imperialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Imperialism "New Imperialism") and [Pacific Century](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Century "Pacific Century")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Bathyscaphe_Trieste_with_USS_Lewis_%28DE-535%29_over_the_Marianas_Trench%2C_23_January_1960_%28NH_96797%29.jpg/250px-Bathyscaphe_Trieste_with_USS_Lewis_%28DE-535%29_over_the_Marianas_Trench%2C_23_January_1960_%28NH_96797%29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Bathyscaphe_Trieste_with_USS_Lewis_(DE-535)_over_the_Marianas_Trench,_23_January_1960_(NH_96797).jpg) The bathyscaphe _[Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste_(bathyscaphe) "Trieste (bathyscaphe)")_ before her record dive to the bottom of the [Mariana Trench](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariana_Trench "Mariana Trench"), 23 January 1960[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/TahitiDupetitThouars.jpg/250px-TahitiDupetitThouars.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:TahitiDupetitThouars.jpg) [Abel Aubert du Petit-Thouars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abel_Aubert_du_Petit-Thouars "Abel Aubert du Petit-Thouars") taking over [Tahiti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tahiti "Tahiti") on 9 September 1842

Growing [imperialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperialism "Imperialism") during the 19th century resulted in the occupation of much of Oceania by European powers, and later [by Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_colonial_empire "Japanese colonial empire") and the [by United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_interventions_by_the_United_States "Foreign interventions by the United States"). Significant contributions to [oceanographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanographic "Oceanographic") knowledge were made by the voyages of [HMS _Beagle_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Beagle "HMS Beagle") in the 1830s, with [Charles Darwin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Darwin "Charles Darwin") aboard;[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-38) [HMS _Challenger_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Challenger_(1858) "HMS Challenger (1858)") during the 1870s;[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-39) the [USS _Tuscarora_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Tuscarora_(1861) "USS Tuscarora (1861)") (1873–76);[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-40) and the German _Gazelle_ (1874–76).[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-41)

In Oceania, France obtained a leading position as imperial power after making [Tahiti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tahiti "Tahiti") and [New Caledonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Caledonia "New Caledonia") protectorates in 1842 and 1853, respectively.[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Asiapacific-42)
After navy visits to [Easter Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Easter_Island "Easter Island") in 1875 and 1887, Chilean navy officer [Policarpo Toro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policarpo_Toro "Policarpo Toro") negotiated the incorporation of the island into [Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile") with native [Rapanui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rapanui "Rapanui") in [1888](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1888_in_Chile "1888 in Chile"). By occupying Easter Island, Chile joined the imperial nations.[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-sater-43): 53  By 1900 nearly all Pacific islands were in control of Britain, France, United States, Germany, Japan, and Chile.[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Asiapacific-42)

In 1898 ( [Spanish–American War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War "Spanish–American War")), the United States gained control of [Guam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guam "Guam") and the Philippines from Spain.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-TewariAlvarez2008-44)

### 20th century

Prior to World War I United States attempted to purchase or at least establish a naval base in [Galápagos Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gal%C3%A1pagos_Islands "Galápagos Islands"). Ecuador's staunch resistance to a US purchase or bases in the islands can be credited to Chilean diplomacy, which in turn was informally backed on this issue by [Great Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland "United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland") and [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire "German Empire").[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Fischer2008-45)

The end of World War I strenghtened Japan and the United States in the Pacific, Japan in East Asia and United States mostly so in Latin America. Pacific rim countries like Colombia, Ecuador and Peru started to treat the United States as a hegemon, often seeking its mediation in international disputes.[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMares200168-46) Chile on the other hand attempted to resist United States influence.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMares200169-47)

The [Washington Naval Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_Naval_Treaty "Washington Naval Treaty") of 1921 is associated with various geopolitical effects in the Pacific Ocean, including the end of the [Anglo-Japanese Alliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Japanese_Alliance "Anglo-Japanese Alliance"). Although it was not part of the Washington Treaty in any way, the American delegates had made it clear that they would not agree to the treaty unless the British ended their alliance with the Japanese.[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHowarth1983167-48) The [1921 Imperial Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1921_Imperial_Conference "1921 Imperial Conference") earlier in the year had already decided not to renew the Alliance.[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-49) The regrouping the Royal Navy in 1921 also meant that it left the South Pacific that year.[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Sater91-50) This weakened the position of Britain's old-time ally [Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile") and increased the influence of the United States in the Americas. Indirectly, the withdrawal emboldened Peruvian and Bolivian claims against Chile as these two states were often supported diplomatically by the United States.[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Sater91-50)[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMares200165-51)

Japan occupied many Pacific islands during the [Pacific War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_War "Pacific War") (1941–1945); however, by the end of that war, Japan was defeated and the [U.S. Pacific Fleet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Navy "United States Navy") was the virtual master of the ocean. The Japanese-ruled [Northern Mariana Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands "Northern Mariana Islands") came under the control of the United States.[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-52)

Since the end of World War II, many former colonies in the Pacific have become independent [states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state "Sovereign state") (→ [Decolonisation of Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decolonisation_of_Asia "Decolonisation of Asia")).

## Geography

Tops of [thunderclouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumulonimbus_cloud "Cumulonimbus cloud") are also visible.[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Pacific_Basin_Island_Geography.jpg/330px-Pacific_Basin_Island_Geography.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pacific_Basin_Island_Geography.jpg) The island geography of the Pacific Ocean Basin[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Pacific_Culture_Areas.png/500px-Pacific_Culture_Areas.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pacific_Culture_Areas.png) The three major cultural areas of the [Pacific Ocean islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_in_the_Pacific_Ocean "List of islands in the Pacific Ocean"): [Micronesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micronesia "Micronesia"), [Melanesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melanesia "Melanesia") and [Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia")

The Pacific separates Asia and Australia from the Americas. It may be further subdivided by the equator into northern (North Pacific) and southern (South Pacific) portions. It extends from the [Antarctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic "Antarctic") region in the South to the [Arctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic "Arctic") in the north.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-ebc-1) The Pacific Ocean encompasses approximately one-third of the Earth's surface, having an area of 165,200,000 km2 (63,800,000 sq mi) – larger than Earth's entire landmass combined, 150,000,000 km2 (58,000,000 sq mi).[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-53)

Extending approximately 8,400 nautical miles (15,600 km; 9,700 mi) from the [Bering Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bering_Sea "Bering Sea") in the [Arctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic "Arctic") to the northern extent of the circumpolar [Southern Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Ocean "Southern Ocean") at [60°S](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/60th_parallel_south "60th parallel south") (older definitions extend it to [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica")'s [Ross Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ross_Sea "Ross Sea")), the Pacific reaches its greatest east–west width at about [5°N latitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5th_parallel_north "5th parallel north"), where it stretches approximately 10,700 nautical miles (19,800 km; 12,300 mi) from Indonesia to the coast of [Colombia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombia "Colombia") – halfway around the world, and more than five times the diameter of the Moon.[\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Nuttall2005-54) Its geographic center is in eastern [Kiribati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiribati "Kiribati") south of [Kiritimati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiritimati "Kiritimati"), just west from [Starbuck Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Starbuck_Island "Starbuck Island") at

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/WMA_button2b.png/20px-WMA_button2b.png) WikiMiniAtlas

[4°58′S158°45′W﻿ / ﻿4.97°S 158.75°W﻿ / -4.97; -158.75](https://geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Pacific_Ocean&params=4.97_S_158.75_W_type:landmark_region:FR_scale:10000).[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-55) The lowest known point on Earth – the [Mariana Trench](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariana_Trench "Mariana Trench") – lies 10,911 m (5,966 [fathoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fathom "Fathom"); 35,797 [ft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foot_(unit) "Foot (unit)")) below sea level. Its average depth is 4,280 m (2,340 fathoms; 14,040 ft), putting the total water volume at roughly 710,000,000 cubic kilometres (170,000,000 cu mi).[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-ebc-1)

Due to the effects of [plate tectonics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plate_tectonics "Plate tectonics"), the Pacific Ocean is currently shrinking by roughly 2.5 centimetres (0.98 in) per year on three sides, roughly averaging 0.52 km2 (0.20 sq mi) a year. By contrast, the Atlantic Ocean is increasing in size.[\[56\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-56)[\[57\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Young2009-57)

Along the Pacific Ocean's irregular western margins lie many seas, the largest of which are the [Celebes Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celebes_Sea "Celebes Sea"), [Coral Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral_Sea "Coral Sea"), [East China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_China_Sea "East China Sea") (East Sea), [Philippine Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippine_Sea "Philippine Sea"), [Sea of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Japan "Sea of Japan"), [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea") (South Sea), [Sulu Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulu_Sea "Sulu Sea"), [Tasman Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tasman_Sea "Tasman Sea"), and [Yellow Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_Sea "Yellow Sea") (West Sea of Korea). The [Indonesian Seaway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geology_of_Indonesia#Indonesian_Seaway "Geology of Indonesia") (including the [Strait of Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca "Strait of Malacca") and [Torres Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torres_Strait "Torres Strait")) joins the Pacific and the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") to the west, and [Drake Passage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drake_Passage "Drake Passage") and the [Strait of Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan "Strait of Magellan") link the Pacific with the Atlantic Ocean on the east. To the north, the [Bering Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bering_Strait "Bering Strait") connects the Pacific with the [Arctic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic_Ocean "Arctic Ocean").[\[58\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Organization1953-58)

As the Pacific straddles the [180th meridian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/180th_meridian "180th meridian"), the _West Pacific_ (or _western Pacific_, near Asia) is in the [Eastern Hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Hemisphere "Eastern Hemisphere"), while the _East Pacific_ (or _eastern Pacific_, near the Americas) is in the [Western Hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Hemisphere "Western Hemisphere").[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Lydia-59)

The Southern Pacific Ocean harbors the [Southeast Indian Ridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Indian_Ridge "Southeast Indian Ridge") crossing from south of Australia turning into the [Pacific-Antarctic Ridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific-Antarctic_Ridge "Pacific-Antarctic Ridge") (north of the [South Pole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Pole "South Pole")) and merges with another ridge (south of South America) to form the [East Pacific Rise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Pacific_Rise "East Pacific Rise") which also connects with another ridge (south of North America) which overlooks the [Juan de Fuca Ridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_de_Fuca_Ridge "Juan de Fuca Ridge").

For most of Magellan's voyage from the [Strait of Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan "Strait of Magellan") to the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"), the explorer indeed found the ocean peaceful; however, the Pacific is not always peaceful. Many [tropical storms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_storm "Tropical storm") batter the islands of the Pacific.[\[60\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-60) The lands around the [Pacific Rim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Rim "Pacific Rim") are full of [volcanoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") and often affected by [earthquakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earthquake "Earthquake"). [Tsunamis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsunami "Tsunami"), caused by underwater earthquakes, have devastated many islands and in some cases destroyed entire towns.[\[61\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Bryant2008-61)

The [Martin Waldseemüller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Waldseem%C3%BCller "Martin Waldseemüller") map of 1507 was the first to show the Americas separating two distinct oceans.[\[62\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-62) Later, the [Diogo Ribeiro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogo_Ribeiro_(cartographer) "Diogo Ribeiro (cartographer)") map of 1529 was the first to show the Pacific at about its proper size.[\[63\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-63)

### Landmasses and islands

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Landmasses and islands")\]

Main article: [List of islands in the Pacific Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_in_the_Pacific_Ocean "List of islands in the Pacific Ocean")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/South_Tarawa_from_the_air.jpg/250px-South_Tarawa_from_the_air.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:South_Tarawa_from_the_air.jpg) [Tarawa Atoll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarawa_Atoll "Tarawa Atoll") in [Kiribati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiribati "Kiribati")

The Pacific Ocean has most of the islands in the world. There are about 25,000 islands in the Pacific Ocean.[\[64\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-64)[\[65\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-65)[\[66\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-66) The islands entirely within the Pacific Ocean can be divided into three main groups known as [Micronesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micronesia "Micronesia"), [Melanesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melanesia "Melanesia") and [Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia"). Micronesia, which lies north of the equator and west of the [International Date Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Date_Line "International Date Line"), includes the [Mariana Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariana_Islands "Mariana Islands") in the northwest, the [Caroline Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caroline_Islands "Caroline Islands") in the center, the [Marshall Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Islands "Marshall Islands") to the east and the islands of [Kiribati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiribati "Kiribati") in the southeast.[\[67\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-AAE-67)[\[68\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-LalFortune2000p63-68)

Melanesia, to the southwest, includes [New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Guinea "New Guinea"), the world's second largest island after [Greenland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenland "Greenland") and by far the largest of the Pacific islands. The other main Melanesian groups from north to south are the [Bismarck Archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bismarck_Archipelago "Bismarck Archipelago"), the [Solomon Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon_Islands "Solomon Islands"), [Santa Cruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Cruz_Islands "Santa Cruz Islands"), [Vanuatu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanuatu "Vanuatu"), [Fiji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiji "Fiji") and [New Caledonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Caledonia "New Caledonia").[\[69\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-West2009-69)

The largest area, [Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia"), stretching from [Hawaii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii "Hawaii") in the north to New Zealand in the south, also encompasses [Tuvalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuvalu "Tuvalu"), [Tokelau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokelau "Tokelau"), [Samoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samoa "Samoa"), [Tonga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonga "Tonga") and the [Kermadec Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kermadec_Islands "Kermadec Islands") to the west, the [Cook Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cook_Islands "Cook Islands"), [Society Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_Islands "Society Islands") and [Austral Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austral_Islands "Austral Islands") in the center, and the [Marquesas Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marquesas_Islands "Marquesas Islands"), [Tuamotu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuamotus "Tuamotus"), [Mangareva Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangareva_Islands "Mangareva Islands"), and [Easter Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Easter_Island "Easter Island") to the east.[\[70\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-DunfordRidgell1996-70)

Islands in the Pacific Ocean are of four basic types: continental islands, high islands, coral reefs and uplifted coral platforms. Continental islands lie outside the andesite line and include New Guinea, the islands of New Zealand, and the Philippines. Some of these islands are structurally associated with nearby continents. High islands are of volcanic origin, and many contain active volcanoes. Among these are [Bougainville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bougainville_Island "Bougainville Island"), Hawaii, and the Solomon Islands.[\[71\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-GillespieClague2009-71)

The [coral reefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral_reef "Coral reef") of the South Pacific are low-lying structures that have built up on basaltic lava flows under the ocean's surface. One of the most dramatic is the [Great Barrier Reef](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Barrier_Reef "Great Barrier Reef") off northeastern Australia with chains of reef patches. A second island type formed of coral is the uplifted coral platform, which is usually slightly larger than the low coral islands. Examples include [Banaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banaba_Island "Banaba Island") (formerly Ocean Island) and [Makatea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makatea "Makatea") in the Tuamotu group of [French Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Polynesia "French Polynesia").[\[72\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-72)[\[73\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-73)

## Water characteristics

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Water characteristics")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Sunset_Marina.JPG/250px-Sunset_Marina.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sunset_Marina.JPG) Sunset in [Monterey County, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monterey_County,_California "Monterey County, California"), U.S.

The volume of the Pacific Ocean, representing about 50.1 percent of the world's oceanic water, has been estimated at some 714 million cubic kilometers (171 million cubic miles).[\[74\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-74) Surface water temperatures in the Pacific can vary from −1.4 °C (29.5 °F), the freezing point of seawater, in the poleward areas to about 30 °C (86 °F) near the equator.[\[75\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Mongillo2000-75) [Salinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salinity "Salinity") also varies latitudinally, reaching a maximum of 37 parts per thousand in the southeastern area. The water near the equator, which can have a salinity as low as 34 parts per thousand, is less salty than that found in the mid-latitudes because of abundant equatorial precipitation throughout the year. The lowest counts of less than 32 parts per thousand are found in the far north as less evaporation of seawater takes place in these frigid areas.[\[76\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-76) The motion of Pacific waters is generally clockwise in the [Northern Hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere "Northern Hemisphere") (the [North Pacific gyre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_gyre "Ocean gyre")) and counter-clockwise in the [Southern Hemisphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere "Southern Hemisphere"). The [North Equatorial Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Equatorial_Current "North Equatorial Current"), driven westward along [latitude 15°N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/15th_parallel_north "15th parallel north") by the [trade winds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_winds "Trade winds"), turns north near the Philippines to become the warm Japan or [Kuroshio Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuroshio_Current "Kuroshio Current").[\[77\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-77)

Turning eastward at about [45°N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/45th_parallel_north "45th parallel north"), the Kuroshio forks and some water moves northward as the [Aleutian Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleutian_Current "Aleutian Current"), while the rest turns southward to rejoin the North Equatorial Current.[\[78\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-78) The Aleutian Current branches as it approaches North America and forms the base of a counter-clockwise circulation in the [Bering Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bering_Sea "Bering Sea"). Its southern arm becomes the chilled slow, south-flowing [California Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Current "California Current").[\[79\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-79) The [South Equatorial Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Equatorial_Current "South Equatorial Current"), flowing west along the equator, swings southward east of [New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Guinea "New Guinea"), turns east at about [50°S](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/50th_parallel_south "50th parallel south"), and joins the main westerly circulation of the South Pacific, which includes the Earth-circling [Antarctic Circumpolar Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Circumpolar_Current "Antarctic Circumpolar Current"). As it approaches the Chilean coast, the South Equatorial Current divides; one branch flows around [Cape Horn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Horn "Cape Horn") and the other turns north to form the Peru or [Humboldt Current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humboldt_Current "Humboldt Current").[\[80\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-80)

## Climate

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=15 "Edit section: Climate")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/El_nino_north_american_weather.png/330px-El_nino_north_american_weather.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:El_nino_north_american_weather.png) Impact of [El Niño](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o "El Niño") and [La Niña](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Ni%C3%B1a "La Niña") on North America[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Tip_1979-10-12_0533Z.png/250px-Tip_1979-10-12_0533Z.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Tip_1979-10-12_0533Z.png) [Typhoon Tip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typhoon_Tip "Typhoon Tip") at global peak intensity on 12 October 1979

The climate patterns of the Northern and Southern Hemispheres generally mirror each other. The [trade winds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_winds "Trade winds") in the southern and eastern Pacific are remarkably steady while conditions in the North Pacific are far more varied with, for example, cold winter temperatures on the east coast of Russia contrasting with the milder weather off [British Columbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Columbia "British Columbia") during the winter months due to the preferred flow of [ocean currents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_current "Ocean current").[\[81\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-81)

In the tropical and subtropical Pacific, the [El Niño Southern Oscillation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o_Southern_Oscillation "El Niño Southern Oscillation") (ENSO) affects weather conditions. To determine the phase of ENSO, the most recent three-month [sea surface temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature "Sea surface temperature") average for the area approximately 3,000 km (1,900 mi) to the southeast of [Hawaii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii "Hawaii") is computed, and if the region is more than 0.5 °C (0.9 °F) above or below normal for that period, then an [El Niño](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o "El Niño") or [La Niña](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Ni%C3%B1a "La Niña") is considered in progress.[\[82\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-82)

In September 2025, NOAA reported that global ocean surface temperatures remained at near-record levels, with June–August 2025 ranking as the third warmest in their 176-year record.[\[83\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-83)

In the tropical western Pacific, the [monsoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoon "Monsoon") and the related [wet season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wet_season "Wet season") during the summer months contrast with dry winds in the winter which blow over the ocean from the Asian landmass.[\[84\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-84) Worldwide, [tropical cyclone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_cyclone "Tropical cyclone") activity peaks in late summer, when the difference between temperatures aloft and sea surface temperatures is the greatest; however, each particular basin has its own seasonal patterns. On a worldwide scale, May is the least active month, while September is the most active month. November is the only month in which all the [tropical cyclone basins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins "Tropical cyclone basins") are active.[\[85\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-85) The Pacific hosts the two most active [tropical cyclone basins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins "Tropical cyclone basins"), which are the northwestern Pacific and the eastern Pacific. [Pacific hurricanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_hurricane "Pacific hurricane") form south of Mexico, sometimes striking the western Mexican coast and occasionally the Southwestern United States between June and October, while [typhoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typhoon "Typhoon") forming in the northwestern Pacific moving into southeast and east Asia from May to December. Tropical cyclones also form in the [South Pacific basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Pacific_tropical_cyclone "South Pacific tropical cyclone"), where they occasionally impact island nations.[\[86\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-86)

In the arctic, icing from October to May can present a hazard for shipping while persistent fog occurs from June to December.[\[87\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-87) A climatological low in the Gulf of Alaska keeps the southern coast wet and mild during the winter months. The [Westerlies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westerlies "Westerlies") and associated [jet stream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jet_stream "Jet stream") within the Mid-Latitudes can be particularly strong, especially in the Southern Hemisphere, due to the temperature difference between the tropics and Antarctica,[\[88\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Stimac-88) which records the coldest temperature readings on the planet. In the Southern hemisphere, because of the stormy and cloudy conditions associated with [extratropical cyclones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone "Extratropical cyclone") riding the jet stream, it is usual to refer to the Westerlies as the Roaring Forties, Furious Fifties and Shrieking Sixties according to the varying degrees of latitude.[\[89\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-89)

## Geology

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=16 "Edit section: Geology")\]

Main articles: [Geology of the Pacific Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geology_of_the_Pacific_Ocean "Geology of the Pacific Ocean") and [Pacific plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_plate "Pacific plate")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Pacific_Ring_of_Fire.svg/500px-Pacific_Ring_of_Fire.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pacific_Ring_of_Fire.svg) A [Ring of Fire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ring_of_Fire "Ring of Fire"); the Pacific is ringed by many volcanoes and [oceanic trenches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_trench "Oceanic trench"). This map does not show the [Cascadia subduction zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cascadia_subduction_zone "Cascadia subduction zone") along part of the west coast of North America, whose trench is completely buried in sediments.[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Ulawun.jpg/250px-Ulawun.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ulawun.jpg) A [stratovolcano](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stratovolcano "Stratovolcano") in [Ulawun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ulawun "Ulawun") on the island of [New Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Britain "New Britain") in [Papua New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea "Papua New Guinea")[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Mount_Saint_Helens%2C_June_2020.jpg/250px-Mount_Saint_Helens%2C_June_2020.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Mount_Saint_Helens,_June_2020.jpg) [Mount St. Helens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_St._Helens "Mount St. Helens") in [Skamania County, Washington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skamania_County,_Washington "Skamania County, Washington"), U.S. in 2020

The ocean was first mapped by [Abraham Ortelius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abraham_Ortelius "Abraham Ortelius"); he called it Maris Pacifici following [Ferdinand Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan "Ferdinand Magellan")'s description of it as "a pacific sea" during his circumnavigation from 1519 to 1522. To Magellan, it seemed much more calm (pacific) than the Atlantic.[\[90\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Library2006-90)

The [andesite line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andesite_line "Andesite line") is the most significant regional distinction in the Pacific. A petrologic boundary, it separates the deeper, [mafic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mafic "Mafic") [igneous rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igneous_rock "Igneous rock") of the Central Pacific Basin from the partially submerged continental areas of [felsic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felsic "Felsic") igneous rock on its margins.[\[91\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-TrentHazlett2010-91) The andesite line follows the western edge of the islands off California and passes south of the [Aleutian arc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleutian_Islands "Aleutian Islands"), along the eastern edge of the [Kamchatka Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamchatka_Peninsula "Kamchatka Peninsula"), the [Kuril Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuril_Islands "Kuril Islands"), Japan, the [Mariana Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariana_Islands "Mariana Islands"), the [Solomon Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon_Islands "Solomon Islands"), and New Zealand's [North Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Island "North Island").[\[92\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-LalFortune2000p4-92)[\[93\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Mueller-Dombois1998-93)

The dissimilarity continues northeastward along the western edge of the [Andes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andes "Andes") [Cordillera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Cordillera "American Cordillera") along South America to Mexico, returning then to the islands off California. Indonesia, the Philippines, Japan, New Guinea, and New Zealand lie outside the andesite line.

Within the closed loop of the andesite line are most of the deep troughs, submerged volcanic mountains, and oceanic volcanic islands that characterize the Pacific basin. Here basaltic lavas gently flow out of rifts to build huge dome-shaped volcanic mountains whose eroded summits form island arcs, chains, and clusters. Outside the andesite line, volcanism is of the explosive type, and the Pacific [Ring of Fire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ring_of_Fire "Ring of Fire") is the world's foremost belt of explosive [volcanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanism "Volcanism").[\[67\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-AAE-67) The Ring of Fire is named after the several hundred active volcanoes that sit above the various subduction zones.

The Pacific Ocean is the only ocean which is mostly bounded by [subduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subduction "Subduction") zones. Only the central part of the North American coast and the Antarctic and Australian coasts have no nearby subduction zones.

### Geological history

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=17 "Edit section: Geological history")\]

The Pacific Ocean was born 750million years ago at the breakup of [Rodinia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodinia "Rodinia"), although it is generally called the [Panthalassa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panthalassa "Panthalassa") until the breakup of [Pangea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pangea "Pangea"), about 200million years ago.[\[94\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-geol.umd.edu-94) The oldest Pacific Ocean floor is only around 180 [Ma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mega-annum "Mega-annum") old, with older crust subducted by now.[\[95\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-MussettKhan2000-95)

### Seamount chains

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=18 "Edit section: Seamount chains")\]

The Pacific Ocean contains several long [seamount](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seamount "Seamount") chains, formed by [hotspot volcanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hotspot_(geology) "Hotspot (geology)"). These include the [Hawaiian–Emperor seamount chain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaiian%E2%80%93Emperor_seamount_chain "Hawaiian–Emperor seamount chain") and the [Louisville Ridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisville_Ridge "Louisville Ridge").

## Economy

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=19 "Edit section: Economy")\]

The exploitation of the Pacific's mineral wealth is hampered by the ocean's great depths. In shallow waters of the continental shelves off the coasts of Australia and New Zealand, [petroleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum "Petroleum") and [natural gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas "Natural gas") are extracted, and [pearls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pearl "Pearl") are harvested along the coasts of Australia, Japan, [Papua New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea "Papua New Guinea"), Nicaragua, Panama, and the Philippines, although in sharply declining volume in some cases.[\[96\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-96)

### Fishing

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=20 "Edit section: Fishing")\]

[Fish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fish "Fish") are an important economic asset in the Pacific. The shallower shoreline waters of the continents and the more temperate islands yield [herring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herring "Herring"), [salmon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salmon "Salmon"), [sardines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sardine "Sardine"), [snapper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australasian_snapper "Australasian snapper"), [swordfish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swordfish "Swordfish"), and [tuna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuna "Tuna"), as well as [shellfish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shellfish "Shellfish").[\[97\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-97) [Overfishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overfishing "Overfishing") has become a serious problem in some areas. Overfishing leads to depleted fish populations and closed fisheries, causing both economic and ecologic consequences.[\[98\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-98) For example, catches in the rich fishing grounds of the [Okhotsk Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk "Sea of Okhotsk") off the Russian coast have been reduced by at least half since the 1990s as a result of overfishing.[\[99\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-99)

## Environment

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=21 "Edit section: Environment")\]

See also: [Great Pacific Garbage Patch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pacific_Garbage_Patch "Great Pacific Garbage Patch") and [Environmental impact of shipping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_impact_of_shipping "Environmental impact of shipping")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Pacific-garbage-patch-map_2010_noaamdp.jpg/500px-Pacific-garbage-patch-map_2010_noaamdp.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pacific-garbage-patch-map_2010_noaamdp.jpg) Pacific Ocean currents have created three islands of debris.[\[100\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-100)[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Marine_debris_on_Hawaiian_coast.jpg/250px-Marine_debris_on_Hawaiian_coast.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Marine_debris_on_Hawaiian_coast.jpg) [Marine debris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_debris "Marine debris") on a [Hawaiian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii "Hawaii") coast in 2008

The Northwestern Pacific Ocean is most susceptible to micro plastic pollution due to its proximity to highly populated countries like Japan and China.[\[101\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-101) The quantity of small plastic fragments floating in the north-east Pacific Ocean increased a hundredfold between 1972 and 2012.[\[102\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-102)\[ _[verification needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability "Wikipedia:Verifiability")_\] The ever-growing [Great Pacific Garbage Patch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pacific_Garbage_Patch "Great Pacific Garbage Patch") between California and Japan is three times the size of France.[\[103\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-103) An estimated 80,000 metric tons of plastic inhabit the patch, totaling 1.8trillion pieces.[\[104\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-104)

[Marine pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_pollution "Marine pollution") is a generic term for the harmful entry into the ocean of chemicals or particles. The main culprits are those using the rivers for disposing of their waste.[\[105\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-news.nationalgeographic.com-105) The rivers then empty into the ocean, often also bringing chemicals used as [fertilizers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fertilizer "Fertilizer") in agriculture. The excess of oxygen-depleting chemicals in the water leads to [hypoxia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypoxia_(environmental) "Hypoxia (environmental)") and the creation of a [dead zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_zone_(ecology) "Dead zone (ecology)").[\[106\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-106)

[Marine debris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_debris "Marine debris"), also known as marine litter, is human-created waste that has ended up floating in a lake, sea, ocean, or waterway. Oceanic debris tends to accumulate at the center of gyres and coastlines, frequently washing aground where it is known as beach litter.[\[105\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-news.nationalgeographic.com-105)

In addition, the Pacific Ocean has served as the crash site of satellites, including [Mars 96](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars_96 "Mars 96"), [Fobos-Grunt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fobos-Grunt "Fobos-Grunt"), and [Upper Atmosphere Research Satellite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_Atmosphere_Research_Satellite "Upper Atmosphere Research Satellite").

### Nuclear waste

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=22 "Edit section: Nuclear waste")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/20200926fukushima01.jpg/250px-20200926fukushima01.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:20200926fukushima01.jpg) In 2020, [Japanese Prime Minister Suga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoshihide_Suga "Yoshihide Suga") declined to drink the bottle of [Fukushima's treated radioactive water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discharge_of_radioactive_water_of_the_Fukushima_Daiichi_Nuclear_Power_Plant "Discharge of radioactive water of the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant") that he was holding, which would otherwise be discharged to the Pacific.[\[107\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Asahi_Shimbun_20201103-107)

From 1946 to 1958, [Marshall Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Islands "Marshall Islands") served as the [Pacific Proving Grounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Proving_Grounds "Pacific Proving Grounds"), designated by the United States, and played host to a total of 67 [nuclear tests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_test "Nuclear test") conducted across various atolls.[\[108\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-108)[\[109\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-109) Several [nuclear weapons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_weapon "Nuclear weapon") were lost in the Pacific Ocean,[\[110\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-110) including one-megaton bomb that was lost during the [1965 Philippine Sea A-4 incident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1965_Philippine_Sea_A-4_incident "1965 Philippine Sea A-4 incident").[\[111\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-111)

In 2021, the [discharge of radioactive water from the Fukushima nuclear plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discharge_of_radioactive_water_of_the_Fukushima_Daiichi_Nuclear_Power_Plant "Discharge of radioactive water of the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant") into the Pacific Ocean over a course of 30 years was approved by the Japanese Cabinet. The Cabinet concluded the radioactive water would have been diluted to drinkable standard.[\[112\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-BBC_20210413-112) Apart from dumping, [leakage of tritium into the Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leakage_of_radioactive_water_of_the_Fukushima_Daiichi_Nuclear_Power_Plant "Leakage of radioactive water of the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant") was estimated to be between 20 and 40 trillion [Bqs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becquerel "Becquerel") from 2011 to 2013, according to the Fukushima plant.[\[113\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Scientific_American_20130813-113)

### Deep sea mining

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Ocean&action=edit&section=23 "Edit section: Deep sea mining")\]

An emerging threat for the Pacific Ocean is the development of [deep-sea mining](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep-sea_mining "Deep-sea mining").
Deep-sea mining is aimed at extracting [manganese nodules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manganese_nodules "Manganese nodules") that contain minerals such as magnesium, nickel, copper, zinc and cobalt. The largest deposits of these are found in the Pacific Ocean between Mexico and Hawaii in the [Clarion Clipperton fracture zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Clarion_Clipperton_fracture_zone&action=edit&redlink=1 "Clarion Clipperton fracture zone (page does not exist)").

Deep-sea mining for manganese nodules appears to have drastic consequences for the ocean. It disrupts deep-sea ecosystems and may cause irreversible damage to fragile marine habitats.[\[114\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-Halfar2007-114) Sediment stirring and chemical pollution threaten various marine animals. In addition, the mining process can lead to greenhouse gas emissions and promote further climate change. Preventing deep-sea mining is therefore important to ensure the long-term health of the ocean.[\[115\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean#cite_note-115)

Pacific Ocean Is Much Bigger Than You Think 🌊 The Deepest Place on Earth ⬇️ Mariana Trench

333KLikes

9,540,210Views

2025Feb 8

Did you know how truly massive the Pacific Ocean is? Spanning over 12,000 miles 🌎 from Asia to South America, it’s so vast that you could line up five Moons 🌕 across it! Not only that, but this ocean is larger than all the land on Earth combined, with enough space left over to fit 23 Texases! 🤠 Covering nearly half of the planet's water surface 💦 and a third of Earth's total area, the Pacific Ocean is an endless watery expanse. From space 🚀 it’s almost all you can see, just water with tiny glimpses of America, Asia, and Australia at the edges. But the Pacific’s size isn’t its only impressive feature. It’s home to the planet's three deepest points: the Tonga Trench, the Philippine Trench, and the Mariana Trench. These underwater abysses are so deep they could swallow Mount Everest 🏔️ two Burj Khalifas, and one and a half Eiffel Towers 🗼 stacked on top of each other!

Did you know 71% of the planet is water? And that the world’s biggest ocean – the Pacific – is larger than all seven continents combined? But their impact on the world is even great than their size. Oceans produce over half of the world’s oxygen (through underwater plants), plus new medicines and jobs.

Yet despite this, there are being maltreated. Overfishing, deep-sea mining, fossil fuels and garbage pollution mean that [50% of coral reefs](https://sloactive.com/coral-reef-crisis-guide/#:~:text=Recent%20studies%20have%20revealed%20that,over%20the%20next%2030%20years.) have been destroyed and whales, sea turtles, sea otters and sea lions are endangered.

So to ‘ignite collective action to revitalise the ocean and all it sustains’ this [World Ocean Day](https://www.un.org/en/observances/oceans-day) (8 June 2022), here are 7 incredible facts about the biggest ocean on the planet.

## **Crazy facts about the world’s biggest ocean**

### **It’s bigger than Mars**

![world's biggest ocean](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

With an area of 60 million square miles (165,250,000 square km) the Pacific ocean is simply humongous. How big? It’s bigger than Mars! Covering approximately 30% of the world’s surface, it stretches from China to California – bordering 41 countries – with North and South Americas lying to the East and Australia and Asia to the West. To get an idea, all 7 continents could fit into the Pacific ocean basin (with room left over).

### **‘The Ring of Fire’ exists here**

![world's biggest ocean](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Encircling the Pacific ocean, you’ll find a 40,000km long horseshoe-shaped rim that contains 75% of all the world’s active volcanoes. The Circum-Pacific Belt, commonly known as ‘The Ring of Fire’ is a natural disaster waiting to happen. In fact, the 452 volcanoes here cause approximately [90% of the world’s earthquakes](https://education.nationalgeographic.org/resource/plate-tectonics-ring-fire). Several tectonic plates here (including Pacific, Nazca, Juan de Fuca, Cocos, Indian-Australian, Philippine and North American Plates) could collide and sink beneath one another, causing seismic tectonic activity. Some notable tragedies include the Valdivia Earthquake of Chile in 1960 and Japan earthquake in 2011.

### **The world’s deepest place** is found here

The Pacific isn’t just the world’s biggest ocean, it’s also the deepest (with an average depth of 13,000 feet). And contains the world’s deepest point: the Mariana Trench. In the West Pacific, to the East of the Philippines, you’ll find this crescent-shaped oceanic trench (created by tectonic plate movements that create a depression on the seafloor). Staggeringly, it’s almost 7 miles deep. That’s 11,034 meters (36,201 feet). To give it some perspective, it’s even deeper than Mount Everest is high.

### The **Great Barrier Reef** is in the world’s biggest ocean

![Great Barrier Reef](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Glowing off the north-eastern coast of Australia, the Great Barrier Reef is a pearl in the Pacific. It’s registered as a UNESCO World Heritage site and listed as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World. And unsurprisingly, visitors flock here to snorkel in its crystal-clear waters, soak in warm sun and marvel at colourful coral. It is the largest living organism in the world (stretching 2,600km) and can be seen from space. There’s a reason it’s top on bucket lists of explorers everywhere.

### **25,000 islands dot the water**

![world's biggest ocean](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

25,000 unique islands float in Pacific waters. And lucky for travellers – many of them are begging to be explored. These range from New Zealand’s South Island to dreamy Hawaii in the North Pacific. A hotspot for ex-pats who visit for its white-sand beaches and pristine surfing waves. Other standouts include New Caledonia, Tonga, Samoa and Fiji. Plus Australia’s Tasmania and Japan’s Hokkaido and Honshu.

### Atolls are everywhere here in the world’s biggest ocean

![world's biggest ocean](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

You can spot many coral rings – ‘atolls’ – that encircle lagoons and small islands in the Pacific. They’re caused by volcanic underwater activity and look as postcard-perfect as it gets. And with the majority of atolls existing here, they’re a unique part of the world’s biggest ocean.

### **The Great Pacific Garbage Patch**

https://www.instagram.com/p/CZRISz4vBoD/?utm\_source=ig\_web\_copy\_link

Stretching from West Coast California to Japan, ‘The Great Pacific Garbage Patch’ is a brutal reminder of our need to protect the world’s oceans. In the north Pacific sea, this huge chunk of marine debris is three times the size of France. A large system of swirling ocean currents collate the plastics together into this enormous patch.

### The world’s biggest ocean is shrinking

![world's biggest ocean](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Yes, you heard it right. The Pacific Ocean is shrinking 0.5 square kilometres. This is due to the Pacific’s three subduction zones, when it submerges under other plates.
