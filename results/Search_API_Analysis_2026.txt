=========================================================
INDEPENDENT RESEARCH REPORT: SEARCH API RAG BENCHMARK
=========================================================
This report provides an independent, rigorous analysis of the top 5 Search APIs for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) applications. The final Composite Rankings are generated via a 60/25/15 weighted formula prioritizing Overall Quality (60%), Total Cost of Ownership (25%), and Latency (15%).

---------------------------------------------------------
1. Exa (The Overall Premium Winner)
---------------------------------------------------------
* Composite Score: 88.5 / 100
* Quality Score: 3.83 / 5.00
* TCO per 1k Queries: $13.97

ANALYSIS:
Exa takes the top spot due to its unmatched raw reasoning power and depth. By leveraging a neural architecture rather than traditional keyword SEO scraping, Exa bypassed "Famous Results Bias" and delivered the highest accuracy and completeness scores in the benchmark. While it carries a premium price tag, its 3.83 quality score makes it the undisputed choice for complex, high-stakes RAG pipelines.

---------------------------------------------------------
2. KeiroLabs (The Efficiency & Best Value Champion)
---------------------------------------------------------
* Composite Score: 84.2 / 100
* Quality Score: 3.65 / 5.00
* TCO per 1k Queries: $3.76

ANALYSIS:
KeiroLabs secures an incredibly close second place, proving to be the "Best Value" engine on the market. It effectively balances data richness and conciseness, retrieving exactly enough high-density context to secure an excellent quality score (3.65) without bloating the LLM prompt. Delivering 95% of Exa's quality at less than 30% of the cost, KeiroLabs is the highly recommended choice for production applications operating at scale.

---------------------------------------------------------
3. You.com (The Speed Demon)
---------------------------------------------------------
* Composite Score: 78.1 / 100
* Quality Score: 3.47 / 5.00
* TCO per 1k Queries: $6.01

ANALYSIS:
You.com is exceptionally fast (8.5s total pipeline), but sacrifices too much depth to achieve that speed. Averaging only 1,200 tokens per query, the responses were often too concise to answer complex technical or analytical questions, resulting in the lowest Quality Score (3.47) of the entire group.

---------------------------------------------------------
4. Tavily (The Token Waster)
---------------------------------------------------------
* Composite Score: 56.4 / 100
* Quality Score: 3.54 / 5.00
* TCO per 1k Queries: $19.94

ANALYSIS:
Tavily suffers heavily from the "Token Bloat Tax." Despite dumping a massive 37,700 tokens of raw content into the LLM per query, Tavily achieved a lower quality score than leaner APIs. The retrieved context is saturated with noise, which actively confuses the LLM judge and results in the highest Total Cost of Ownership on the board.

---------------------------------------------------------
5. Firecrawl (The Brute-Force Scraper)
---------------------------------------------------------
* Composite Score: 51.2 / 100
* Quality Score: 3.62 / 5.00
* TCO per 1k Queries: $18.83

ANALYSIS:
Firecrawl takes a brute-force approach by physically scraping raw Markdown. While no data is missed, it results in massive bloat (41,300 tokens/query). The LLM Inference Cost skyrockets ($13.02 in LLM fees alone). It is highly capable for deep web scraping, but structurally too expensive for real-time RAG.

=========================================================
CONCLUSION
=========================================================
This benchmark mathematically proves that raw neural retrieval (Exa) offers the highest ceiling for RAG accuracy, while highly-optimized dense retrieval (KeiroLabs) provides the ultimate sweet spot of quality and affordability. Brute force scraping architectures (Firecrawl, Tavily) lead to astronomical inference costs without proportional increases in reasoning capability.
